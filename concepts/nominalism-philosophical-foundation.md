# Nominalism Philosophical Foundation

## Overview
This reference explains nominalism: only particulars exist, while universals and abstract objects are names or constructs.

## Main points
- Hume, Berkeley, and Ockham are central reference figures. Ockham strips away universals (keeps material substance), Berkeley strips away matter (keeps minds), Hume strips away even the self and causal necessity (keeps only perceptions). Each is more radical than the last.
- Hume's causation: we never observe the "power" by which one event produces another — only contiguity, temporal priority, and constant conjunction. The feeling of necessity is a psychological habit projected onto the world.
- Hume's bundle theory: no persisting "self" underlies experience. Introspection finds only "a bundle or collection of different perceptions" in perpetual flux. Identity over time is a fiction produced by the mind's tendency to confuse resemblance with identity.
- Berkeley's esse est percipi: sensible objects have no existence distinct from being perceived. The world consists of minds (which perceive) and ideas (which are perceived), with no third category of "matter."
- Ockham's Razor: "Entities must not be multiplied beyond necessity." You do not need universals to explain why things are similar.
- Modern nominalism: Quine and Goodman attempted to reconstruct mathematics using only concrete particulars. Trope theory replaces universals with particular property-instances (this redness of this apple). Hartry Field showed science can be reformulated without numbers or sets.
- Nominalism and statistics: if one avoids governing-law metaphysics, science can be read as discovering patterns — regularities, correlations, constant conjunctions. Statistics is one support language for describing patterns without committing to underlying necessities; this page should not turn that support language into a primitive of the live system by itself.
- Humean laws: laws are descriptions of regularities (Lewis's Best System Account), not governing principles. The world is "a vast mosaic of local matters of particular fact, just one little thing and then another."
- Anti-essentialism: if no universals, things have no essences — no fixed abstract nature. Categories are conventional, mutable, context-dependent.

## Why it matters
This is one of the philosophical support roots behind the wiki's insistence on explicit witnesses, finite probes, and anti-smuggling constraints. It supports the move toward probe-relative identity and away from intrinsic essence, but it does not by itself derive the system axiom `a=a iff a~b` or outrank the direct distinguishability/constraint surfaces that do that work more specifically. Confucian rectification of names is a support tradition for grounding names in actuality, while later Neo-Confucian qi-monist readings support immanent pattern without requiring transcendent universals. The best support here comes from Zhang Zai and Wang Fu-chih style readings where pattern never leaves process; once li is treated like a separable principle, the fit weakens.
