## Causality as a compression narrative

Classical causality — the past causing the present — is not treated as
fundamental in this model. Cause and effect is described as a story
consciousness tells, a compression of an uncertain universe into causal
narratives. These stories become culture and technology, and they survive and
work within bounded domains.

Traditional determinism is described as only working by building artificial
walls or closed domains — "semi-determinism." The claim is that actual
reality does not fit machine-like causal processes.

The jk fuzz — the retrocausal weighting term in the bridge construction — is
named as more nearly the actual causal force. This is explicitly not
classical retrocausality; it reflects the idea that future compression is the
driving force in the entropic-monism picture.

## FEP read literally

The Free Energy Principle is treated as literally how the universe works, not
only as a model of how brains work. Perception is framed as a prediction
corrected by the senses — the prediction comes first. The QIT engines are
described as the innate attractor basin: evolution, physics laws, perception,
emotions, and consciousness are all read as reflections of one underlying
structure.

A caveat is recorded against Friston's FEP specifically: Friston's version is
Hume-directional but still presumes a Bayesian generative-model prior as
primitive. The deeper move attributed to the owner's position is to derive
the prior from the constraint surface itself, without presuming a Bayesian
prior — the retrocausal possibility field stands in for the un-presumed
prior.

## The Hume-confirmed-by-neurology argument

The owner frames his confidence as resting on a near-unbeatable argument that
requires only removing baseless presumptions (causality, matter's supremacy),
while aligning with both the math and empirical reality. The mainstream is
framed as a Cartesian/Newtonian/clockwork "cult in power" — rationalist
biases dressed as science — with empiricism's narrative buried while
individual empiricists (the Mach lineage) quietly built real science.

Three specific neurological confirmations of Hume are recorded:

1. No unified self, only a bundle — supported by split-brain research and
   Gazzaniga's "interpreter": the left hemisphere confabulates reasons for
   acts initiated by the right hemisphere.
2. "Reason is the slave of the passions" given a mechanism — Damasio's vmPFC
   patients retain intact logic but lose emotional integration, and as a
   result cannot decide. Reason without passion does not run, which is read
   as empirical support for feeling-operators-first.
3. Perception as prediction, not intake — predictive processing and Friston's
   work, read as Hume's impressions-first claim with the arrow sharpened.

Kant is described as a concession-and-reversal: he conceded causality is not
in the world, made it a mind-imposed category, then re-absolutized it as
necessary and universal against Hume's habit-based account. Mach is
credited with continuing past Kant — dropping absolute space and keeping only
what probes register, a lineage running to Einstein.

A pattern is named: everyone who rediscovers Hume tends to fence him to their
own field (Haidt to morality, Damasio to neurology, Friston to perception).
The claim is that nobody lets the causality argument reach physics — which is
the move this model is said to make.

A method/model wall is recorded to keep this argument honest: the *method*
(dropping unwarranted presumptions) is rightly unfalsifiable, since it only
removes claims and makes none. The *model* (the specific physical
construction — micro-gravitational-wave loops, engine geometry, linked-versus-
knotted structure) must stay killable, and is checked against specific
falsifiers (graviton, WIMP, inflation, Standard-Model-gauge kills). The
recorded danger is method-confidence leaking into model-guesses.

The method/model wall was never actually at risk — the owner already

## Science as the correlations

A related, separately dated statement (2026-07-03) gives the cleanest form of
the owner's view of what science is:

> "causality can't be proven... much of what people think science is, is
> causation stories. all causation stories will be supplanted by a better
> story. science for me is the correlations. all science rests on
> correlations. and better science digs down into deeper nuanced
> correlations. and when those shatter an existing causal paradigm story, it
> is earth shattering. for me, i don't care."

This is read as the correct portfolio position: across scientific
revolutions, correlations survive while only the story dies (Newton's
predictions still hold in-domain; Maxwell's equations passed through
relativity unchanged; thermodynamic relations survived atomism). This is
cross-referenced against structural realism (Worrall) and ontic structural
realism (Ladyman) as an independent academic route arriving near the same
place, though the owner's position is stated as going one step further:
structure just is correlations, with nothing deeper behind it.

A fence is kept on this picture from the owner's own material: stories keep
one job. The deductive direction of science requires them, because
"empiricism can't tell stories nor imagine cause." A story is a search
heuristic for generating experiments — never the content of knowledge. The
two roles are not to be merged.

## Free will as trained 4F state-transition mastery

Owner doctrine, 2026-07-03. The mind and all thoughts are held to be fully
deterministic — Hume is described as having "basically had it nailed." Free
will is not located in the head, and it is not indeterminacy. It is defined
as: integrating and training all four 4F states (fight, flight, freeze,
fawn), knowing which state one is in, and then doing sets of correlated
things that raise the likelihood of transitioning to a chosen state.

Free will, on this account, is trained transition control over one's own
affective state machine — a skill that scales with emotional range, not a
metaphysical exemption from causation. It is described as correlation-native:
you do not cause a state, you shift transition probabilities via correlated
inputs, matching the "stable under probe" language used elsewhere in the
model, applied here to the self.

A named illustrative critique: free-will-denying determinist accounts (for
example, Sapolsky's) are read as coming from people with high emotional
affect but a narrow range — stuck in stress states, and so unable to feel
transition control themselves, generalizing their own narrow introspective
sample into a general theorem. The determinism claim about the substrate is
accepted as correct; the conclusion that this settles free will is held to be
the error, since the actual variable is trainable affect-transition, not
mental determinism.

## Newton as the unproven story that rules engineering

A related point, same date: Newton's underlying causal story — force,
absolute space, action-at-a-distance — was never proved in engineering
practice; only his correlations were. The story survives because it is
appealing, and it is nearly all that engineering education teaches, while
relativity and quantum mechanics are read publicly as adjustments rather than
overthrows. The claim is that Newton's own mathematical model was already
overthrown internally by Lagrangian and Hamiltonian mechanics (energy-first
rather than force-first), and that quaternions carry rotation and spinor
structure that vector notation hides — historically losing out to Gibbs–
Heaviside vector notation for reasons of convenience, not correctness. The
clockwork story is said to leak into non-physics domains as well: named
examples include MMT economics, centralized-planning failures generally, and
persistent force/control/determinism framing in biology.
