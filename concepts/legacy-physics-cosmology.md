## Grandmaster of the Universe: Space-Time Cosmology Thread

The Grandmaster builds a unified cosmology from the ground up starting with mathematical foundations. The cosmology/space-time content is inseparable from the math framing but can be extracted as follows.

### Space as possibility; time as observation

The central move is to identify empty space with possibility and time with the act of observation or determination:

- "Space is possibility. Time is the observation of the result of a dice roll."
- A universe-sized die spinning forever is space (nothingness, all possibilities simultaneous). When it settles on one face, time begins.
- "The vastness of space and its emptiness is the very nursery of universes."
- "The amount of nothingness and space required to create this universe is just a measure of the possibility of this universe existing."

This identification — space = possibility, time = determinacy — is one of the deepest legacy roots of later wiki material connecting spatial extent to entropy and entropy to time.

### Probability fields and non-Euclidean structure

- Matter emerges from observation of probability fields, not from objects.
- "Things exist as objects through observation."
- Non-Euclidean geometry (curved/warped planes) is the correct home for physics because flat idealized Cartesian planes are approximations. "Crumple up your paper, and the underlying patterns you drew on them go unchanged."
- Quantum fields (imaginary-number structure) are the correct layer below classical geometry: "There are many different kinds of space to map reality and math into. The one that is most interesting is to map reality into a field of probability."

### Black holes, dark matter, and information

A speculative but recurring cosmology claim: black holes break matter down into pure spacetime and re-emit it as information encoded in gravitational structure.

- "Matter is broken down into pure spacetime when it is turned into a blackhole."
- Black holes must be observed to exist; as they move beyond a universe's event horizon they emit energy back into the universe.
- Dark matter is proposed as the intersecting spacetime spheres of many black holes: "These vast spheres could intersect across space and create a kind of structure. Maybe this is what Dark Matter is in reality."
- Dark energy is thin, expansive space with low information density; black holes are dense space with maximal information.

### Universe from nothingness

- "The vastness of space and its emptiness is the very nursery of universes."
- Probability and randomness create structure without requiring a designer: "the universe coming from randomness doesn't make it random."
- Any pattern is a least-tension solution: systems evolve toward their eigenvectors. A solar system is "dust" finding its pattern via rotation toward minimum tension.

This thread is genealogically upstream of [[owner-thesis-and-cosmology]]'s entropic monism claim and the distinguishability / admissibility framing in [[constraint-on-distinguishability]].

## Dark Empress: Dimensionality and Cosmology Thread

The Dark Empress Part 3 (Chapters 9–12) carries distinct cosmology content organized around a dimensional ladder and a white-hole origin story.

### Chapter 10 — Evolving Dimensionality

A ladder of dimensions built from zero upward, with each step adding a degree of freedom:

- Zero-dimensional space = a single point; solves Zeno's paradox by making the infinitesimal not truly nothing but the smallest determinate quantum of extent.
- The text proposes that time itself emerges from properties of zero-dimensional space: "Time emerges from physical properties in zero-dimensional space."
- Vibration in a line (1D) at the speed of light is the first time-bearing structure.
- The 4D universe (3 space + 1 time) is presented as something the human mind can navigate through subjective visualization — the same "leap" that lets the mind enter zero-dimensional nothingness lets it grasp four-dimensional structure.
- The 5th dimension is proposed as concretely observable and connected to the dimensional ladder rather than being purely abstract.

### Chapter 11 — General Relativity and the Ideal Observer

The Einstein/Newton contrast is used to argue for a specific model of causality:

- Newton: ideal observer stands outside and above the system, sees causes and effects as an external god. This produces a clockwork universe.
- Einstein: no god's-eye view exists. Causality is local. "Relativity isn't really about relativity. It is about Locality."
- The tragedy of 20th-century science is reading "everything is relative" as moral relativism; the actual claim is that causality is local and observer-dependent, not that truth is absent.
- The text frames Einstein as a philosopher first whose method (imagination → prediction → falsification) inverted normal empiricism.

### Chapter 12 — White Hole Cosmology and Sequential Universes

The clearest explicit cosmology claim in the book:

- "Universes could be born out of other universes." The multiverse is separated by lengths in time, not only by distance.
- The most likely first event: "the universe had its very first stable state. It didn't just come and go. It endured a bit." The vacuum (least possible thing) is the most likely structure to randomly emerge from nothing.
- The end state of one universe seeds the next: "the conditions of the preceding universe affecting the following universe." Small improvements can transfer across cycles, making universal evolution ratchet-like.
- White holes are the positive-entropy bubble counterpart to black holes: white holes seed universes; black holes are the negative-entropy sinks.
- A chromatic multiverse metaphor: "our universe is a tiny sliver or even photon in the spectrum of a circular rainbow." Nearby universes are nearly identical; distant universes have very different starting constants.

This thread aligns closely with the [[owner-thesis-and-cosmology]] cosmology section (white-hole / big-bang structure, recursive-universe succession, simplest stable state as origin).

## Dark Empress: Three Axioms of Space-Time (Chapter 12)

The most formally stated cosmological claims in The Dark Empress appear as three source-numbered axioms in Chapter 12:

**Axiom 1 — Evolution of Space-Time.**
"Universes could be born out of other universes." Sequential evolution: the end state of one universe seeds the next, with conditions transferring between cycles. Each cycle can produce "a small change that actually works and makes the universe little 'better'." Universal improvement is ratchet-like — small transferable improvements accumulate across universe-generations.

**Axiom 2 — Consistency of Time.**
Only what is internally consistent can persist through time. The most stable state is the most probable first state: "the universe had its very first stable state. It didn't just come and go. It endured a bit." The vacuum — least possible thing — is the most likely first structure because it requires the fewest prior conditions.

**Axiom 3 — Probability of the Possible and Impossible.**
Reality bootstraps from probability in the source's axiom voice: "The most likely thing that could happen, is that the universe had its very first stable state." Impossible things are excluded not by decree but by their inability to survive their own internal contradictions. Survival through time IS the selection criterion in this legacy source framing, not a current selection theorem by itself.

These three axioms align closely with:
- [[owner-thesis-and-cosmology]] — entropic monism, simplest-stable-state origin, white-hole seeding
- [[evolutionary-epistemology-reference]] — universal Darwinism, ratchet-like transitions, survival-over-time as selection
- [[viability-theory-reference]] — survival within constraint as primary criterion

## What these threads share

Both books echo a handful of recurring commitments:
- nothingness/possibility as the most solid foundation (not objects or matter)
- space and time as paired aspects of one structure (space = possibility / uncertainty; time = observation / determinacy)
- entropy, information, and gravity as intimately linked
- universes as evolutionary systems that can improve across cycles

These are legacy-framed intuitions, not current/front-door claims. Read them through [[legacy-speculative-frameworks]] and [[owner-thesis-and-cosmology]] for placement in the current system.
