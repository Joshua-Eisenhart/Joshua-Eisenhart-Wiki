# Mimetic Meme Manifold -- Source Synthesis

## Overview
One-place source synthesis for the mimetic meme manifold language, the user's framing about research/harness/salience/Leviathan language control, and how that framing relates to current work. Merges source-backed manifold notes with current harness framing without claiming repo-canonical proof.

## What the Source Corpus Says
A mimetic meme manifold is the macro arena in which many engine instances couple, imitate, synchronize, fork, and prune through language/meme dynamics. Axes 7-12 are the proposed control axes for that coupling layer. The layer is still open/proposed and needs a QIT-native contract before simulation-ready.

Cross-file synthesis supports: group language matters, vocabulary is a control surface, membership and narrative are operationally meaningful, AI can be treated as a manifold/coupling system, Axes 7-12 are the proposed macro coupling layer (still open).

## The User's Morning Framing
Research is key to [[constraint-surface-and-process|constraint engineering]] and building the harness. Key points:
- A harness needs a dictionary of aligned language
- That dictionary makes the right things salient for an LLM
- Each fresh LLM instance needs a Mimetic Meme Manifold of language to load it into the right thinking space
- Constraint engineering needs its own dictionary
- Each project can build a custom dictionary on top to shape saliency
- A group's language layer can shape salience, coupling, and membership through its mimetic meme manifold
- Control over language is what makes someone sovereign (Leviathan)
- Leviathan and constraint engineering belong together

## What This Framing Implies
- A harness is partly a language-loading problem
- A project-specific dictionary is a salience-shaping layer
- Language control is an operational power, not decoration
- Group-level language structure is an operational salience/control surface
- The mimetic meme manifold is the language layer or vocabulary that helps load a model into the right conceptual space

It is NOT a claim that language itself is the primitive substrate. It IS a claim that language control is essential for building and using the [[boot-prompt-templates|harness]].
