# Systems Philosophy and Attractor-Basin Inversion

## Purpose
This page adds the systems-philosophy layer requested during the whole-wiki/MMM upgrade.

The central point:

Strong constraints do not mean narrow search. Strong constraints require wide exploration. The basin emerges only after many candidates, probes, failures, tensions, and partial survivors are thrown at the constraint field.

This is one of the main inversions needed to defeat normal LLM bias.

## The inversion
Normal LLM process tends to:
- pick a plausible center too early;
- smooth contradictions into a single story;
- treat salience as evidence;
- turn a named attractor into a pre-existing object;
- compress before the exclusion field has done enough work.

The desired process is different:
- explore many candidates widely;
- keep contradictions and negative evidence visible;
- apply strong constraints without narrowing prematurely;
- let basins appear from repeated survival/exclusion patterns;
- only then compress into a harness, MMM, route, or doctrine page.

## Attractor basin rule
An attractor basin is not assumed in advance.

It is inferred when many trajectories, probes, source families, tools, and worker narratives repeatedly fall into the same surviving pattern under bounded exclusion pressure.

A basin is stronger when:
- different tools expose the same boundary;
- different language reservoirs converge without being forced;
- negative controls kill nearby alternatives;
- independent workers find compatible survivors;
- repo artifacts and wiki routes agree on the same support layer;
- the basin still survives after the obvious LLM story is denied.

A basin is weaker when:
- it appears only because one summary smoothed the data;
- it depends on a favorite metaphor;
- no negative cases were admitted;
- the same terms are reused without probe-relative distinction;
- it collapses under a tool or repo check.

## Why wide exploration is required
Strong constraints are not a small funnel at the beginning. They are a hard exclusion field applied across a wide admission pool.

Wide exploration is needed because:
- a narrow start imports the model's default ontology;
- the right carrier/support may be non-obvious;
- local failures reveal the shape of the constraint boundary;
- independent tool families expose different cuts through the same space;
- high-entropy source material may preserve kernels that front-door summaries miss;
- MMM quality improves when it samples sentences, nouns, thinkers, tools, and formal structures before compressing.

## Relation to this wiki
The wiki should behave like an attractor-basin machine:

1. intake many source/tool/repo/research surfaces;
2. classify role and evidence status;
3. preserve killed/open/survived branches;
4. route the survivors into concept pages, tool routers, research queues, and MMM reservoirs;
5. compress only after bounded work shows a basin.

This means the wiki should not become:
- a single doctrine blob;
- a version-history swamp;
- a pure research bibliography;
- a proof theater;
- a style mimicry machine.

It should become a harness where future agents are pulled into the right basin by constraints, examples, great sentences, formal nouns, tools, and negative evidence.

- distributed cognition and stigmergy: shared surfaces as collective memory and routing field;
- formal language / grammar constraints: how language shapes the reachable completion basin.

## MMM sentences
- Strong constraints need a wide field to show what they actually exclude.
- The basin is discovered by throwing enough trajectories at the constraint field.
- Do not name the attractor before the basin has survived exploration.
- A contradiction is a terrain feature until a constraint kills it.
- Compression is earned when many paths fall into the same surviving shape.
- The harness should create a basin, not merely describe one.
- The LLM's first smooth story is usually the wrong attractor.
- Negative evidence is how the basin gets its edges.

## Tool implications
- z3/cvc5 test whether a proposed basin violates encoded constraints.
- XGI/TopoNetX/GUDHI can represent multi-way/topological basin structure.
- rustworkx/NetworkX can expose route and dependency basins.
- geomstats can test manifold/metric assumptions about basin shape.
- e3nn/PyG can test whether learned dynamics respect symmetry constraints.
- Grok/Gemini/Claude/Codex can supply independent trajectories, but their agreement is pressure only, not proof.
