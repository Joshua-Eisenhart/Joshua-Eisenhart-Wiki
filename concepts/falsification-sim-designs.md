# Falsification Sim Designs

## Overview
Three sim specs designed from tradition-mapping mismatches. Each is designed to KILL a specific claim if the claim is wrong. Maximum Popper pressure: test the most falsifiable version of the owner's thesis first.

## Sim 1: Gauge Group Correspondence

**Claim under test:** The engine's constraint-surviving symmetry structure corresponds to Standard Model gauge groups (SU(3)xSU(2)xU(1)).

**What to test:** Compute the Lie algebra generated by all 6 pairwise commutators of the 4 operators (Ti, Fe, Te, Fi). Check dimension, closure, and whether it matches any subalgebra of su(3)xsu(2)xu(1).

**Kill condition:** If the generated algebra has no structural correspondence to any Standard Model gauge subalgebra, the claim is killed at this level.

**Resolution:** Geometric (operators). Boot type: A1/recon.

## Sim 2: Viability Kernel vs Attractor Basin

**Claim under test:** The system behaves as a viability kernel (survival under constraint) not an attractor basin (convergence to fixed point).

**What to test:** Run engine from 20 different initial conditions, 10 full cycles each. Track whether trajectories converge (attractor) or remain bounded without converging (viability). Perturb mid-cycle: return to same path (attractor) or stay within bounds on different path (viability).

**Kill condition:** If all trajectories converge to the same fixed point regardless of initial condition, the viability framing is wrong and attractor basin is correct.

**Resolution:** Geometric (engine dynamics). Boot type: B/ratchet.

## Sim 3: Metric Uniqueness (Cencov vs Petz)

**Claim under test:** The system's geometry is effectively unique (Cencov-like behavior) despite quantum non-uniqueness (Petz: infinitely many monotone metrics).

**What to test:** Compute distances between consecutive engine stages using three quantum metrics: Bures (smallest), Wigner-Yanase (middle), Kubo-Mori (largest). Compare orderings.

**Kill condition:** If the three metrics give qualitatively different geometries (different ordering of which stages are near vs far), the system must address quantum metric non-uniqueness explicitly.

**Resolution:** Geometric (density matrix geometry). Boot type: A1/recon.

## Why These Three

1. **Gauge group** — owner's strongest claim (engine = universal pattern), tested at most specific. Maximum Popper pressure.
2. **Viability vs attractor** — tests the fundamental framing. If the engine is an attractor, the entire process doc needs revision.
3. **Metric uniqueness** — tests whether classical intuitions survive at the quantum level where the engine actually operates.

Each sim is designed to KILL. If the claim survives, that's informative. If it dies, that's MORE informative — it maps the boundary of [[constraint-surface-and-process|M(C)]].
