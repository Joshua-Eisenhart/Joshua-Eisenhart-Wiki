# Constraint Surface And Process

## Overview
Active governing document for the admissible surface M(C) and the process that explores it. Two root constraints constitute the surface — they are not a ladder, they coexist. The process is not designed independently; it is derived from the same constraints it applies.

## The Surface

Two root constraints constitute M(C):

**F01 (Finitude):** All distinguishability is bounded. Finite carriers, finite probes, finite operators, finite paths. No completed infinities.

**N01 (Noncommutation):** Order-sensitive composition. AB != BA in general. Sequence of composition belongs to the object.

M(C) = {x : x satisfies F01 and N01 and all constraints derived from them}. Every point on M(C) satisfies ALL constraints simultaneously. Points not on M(C) are dead. The constraint set includes the extended charter (24 constraints) and all admissibility fences: no primitive identity, no primitive equality, no primitive geometry, no primitive time, no primitive causality, no primitive probability, no free closure, finite witness discipline, fail-closed admission.

## Constraints Create the Process

From F01: the process must be finite (finite sims, finite steps, finite checks). Each sim must terminate. You cannot explore the entire surface — only finite samples.

From N01: the order of exploration matters. Kills are irreversible within a pass. Peeking at unchecked regions to bias current checks violates N01.

## Anti-Teleology
The system is anti-teleological at the process level. There is no predetermined destination. The constraints do not know what they will select for. They only know what they select against. The ratchet is blind. It does not optimize toward a goal. It filters. What survives is what's compatible with the constraints — not what any agent wanted to survive.

## Ratchet as dependency chain
The thread-level correction is that the ratchet should not be read as only repeated filtering. It is also an ordered dependency chain: later admissible structures depend on earlier constrained survivals. Each stage both restricts and permits what can come next. Candidate structures still need to be simulated, not declared. See [[support-first-constraint-manifold-dependency-chain]].

## The Candidate

The current candidate geometry is a coordinate chart on M(C): S3 (spinor carrier), nested Hopf tori, left/right Weyl spinors with H_L = +H_0 and H_R = -H_0, fiber/base loop geometry, four geometric operators (Ti, Fe, Te, Fi), eight terrains, dual loop grammar, 64-step engine cycle, joint bipartite density rho_AB. The candidate gets no privilege inside the process. If a charted point is not on the surface, that region is dead.

## The Graveyard

The graveyard is not a failure log — it is the primary scientific output. Every candidate region shown to be off M(C) goes to the graveyard. Status vocabulary: NOT_YET_TESTED, SURVIVED, KILLED, OPEN. Nothing "passes." Things are not-yet-killed.

## Multiple Boots

The system runs multiple boots simultaneously:

- **A1 / Recon Boot**: advocate and map the candidate. Cheating allowed — building, not judging. Test the weirdest predictions first.
- **B / Ratchet Boot**: blind constraint enforcement. Candidate-blind. The constraint surface does the killing.
- **S / Compiler Boot**: structural audit and record. Extractive only, never invents.
- **A / Orchestrator Boot**: route between boots. Teach the fences. Cannot write canon.

A1 output does NOT become B evidence without going through the proper pipeline. Direct citation is contamination.

## Prediction-First (FEP Alignment)

The process has structural correspondence with FEP active inference: candidate = prediction, constraint chain = sensory error-correction, what survives = updated prediction. Prediction comes first. You enter with a candidate. The constraints narrow what remains. A wrong candidate that is killed teaches about that region of M(C).

Constraints are simultaneous on M(C), but a finite agent (F01) must check them in some order (N01). Resolution levels: Analytical (F01+N01, admissibility charter), Geometric (operator basis, Weyl layer), Relational (bridge family, cut/entropy/entanglement), Functional (kernel, edge writeback). These are not sequential gates — they are regions of the same surface at different coordinate detail levels. (from CONSTRAINT_SURFACE_AND_PROCESS.md)

## Finite but Not Fixed
F01 says finite at any moment — not fixed forever. Three operations: Constrain (apply next resolution filter), Kill (graveyard, irreversible within pass), Re-enter (new candidate at lowest relevant resolution). Kills are permanent within a pass; candidate space can expand across passes. New math at high resolution goes back to low resolution for full-chain checking — not reversal, but new generation entering same environment. (from CONSTRAINT_SURFACE_AND_PROCESS.md)
