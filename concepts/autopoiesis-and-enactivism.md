# Autopoiesis and Enactivism

## Overview
This reference covers autopoiesis, organizational closure, structural coupling, enactivism, sense-making, and the relation to viability and active inference.

## Main points
- Autopoiesis defines a system by self-production and organizational closure.
- Structural coupling is perturbation, not instruction.
- Enactivism treats cognition as embodied action that enacts a world of significance.
- The viability/organizational-closure connection makes this relevant to constraint-first modeling.

## Why it matters
This is a major reference for the system’s autonomy, boundary, and viability discourse.

## Foundational View (Grandmaster)

The Grandmaster frames self-creation from the ground up: "At the most basic level of description the relationship and connection to things are what create things. The space between objects and their relations is what gives rise to those objects." Systems create themselves through integration: "The very capacity to accept and integrate all of one's feelings, relationships, and perceptions gives rise to new levels of consciousness."

Uncertainty is the foundational axiom: "Uncertainty itself is the foundational axiom to which all can be built on. To assume nothingness and uncertainty is the strongest possible axiom." This maps directly to autopoiesis — the system's organizational closure is maintained not by fixed structure but by continuous self-production in the face of uncertainty. "Life itself and biology emerges from patterns in the very nature of life and things simply finding a way to organize and find connection and relationships."

Relation-first ontology: "You are the set of your relationships and that creates who you are in this world. The soul is just that set of information and its power to order reality."
