# Autopoiesis And Enactivism Reference

## Overview
Reference doc covering autopoiesis (Maturana & Varela), enactivism (Varela, Thompson & Rosch), structural coupling, viability connection, sense-making, and biological autonomy. The actual frameworks, not the system's application.

## Autopoiesis (Maturana & Varela, 1972/1980)

An autopoietic system is a network of processes of production of components that: (a) continuously regenerate the network that produced them, and (b) constitute the system as a distinguishable unity. The sole "product" is the continuation of its own relational unity. Contrast with allopoietic systems (factories) that produce something other than themselves.

## Organization vs Structure

Critical distinction: Organization = relations between components that define the system as a system of a particular kind. INVARIANT — if it changes, the system ceases to be that kind of system. Structure = actual components and concrete relations realizing the organization at any moment. VARIABLE. A cell's organization (self-producing network) stays the same; its structure (specific molecules) changes constantly.

## Organizational Closure

Processes of production form a closed network — each component produced by others within the network. The network produces the boundary (membrane) that makes it a discrete unity. This closure is ORGANIZATIONAL, not thermodynamic — open to flows of matter and energy, closed in organization.

## Structural Coupling

When an autopoietic system and its environment have a history of recurrent interactions leading to structural congruence, they are structurally coupled. The environment PERTURBS the system — does not INSTRUCT it. The system is structurally determined: its own current structure determines what changes a perturbation triggers. Same perturbation, different systems → different responses. No "instructive interactions."

## Enactivism (Varela, Thompson & Rosch, 1991)

From The Embodied Mind. Cognition is not internal computation over representations — it is embodied action. Core claims: cognition depends on experience from having a body with particular sensorimotor capacities. The organism brings forth (enacts) a world of significance through its activity. Organism and environment are co-specified. No pre-given world "out there" that the mind mirrors. Draws on Merleau-Ponty, Husserl, and Buddhist dependent co-arising.

## Autonomy as Organizational Closure

Autonomy = self-determination of identity through self-production. Not independence from environment. An autopoietic system is autonomous because its organization specifies what counts as perturbation, where its boundaries are, what interactions are possible. Identity emerges from operational closure, not external imposition. The system defines its own norms — what counts as maintaining or threatening its existence is determined by its own organization.

## Relation to FEP

Three positions: (1) Compatibility (Ramstead, Kirchhoff & Friston, 2020): "Active inference is enactive inference." Generative models realize inference-and-control, not structural representations. (2) Bridging (Kirchhoff, 2018): FEP and autopoietic enactivism are "genuinely continuous on central issues." (3) Tension (Froese & Di Paolo): biological systems are NOT well described as state-determined systems attracted toward NESS. Enactive life-mind continuity requires autonomy, adaptivity, sense-making — FEP alone doesn't meet these demands. Key tension: FEP applies to ANY system that persists; enactivism insists cognition requires specific organizational features beyond mere persistence.

## Viability Connection

An autopoietic system must maintain its organization or disintegrate (die). The set of structural states compatible with maintaining organization = the domain of viability. Connection to viability theory (Aubin 1991): the viability kernel maps to the set of structural configurations from which organization can be maintained. Di Paolo explicitly connects: adaptivity is regulating oneself with respect to conditions of viability.

## Biological Autonomy (Moreno & Mossio, 2015)

Most rigorous formalization of organizational closure. Distinguishes processes (thermodynamic flows) from constraints (entities acting on processes while being conserved at relevant timescale). Biological organization = closure of constraints: constraints are mutually dependent, each contributing to maintaining others. Function: a trait is functional when part of the closed, mutually dependent constraint network that results in self-production and self-maintenance. Naturalized, non-teleological account of biological function.

## Sense-Making (Di Paolo, 2005)

Bare autopoiesis is binary — system maintains organization or doesn't. Adaptivity: an adaptive system can monitor its states relative to the boundary of its viability domain and actively regulate to remain viable or improve conditions. Allows DEGREES. Sense-making: the most basic cognition — an organism evaluates interactions relative to maintenance of its own viability. The world becomes significant (has valence, matters) because the organism's continued existence is at stake and it can differentially respond. Not representation — the organism's activity of constituting a meaningful world through adaptive engagement.

## Fit for this wiki
Best fit:
- structural coupling, not instructive causation
- viability, autonomy, and sense-making on a living boundary
- organism-environment co-specification without a detached observer

- autopoiesis is about living organization, not generic systems in general
- enactivism is not the same thing as FEP, even when they overlap
