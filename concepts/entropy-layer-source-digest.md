## Overview
This digest keeps the source-backed claims that entropy is a later layer, that bridge closure precedes final entropy closure, and that entropy-family promotion should be earned by layer-specific sweeps rather than assumed from one favored functional.

## Main points
- Entropy is layer 17 in the pre-entropy ladder (see [[ladders-fences-admission-reference]]).
- Signed/unsigned entropy objects include `S`, `S(A|B)`, `I`, and `I_c`.
- The bridge layer sits before entropy and must be fixed before final entropy closure.
- In the C2 entropy structure search, Shannon/purity shortcuts were insufficient while von Neumann entropy survived.
- In the C1 entanglement-witness lane, mutual information did not kill fake coupling while concurrence/negativity did better.
- Distinguishability-side routing points toward coherent information as the strongest kernel candidate in this digest lane.

## Practical interpretation
Use this page to recover the narrow source-backed caution: entropy is downstream of the bridge and should be swept family-by-family under negative controls and geometry constraints. If the question is the current axis card, the three-layer entropy architecture, or the live promotion discipline, hand forward to [[axis-and-entropy-reference]] and [[entropy-sweep-protocol]] instead of overloading this digest.

## Candidate families
Shannon, von Neumann, Renyi, Tsallis, min/max entropy, conditional entropy, mutual information, coherent information, and concurrence/negativity as witness measures.
