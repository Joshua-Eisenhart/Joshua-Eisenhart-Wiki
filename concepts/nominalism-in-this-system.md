# Nominalism In This System

## Overview
Working translation of nominalism from the system's own axiom: a=a iff a~b. Identity is probe-relative distinguishability, not intrinsic essence. This page sketches what nominalism means inside the constraint-on-distinguishability framework and traces candidate derivation chains from the identity axiom.

## Core thesis
Nominalism says only particular things exist. Universals, abstract objects, categories, and kinds are names for groups of particulars — not real things that exist independently of their instances. This system's nominalism starts from a=a iff a~b: a thing has identity only if it is distinguishable from something else under a specific finite set of admissible probes.

## Key derivations from a=a iff a~b

**Derivation 1 — No primitive identity:** Identity is the equivalence class in S/~_M. Without M, no quotient exists, no identity exists. F01 adds: the probe family must be finite, so identity is always coarse-grained.

**Derivation 2 — No primitive equality:** Two things are equal iff no probe in M distinguishes them. The DPI guarantees stability: once equal, always equal under that probe family and any coarsening.

**Derivation 3 — No primitive causality:** Causality requires distinguishing sequences. If the probe family cannot distinguish (A then B) from (B then A), "A causes B" has no operational content under M. Causality is the distinguishability of sequences under a particular probe family.

**Derivation 4 — Observer boundary is not primitive:** The probe and probed are both elements of S. There is no privileged observer position outside S. The subject/object boundary is a quotient/probe-relative boundary, not a primitive substrate split.

**Derivation 5 — FEP connection:** Probe family M maps to generative model parameters mu. The quotient S/~_M is the organism's "world." Updating M = perception. Changing what's probed = action. Structural correspondence, not derivation.

**Derivation 6 — Power laws (attempt breaks):** Step 1-3 establish that adding probes creates distinctions and not all probes create equal refinement. Step 4 (why refinement power distributes as a power law) is where the derivation breaks. Open problem.

**Derivation 7 — Feedback loops (suggestive, not formal):** Quotient boundaries generate sensitivity (positive feedback) and interiors generate robustness (negative feedback). Connection to viability kernel boundaries needs formal work.

## How this connects to the system's primitives

Constraint on distinguishability is the current primitive framing. Downstream readings include matter as constraint, energy as constraint flow, geometry as constraint compatibility, dynamics as constraint-respecting transformation, and information as constraint bookkeeping. Entropy is later — a measure of how much distinguishability exists in a region.

Boundary: this is wiki/source framing. Repo admission still requires named predicates, receipts, validators, and current spec mirrors.

Math is not discovered in a Platonic realm. Math is the structure of constraint on distinguishability. It is material.

## What this means for the ratchet
The ratchet doesn't discover pre-existing truths. It tests what particular patterns survive particular constraint checks. "Survived" means not yet distinguished from M(C) by any probe applied so far. Nothing is permanently true. Only not-yet-killed.

## What this means for LLMs
LLMs are trained on overwhelmingly Platonist text. The nominalist equivalents ("the correlation pattern labeled 'gravity' has survived all probes") can become empty formulas. The harness can enforce the language. It cannot enforce the thinking. But enforcing the language makes wrong thinking harder to express.

## Foundational View (Grandmaster)

The Grandmaster takes an explicit anti-Platonic stance: "Nearly all evil and bad ideas can be traced back to Plato. His idealism and ideas of a perfect world separate from this one underpins his radical totalitarian politics." No universals, only relations: "Things exist as objects through observation, and observation arises from the interconnected relationships of all things to each other."

Identity = indistinguishability under probe: "Nothing is equal. Nothing is equal. Nothing can be equal." The identity axiom A=A is rejected as idealistic: "The identity axiom, A=A, is an idealistic axiom for math, that creates much of the standard day to day counting style math we all know. It creates a mechanical model of reality." Instead: "A~B is a practical empirical kind of math. It says that things are similar to each other rather than being equal."

This prefigures the system's a=a iff a~b. The Grandmaster's chain "Indeterminacy-> probability-> imaginary numbers-> negative numbers->positive numbers" motivates the support-layer hypothesis that statistical structure may be prior to identity. See [[process-philosophy-and-relational-physics]] for the relational frame.

## Why Statistics Is Prior to Identity
This page preserves one working nominalist hypothesis: if identity requires distinguishability (a=a iff a~b), and distinguishability is read through repeated probe results, then statistical structure may be a better earlier readout than object-first identity. Treat that as a support-layer interpretation under review, not as a settled primitive that outranks the broader constraint-on-distinguishability doctrine.
