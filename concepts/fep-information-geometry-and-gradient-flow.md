# FEP Information Geometry and Gradient Flow

This page routes information-geometry language into the wiki without treating it as nonclassical carrier admission.

## Useful objects

| Object | External role | Wiki use | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fisher information metric | local distinguishability geometry of probability families | support for distinguishability geometry | not QIT by itself |
| natural gradient | steepest descent in information space | model-update support | not proof of neural or ratchet implementation |
| information length | path length through distribution space | possible cost/trajectory language | not survival proof |
| Shahshahani geometry | simplex geometry linked to evolutionary game dynamics | neighbor to IGT support | not owner-axis proof |
| gradient flow | descent dynamics on an objective | process analogy | objective must be named |

## FEP route

The neural-dynamics source is useful because it links prediction error, belief updating, natural gradient descent, and information length. The particular-physics source links Markov blankets and information geometry. Harper's source helps route Fisher/Shahshahani geometry in a cleaner way for IGT-adjacent pages.

## QIT boundary

Information geometry can support QIT language, but it does not automatically create a QIT carrier.

A QIT-facing local claim still needs:

- carrier state;
- readout object;
- metric/divergence or distinguishability measure;
- finite probe or operation set;
- negative/control condition;
- result or blocked-reason artifact.

## Blocked use

- Do not use "information geometry" as decorative geometry.
- Do not infer density matrices, QFI, or Hilbert-space structure without local carrier evidence.
- Do not promote nonclassical manifold claims from Fisher-metric vocabulary.
- Do not use gradient-flow language without naming objective, state space, and update rule.
