# Sasakian S3 as Prequantum Bundle

## Overview
A striking synthesis from the new worker-reported batch is that the `S³` support may be read not only as a useful carrier geometry, but as the canonical prequantum bundle over `CP¹ = S²` for the one-qubit case, with its standard contact / Sasakian structure doing the geometric work.

This page is snapshot-based from worker output and related authority surfaces; it should be treated as a routed concept pending independent controller rerun and deeper audit.

## The reported synthesis
The reported geometry reading is:
- `S¹ -> S³ -> S² = CP¹` is the Hopf / prequantum bundle
- the contact form on `S³` acts as the prequantum connection `1`-form
- the Hopf fiber circles are the `U(1)` fibers
- the contact distribution `ker(α)` gives the horizontal space relevant to quantum phase transport
- the `G`-structure depth of `S³` lands at the Sasakian level

Unpacked in project terms:
- the support space is not arbitrary
- the geometry that keeps recurring around qubit structure may be the right prequantum support because it survives to the needed contact/Sasakian depth

## Why this matters for the project
This gives a cleaner formal language for something the project has been circling for a long time:
- finding the right graph paper for the qubit support problem
- explaining why `S³`, Hopf structure, holonomy, and phase/fiber separation keep reappearing together

If the synthesis survives rerun and further audit, then the support search is not only about naming a favorite manifold. It is about finding the support that reaches the right geometric depth for the target quantum structure.

## Why it is still fenced
This is not yet the same thing as saying:
- the whole architecture is solved
- every later layer (Weyl, bridge, Axis 0, manifold coupling) automatically inherits closure from this result

It is instead a promising geometry-side anchor for the shell-local support question.
