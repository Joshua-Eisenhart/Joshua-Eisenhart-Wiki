# Process Philosophy and Relational Physics

## Overview
This reference collects process ontology, relational quantum mechanics, structural realism, causal set ideas, and other relational-first frameworks.
Use it as a support/reference cluster, not as a page that declares the system's primitive ontology by itself.

## Main points
- Whitehead's Process and Reality (1929): in Whitehead's own framework, becoming is metaphysically prior to being. Actual occasions are momentary, self-determining "drops of experience." Concrescence is the process by which an occasion integrates data from its past until satisfaction; at satisfaction it perishes and passes to objective immortality as datum for future occasions.
- Whitehead's prehension: the act by which one occasion grasps/feels another. An occasion IS its prehensions — constitutively shaped by its relations (internal relations).
- Process philosophy vs nominalism: both deny fixed enduring substances. But Whitehead retains universals via "eternal objects" (pure potentials that can ingress into actual occasions) — a realist move that strict nominalism forbids.
- Rovelli's Relational QM (1996): there is no observer-independent state in the usual starting sense. Quantum state is a relation between observed and observing system. This is useful support for anti-privileged-observer framing, but it should remain support language here rather than a direct whole-system identity claim.
- Wheeler's "It from Bit" (1989): every item of the physical world has "at bottom an immaterial source." Reality arises from yes-no questions. Existence is participatory.
- Zurek's Quantum Darwinism (2003): classicality emerges via Darwinian selection — the environment proliferates redundant copies of information about selected pointer states. Only states surviving environmental monitoring become accessible.
- Constructor Theory (Deutsch & Marletto, 2013-2015): physics reformulated as which transformations are possible vs impossible. A constructor can cause a task while retaining ability to cause it again. Derives no-cloning and complementarity from constructor principles.
- Ontic Structural Realism (Ladyman, French): structure is ontologically fundamental — relations without relata in the most radical form. Structure is preserved across theory change even when ontology is not.
- Causal Set Theory (Sorkin, Dowker): "Order + Number = Geometry." Spacetime is a discrete partial order. Dynamics via Classical Sequential Growth models.
- Categorical QM (Abramsky, Coecke, 2004): primitive objects are physical PROCESSES and how they compose, not states. Physical theory IS a process theory — a symmetric monoidal category.
- Integrated Information Theory (Tononi, 2004): consciousness = integrated information (Phi). System with Phi > 0 has some consciousness. Controversy: 2023 letter by numerous scholars characterized IIT as potentially unfalsifiable.

## Why it matters
This is a major source cluster for the stack's relational and process-centered framing. The legacy texts (Dark Empress, Grandmaster) hold a relation-first ontology: "At the most basic level of description the relationship and connection to things are what create things." Chinese correlative thinking belongs here as support-only: yin-yang, tongbian, and Daoist transformation language strengthen process-first framing, but they do not function as direct formal proofs. Daoist material is strongest here where it warns against fixed naming and treats transformation, reversion, and non-forcing as primary. Confucian material contributes less to the ontology layer and more to name-discipline, role-grounding, and structure-maintaining practice. ## Legacy Perspective (Both Books)

Both the Dark Empress and the Grandmaster hold a relation-first ontology. The Grandmaster: "At the most basic level of description the relationship and connection to things are what create things. The space between objects and their relations is what gives rise to those objects." Objects are patterns in relations: "A person can change every atom in their body, and yet be the same person. You are the set of your relationships and that creates who you are in this world."

The Dark Empress extends this to physics: entanglement is foundational, not bolted on. "Instead of being something we attach on to theories we use to describe the universe, it probably should lie at the foundational root of all theories." Space replicates via entanglement patterns: "space has just had the most basic reproductive system running for a long time."

The Grandmaster on observation and process: "Things exist as objects through observation." Perception, not reason, is primary: "Perception forms your reality and not your thoughts." The glow of heat is more useful than the object: "All we really see is heat and change. Objects are illusions of our imaginations, that we think we see."

Both books reject substance metaphysics in favor of relational process — objects emerge from the interconnected web of relations, not the other way around. See [[nominalism-in-this-system]] for the philosophical grounding and [[autopoiesis-and-enactivism]] for the self-creation view.
