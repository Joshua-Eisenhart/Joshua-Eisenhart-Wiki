## Overview
This digest preserves the strongest usable kernel from the Jung/IGT map: the system has one runtime geometry layer, one operator layer, and one chart/stage-grammar layer. Those layers connect, but they are not interchangeable.

## The three-layer split

| Layer | What belongs there | What it is not |
|---|---|---|
| carrier geometry | `S^3`, `S^2`, nested Hopf tori, fiber loop, lifted-base loop, left/right sheet realizations | not chart labels or outcome labels |
| operator math | `Ti`, `Te`, `Fi`, `Fe` as dephasing/rotation families acting on density states | not terrain topology classes |
| stage grammar | ordered tokens such as `TiSe`, `NeTi`, plus `WIN`, `LOSE`, `win`, `lose` | not the underlying carrier geometry or the operator algebra |

## Current safe correspondences
- `Se`, `Ne`, `Ni`, and `Si` are topology-and-flow classes on the carrier geometry.
- `Ti`, `Te`, `Fi`, and `Fe` are operator families acting on the state.
- Ordered tokens are produced by topology plus operator family plus precedence order.
- IGT labels name stage positions in the chart grammar; they do not define the runtime geometry.
