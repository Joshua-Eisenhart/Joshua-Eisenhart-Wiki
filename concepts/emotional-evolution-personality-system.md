# Emotional Evolution Personality System

## Overview
A personality theory white paper that links the Four Fs, Jungian functions, feedback loops, entropy, power laws, and a layered brain hierarchy into one model of emotional and behavioral dynamics.

This page should be treated as a legacy synthesis surface, not as current/front-door doctrine. The restored spreadsheet and companion text preserve more structural detail than this short summary currently carries.

## Legacy status and caution
- Status: legacy synthesis / non-canon personality material, not current controller authority.
- The June 2025 white paper is not the original seed artifact by itself. It is a later synthesis that expands and smooths earlier rougher personality material, especially the Type One / Type Two spreadsheet and related legacy notes.
- Read the lane in separate provenance layers rather than as one continuous doctrine voice:
  - original manuscripts
  - restored spreadsheet / extracted tables
  - later white-paper synthesis
  - candidate/current crosswalk / runtime-facing synthesis
- Many claims are framed as broad explanatory correspondences rather than established derivations. The page should preserve them as historical model content, not silently upgrade them into current truth claims.
- Highest leakage risk: when Four Fs, entropy, nominalism, Jung, game theory, politics, and brain-layer language are summarized as if they are part of the current/front-door spine. They are better treated as a legacy rosetta layer and personality-theory branch.

## Main points
- Treats the Four Fs as foundational modes of being, not just survival reflexes.
- Maps SNS/PSNS, positive/negative feedback, and entropy to emotional dynamics.
- Integrates Jungian functions, Hume-style nominalism, and predictive modeling.
- Extends the theory into politics, social media engagement, and game-theoretic strategy.
- Uses a six-layer brain hierarchy to connect physiology to personality differences.
- The most important reusable pattern may not be the literal typology claims but the candidate engine grammar preserved by the sources: Type One / Type Two, WIN/win/lose/LOSE regimes, and correlated strategic-emotional state patterns.

## Missing nuance from the raw sources
- The spreadsheet source is not just a generic precursor. It introduces a stricter Type One / Type Two split, small-win vs big-win and small-loss vs big-loss distinctions, and an 8-state mapping that the current page compresses away.
- The white paper retains the Four Fs and IGT material, but it softens the sharper legacy claim that people are partitioned into two broad types with different outcome grammars.
- The raw materials also make stronger correlations across politics, autonomic state, Jung/MBTI clusters, Big Five openness/agreeableness/conscientiousness, hormones, and moral style. Even when those claims are speculative, they are part of what the legacy system actually said.
- The white paper includes tentative and uneven areas that should be named, not flattened: the emotion cube includes tentative emotions like Anxiety?, Hope?, and Frustration?; the politics section is explicitly marked preliminary; and the document mixes source-backed claims, personal theory, and generated connective tissue.

## Source-specific structure worth preserving
### Spreadsheet lane
- Type One / Type Two are first-order categories, not minor decoration.
- Each type contains four strategy cells arranged around win/loss regimes.
- The matrix distinguishes:
  - win min / WIN max
  - lose min / LOSE max
  - cooperative vs competitive sub-orientations
  - different moral projections such as sympathy, compassion, empathy, and reciprocal altruism
- The strongest reuse candidate is the claim that this is an engine-state grammar rather than merely a typology table.
- In later current synthesis, Type One and Type Two are sometimes read as candidate engine chiralities rather than as MBTI personality labels. Treat the stronger Weyl-spinor bridge as a user-level hypothesis or downstream current crosswalk move, not as something this legacy kernel page proves on its own.

### Restored spreadsheet skeleton
The restored spreadsheet is doing more than assigning personality labels. It proposes a compressed correlation grammar linking strategy, autonomics, cognition, politics, hormones, and time orientation.

| Layer | Freeze/Fawn side | Fight/Flight side |
|---|---|---|
| Nervous system | PSNS | SNS |
| Cognitive emphasis | Sensing / body / morals | Intuition / mind / ethics |
| Feedback | negative feedback | positive feedback |
| Openness | low openness | high openness |
| Politics | right-wing cluster | left-wing cluster |
| Time emphasis | past / present | future / transcendent |
| Hormone emphasis | serotonin / oxytocin / vasopressin | adrenaline / cortisol / dopamine |
| Four-Fs emphasis | Freeze, Fawn | Fight, Flight |

The raw spreadsheet then subdivides those broad sides into four political/strategic quadrants and finally into eight total states. That means the central claim is not just "these MBTI types map here" but that many human differences can be compressed into a recurring quadrant-and-substate grammar.

### What the extracted sheet says about causality and compression
The extracted xlsx is explicit that these surfaces should not be read as a one-way causal derivation. Its own wording is that the mapped layers are "different faces of the same thing" and that one surface does not directly cause the others. The stronger claim is a tight correlation grammar across:
- autonomic polarity
- openness / intuition-versus-sensing polarity
- political and social orientation
- time orientation
- hormone clusters
- Four-Fs states
- game-theoretic strategy labels

That matters because it keeps the personality lane in a correlation/engine-grammar role instead of letting the reader mistake it for a single privileged taxonomy.

### Three-stage partition in the extracted sheet
The sheet is easiest to preserve as three nested partitions:

| Split | Branch A | Branch B | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Activation polarity | Freeze/Fawn = PSNS, sensing, body, morals, negative feedback | Fight/Flight = SNS, intuition, mind, ethics, positive feedback | first engine-polarity split |
| Control posture | object/authoritarian/high-conscientiousness families (SJ/NJ side of the quadrant map) | subject/individualistic/lower-conscientiousness families (SP/NP side of the quadrant map) | second layout split |
| Social objective | competitive / thinker / disagreeable emphasis | cooperative / feeler / agreeable emphasis | third split producing the eight recurrent state families |

Read that way, the eight-state layer is not merely an MBTI roster. It is a compressed candidate grammar for recurring modes of hierarchy-maintenance, bonding, strategic sacrifice, reform pressure, skill/mastery, and reciprocal altruism.

### Type One / Type Two distinction
The restored spreadsheet also preserves a sharper two-family division than the white paper carries forward.

- **Type One** is organized around one ordering of win/loss grammars and MBTI/Jung placements.
- **Type Two** inverts or rotates those placements, producing a different strategic-emotional layout even when some surface labels repeat.
- The important audit point is that the source is not merely listing four Fs; it is claiming two distinct higher-order organizations of those four Fs.

This is one of the most underpreserved structures in the current wiki. Even if the mapping is speculative, the source is clearly making that stronger structural claim.

## Leviathan v3.2 Operationalization

Inside the Leviathan v3.2 internal model, Chapter 5 operationalizes the personality system through the Individual Development Mirror, translating abstract personality theory into scored, measurable metrics. This is legacy institutional design language, not current typology canon or current QIT evidence.

### Three-Axis Social Credit Score

Each individual carries three 1–10 scores with intrinsic (genetic/biological) and trainable factors:

- **Physical Attractiveness** — intrinsic: facial symmetry, genetic height, body proportions; trainable: grooming, posture correction, fitness (VO2 max >40), health metrics (resting HR <70 bpm)
- **Mental/Emotional Attractiveness** — intrinsic: cognitive capacity, emotional depth, temperament stability; trainable: cognitive sharpness, emotional intelligence (empathy score >120, self-awareness via psychometric tests), full emotional range (>5 expression contexts), **Four Fs control (selective response regulation, <10% reactive errors)**, resilience (HRV score >80)
- **Status Attractiveness** — intrinsic: family legacy, birth class, inherited wealth; trainable: social power, earned wealth, job prestige, relationship quality, community standing

The Four Fs appear explicitly as a trainable Mental/Emotional metric — "selective response regulation" with a <10% reactive error target — operationalizing the theoretical Four Fs framing as a concrete behavioral measurement-like bound inside the legacy source.

### Ugly Mirror as Individuation Mechanism

The Ugly Mirror implements Jung's individuation at system scale: AI-driven private self-assessment via image scanning, biosensors, and psychometric tests delivers "brutally honest, unfiltered feedback" without social approval filtering. The goal is confronting shadow material (flaws, deficits, blind spots) as the path to growth — the Jungian individuation process embedded in a CRM-style feedback loop. Mirror rank advancement mints new SBC, creating an economic incentive for individuation.

### Governance Bridge

Inside the Leviathan v3.2 internal governance model, the three-axis scores are load-bearing: leaders with high attractiveness scores earn more delegated votes (delegated proxy voting selects for demonstrated attractiveness); high SBC earnings from useful or rare skills raise Status Attractiveness; Mirror rank advancement mints new currency supply. The source couples the personality development system and the governance system; this does not promote those mechanics into current repo/runtime doctrine.
