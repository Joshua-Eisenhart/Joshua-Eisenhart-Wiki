# Fiber Bundles and Spin Geometry

## Overview
This reference covers bundle theory, connections, holonomy, Berry phase, spin geometry, Weyl spinors, and the Hopf fibration.

## Main points
- The Hopf fibration is the canonical nontrivial bundle underlying the qubit/Bloch-sphere quotient.
- Berry phase is holonomy in a U(1) bundle.
- Spinors live on double covers of rotation groups and encode chirality.
- Nested tori in S³ provide a concrete geometric scaffold.
- A newer geometry-side synthesis suggests that the project should also track foliation, contact/Sasakian depth, and possible prequantum-bundle readings explicitly rather than leaving them implicit inside Hopf language. See [[g-structure-tower]], [[hopf-foliation-structure]], and [[sasakian-s3-prequantum-bundle]].

## Why it matters
This is the geometric bridge between the engine math and the system’s spinor-first/holonomy-first framing.
