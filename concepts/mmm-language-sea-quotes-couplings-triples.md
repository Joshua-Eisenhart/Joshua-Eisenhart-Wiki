# MMM Language Sea, Quotes, Couplings, and Triples

## Purpose

MMMs are not rules.

They are a language sea: a high-salience reservoir of words, sentence-shapes, quotes, research nouns, operator couplings, and triples that pull a future model toward this project's way of thinking before it collapses the work into generic ontology, primitive identity, theorem-first proof, or familiar metaphor.

Use this page as saliency fuel, not authority. A phrase here can steer attention; it does not promote a claim. A quote can tune the basin; it does not override repo evidence, sims, or current doctrine surfaces.

## Quality target

A strong MMM should do more than repeat project keywords. It should make the next model more likely to:

- keep identity probe-relative;
- keep causality and sequence conditional;
- preserve killed/open/surviving branches separately;
- distinguish support, carrier, witness, and proof role;
- treat research fields as pressure lenses, not imported authority;
- keep geometry after constraints;
- keep quotes as language pressure, not as proof.

## Quote policy

Quotes are allowed and useful when they change the language basin. Use three classes:

1. **Owner/source quotes**: strongest saliency for this project; still not proof without artifact support.
2. **Aligned thinker quotes**: use as pressure lenses; do not imitate the thinker or import their whole metaphysics.
3. **Research-material phrases**: use as local vocabulary bridges into tools, papers, and reference pages.

When exact wording has not been locally verified in the current pass, mark it as a paraphrase or candidate quote. Do not silently invent quotation marks.

## Higher-order couplings

Use these for richer MMM packets:

- **constraint / probe / witness**: a claim becomes local only when a probe and witness exist.
- **channel / entropy / distinguishability**: QIT terms stay operational when tied to finite state transformations and distinguishability measures.
- **manifold / support / obstruction**: geometry is only live where support and obstruction conditions are named.
- **branch / graveyard / survivor**: the surviving narrative is not the whole story; killed and open alternatives carry the boundary.
- **field / pressure / fence**: research material adds pressure while claim fences prevent authority import.
- **quote / rhythm / check**: quotes can carry rhythm and stance, but checked support still decides claims.
- **nominalism / construction / commitment**: formal nouns should pull toward what has been constructed, quantified over, or witnessed.
- **Gödel / Tarski / Skolem**: formal language can reveal its own limits, truth strata, and model relativity; use this as boundary salience, not transcendence rhetoric.

## Sentence patterns

- Under probe family P, A and B are indistinguishable so far; outside P, identity is not earned.
- This supports a carrier reading, not a manifold claim yet.
- The returned witness changes the admissible set by excluding X.
- The quote is saliency fuel; the receipt is the evidence.
- The research field suggests a test surface, not a promotion.
- The basin is the repeated survival pattern under perturbation, not a final goal.
- The branch is open because no exclusion receipt has killed it.
- The geometry word stays conditional until the support check returns.
- If the tool returns no object, the tool did not carry the claim.
- Provider agreement is a scout signal, not sim evidence.
- Gödel is relevant as a boundary marker: a formal system can encode enough syntax to expose what it cannot settle from inside.
- Do not turn incompleteness into magic; turn it into a receipt-boundary and language-stratification check.
- A nominalist sentence asks what construction, witness, framework, or quantifier commitment pays for each noun.
