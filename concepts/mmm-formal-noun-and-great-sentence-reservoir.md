# MMM Formal Noun and Great Sentence Reservoir

## Purpose
This page upgrades the MMM idea from word-frequency and phraselet capture into a richer alignment substrate.

Important correction: MMMs are not rules. They are a sea of project-aligned language: words, quotes, couplings, triples, sentence rhythms, research nouns, and thinker-pressure lenses that make future agents more likely to think in the project's basin before they default to primitive identity, causal smoothing, or generic ontology.

An MMM can carry:
- words and phraselets;
- exact quotes and quote retrieval targets;
- great sentences;
- great thinkers as pressure lenses;
- aligned formal nouns;
- couplings and triples;
- tool-action nouns;
- proof/falsifier grammar;
- anti-collapse warnings;
- mathematical carrier vocabulary.

The point is not imitation and not rule-setting. The point is saliency: load enough aligned language that the next model starts closer to the project’s way of seeing, while still letting receipts, sims, and current authority surfaces decide truth.

## Current rule
MMM quality is not measured by surface similarity alone.

A good MMM should improve the next agent's ability to:
- name the active support layer;
- keep object-language conditional;
- preserve live alternatives until evidence excludes them;
- use tools by their proof role rather than by brand name;
- write short sentences that stop overclaim;
- select research lanes that match the system rather than generic keywords.

## Great sentences
For the systems-philosophy/attractor-basin inversion behind this sentence layer, see [[systems-philosophy-attractor-basin-inversion]].

Great sentences are compact alignment moves. They should be short enough to survive context compression and strong enough to change the next completion.

### Axis0 / field-compression anti-collapse sentence forms

These are not doctrine by themselves. They are saliency moves for future MMM / mini-MMM packets when the model risks being normalized into canon, physics-first proof, or single-story identity.

- The wiki is not canon; it is an aligned field of candidates, source kernels, research pressure lenses, and evidence routes.
- Owner-source language can steer the model without admitting the physics claim.
- A support source can improve saliency without changing status.
- Shared loop shape is not identity proof; keep readouts separate until a finite bridge survives controls.
- Future-option pressure is not teleology: many viable continuations constrain the present without one future becoming privileged.
- The present is the survivor/compression state only relative to the current finite support, probes, and exclusions.
- A shell, path, or Kraus-history row has teeth only where it changes a finite readout.
- Geometry/algebra receipts can strengthen the carrier layer while leaving the Axis0/gravity bridge open.
- Holodeck/QIT can be a process analogue without proving Axis0, gravity, or cosmic shell physics.
- Entanglement-information becomes a gravity readout candidate only after a named finite cut state and product/no-boundary controls.
- Do not write `X = Y` when the honest row is `X-as-Y candidate under probe family M`.
- Nominalist language names the probe, support, quotient, and killed alternatives before it names an object.
- Research support is a pressure lens, not a proof surface.
- Result-ledger pressure is not direct rerun evidence until the artifact path is controller-verified.

### General candidate sentence forms:
- A name is earned by the probe that can still distinguish it.
- Equality is shorthand until the constraint surface admits it.
- The survivor is not the essence; it is what the current exclusions failed to kill.
- The support question comes before the operator story.
- A tool is load-bearing only where its returned object changes the admissible set.
- Negative evidence is not failure noise; it is the shape of the boundary.
- Do not promote resonance into proof.
- Do not promote provider agreement into sim evidence.
- The carrier is a question, not a metaphor.
- A bridge is late-stage evidence, not a naming shortcut.
- If the same word survives by habit only, it is not yet the same object.
- The wiki should compress the system without smoothing away the exclusions.

## Aligned formal nouns
Formal nouns should pull agents toward the right mathematical and operational neighborhoods.

### Constraint and proof nouns
For the current Z3/CVC5 refusal/witness lane, see [[smt-formal-falsifier-lane]].

admissibility, witness, countermodel, unsat core, invariant, obligation, certificate, satisfiability, model, refinement, counterexample, trace, proof surface, proof kernel, falsifier, boundary, exclusion, survivor.

### Geometry and carrier nouns
support, carrier, manifold, bundle, fibration, section, connection, curvature, holonomy, torsion, obstruction, atlas, chart, fiber, base, lift, projection, covering, foliation, sheaf, stalk, glueing, local-global passage.

### Spin / chirality / algebra nouns
spinor, rotor, bivector, Clifford algebra, Pauli action, Weyl spinor, chirality, parity, representation, commutator, noncommutation, operator, algebra, module, invariant, spectrum.

### Graph / hypergraph / topology nouns
node, edge, hyperedge, incidence, simplex, cell, chain, boundary operator, homology, persistence, filtration, nerve, complex, path, walk, cut, component, cycle, motif, multi-way relation.

### Category / type / structure nouns
object, morphism, functor, natural transformation, adjunction, limit, colimit, pullback, pushout, quotient, equivalence, identity morphism, type, judgment, context, dependent pair, fibered category.

Use these with caution: category/type vocabulary can reduce object-essentialism, but identity morphisms and equality rules still need primitive-identity audit.

- Clifford: rotor, bivector, spin group, chirality, geometric product.

- rustworkx / NetworkX: graph traversal, cut, component, path, dependency graph.

## Great thinkers as pressure lenses
Thinkers are not authorities over the system. Use them as technique reservoirs.

Useful pressure lenses:
- Hume: checked state, modest inference, no metaphysical inflation.
- Peirce: signs, inquiry, fallibilism, abduction under test.
- Whitehead: process before substance, event grammar, anti-static ontology.
- Wittgenstein: meaning as use, grammar as constraint, language games.
- Grothendieck: structure, topos, relative viewpoint, patient abstraction.
- Lawvere: categorical clarity, adjunctions, structure-preserving maps.
- Noether: invariance, symmetry, conserved structure.
- Gromov: geometry as constraint and coarse structure.
- von Neumann: operator algebra, measurement, formal architecture.
- Shannon: channel, compression, code, information constraint.
- Turing: operational procedure, computation, machine-state discipline.
- Prigogine / Kauffman / Maturana-Varela: far-from-equilibrium, self-maintenance, viability, autopoiesis.
- Zhuangzi / Daoist classics: anti-fixed-name pressure and perspective mobility.
- Confucian classics: rectification of names, role discipline, cultivated order.

## Bad MMM failure modes
- Word-frequency mimicry without operational role.
- Thinker-name decoration without a pressure move.
- Formal nouns used as metaphors instead of tool/proof targets.
- Beautiful sentences that erase uncertainty.
- Research lanes that sound relevant but do not route to a page, tool, or sim family.
- Compression that removes graveyards, blockers, or open branches.
