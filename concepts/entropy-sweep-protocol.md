# Entropy Sweep Protocol

## Overview
The entropy sweep protocol says entropy is a later admissible measure, not a primitive. Each layer of the stack should test which entropy family is actually forced by the carrier, geometry, and bridge structure.

## Main rules
- Do not assume one entropy functional for the whole stack.
- Test candidate families at each admissible layer.
- Promote only the measure that survives the layer’s geometry and negative controls.
- Keep state entropy, signed cut behavior, and entanglement witnesses separate.

## Candidate families
- Shannon / von Neumann / Rényi / Tsallis / min-max forms
- Conditional and mutual information
- Coherent information
- Entanglement entropy, spectrum, negativity, logarithmic negativity
- Shell-, bridge-, history-window-, and transport-weighted variants

## Why it matters
This protocol prevents collapsing all information measures into one universal scalar. It keeps the layer hierarchy honest and preserves the distinction between total correlation, signed cut behavior, and witness behavior.

## Legacy Perspective (Dark Empress)

From Chapter 12: The Dark Empress makes a strong claim that entropy IS quantum gravity — not merely a thermodynamic measure but the fundamental mechanism by which space-time operates at quantum scales.

> "Entropy is quantum gravity, and a proper theory of entropy has to explain how it warps time and space at the smallest levels."

The book argues that micro-gravitational waves carry system information: "we observe particles bouncing around, but in reality, the heat we observe coming off of something, also contains its information in space time itself." Heat movement is gravity at the quantum scale — the way heat distributes is "a product of gravity operating on the quantum scale."

This is complementary to the entropy sweep protocol's insistence that entropy is admissible, not primitive: the Dark Empress treats entropy as emergent from entangled space-time structure, while the sweep protocol tests which entropy family is forced by the carrier geometry. Both reject a single universal entropy functional. See [[entanglement-theory]] for the foundational claim and [[information-geometry-reference]] for the time-as-expansion view.
