# Ratcheting as Obstruction and Refinement

## Overview
This page sharpens one of the thread’s most important conceptual corrections: the ratchet is not only repeated filtering. It can also be read as a staged refinement process governed by obstruction.

That means:
- some local structure is permitted
- a deeper structure is attempted
- obstructions may prevent the next layer from globalizing or surviving
- surviving structure becomes the substrate for later refinement

## Why this matters here
This gives the project a stronger mathematical vocabulary for several repeated themes:
- later layers depend on earlier ones
- not everything admissible locally survives globally
- the graveyard is informative because failed branches define the obstruction boundary
- candidate structures are not simply rejected or accepted abstractly; they can fail at a specific depth of refinement

## Obstruction language
Obstruction theory is useful whenever the project wants to say:
- local pieces exist
- but they do not glue globally
- or one reduction of structure exists but a deeper one does not

That is closely aligned with the thread’s support-first view of the ratchet.

## Refinement language
Refinement is the positive side of the same picture:
- start with a coarse admissible structure
- try to add more specific organization
- keep only what survives the next condition

This is why the ratchet is better modeled as a dependency/refinement chain than as a loose metaphor for “keep narrowing.”

This page sits very naturally next to:

A `G`-structure tower is one concrete instance of obstruction/refinement logic:
- a manifold admits one structure reduction
- a deeper reduction may be blocked
- the deepest surviving level tells you what the support can honestly host

## Honesty boundary
This page is conceptual/methodological. It does not yet assert that every ratchet move in the project has already been formalized in classical obstruction-theoretic terms. It says that obstruction/refinement is one very strong adjacent mathematical frame for the project’s current direction.
