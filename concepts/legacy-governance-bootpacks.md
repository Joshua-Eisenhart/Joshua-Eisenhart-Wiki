# Legacy Governance and Bootpacks

## Overview
Older bootpack and governance material. These docs are useful for provenance and historical runtime design, but they are not the current/front-door spine. The Dark Empress (Part 1 and Part 2) adds explicit economics and institutional-design content not found in the bootpacks.

Some of this material is coercive, technocratic, expansionist, or collapse-coded in tone. It should be queried as genealogy and design history, not as present governance recommendation.
Treat bootpacks, The Dark Empress, and Leviathan v3.2 as genealogy and candidate ideas with provenance; do not inherit dominion or collapse rhetoric as current guidance.

## Dark Empress Governance Genealogy

The Dark Empress devotes two substantial chapters to economics and institutional design. These are legacy-framed proposals, not current/front-door doctrine, but they establish key vocabulary that echoes across later wiki material.

### Chapter 4 — Towards a Better Money

Core claim: wealth should be measured in computing power and service capacity rather than commodity units. Key moves:

- **Computing as currency.** The argument is that if energy is the current substrate of money (oil → dollars), the next substrate is AI-enhanced computing: "Turn human driven AI into money itself." Wealth = degree of symbiosis between human judgment and machine processing.
- **Energy-to-currency genealogy.** Fossil fuels backed dollars the way gold backed older currencies. "Cheap oil gave us a near utopian world of opportunity." The successor commodity must be universally scalable and non-depletable — information and computing power are the proposed candidates.
- **Bitcoin critique.** Cryptocurrency language (ledger, scarcity, token) is the right mechanism, but without a real underlying commodity it has no floor. The proposal: use bitcoin-style infrastructure to tokenize AI compute, not scarce coins.
- **Open Finance / WeChat model.** China's WeChat ecosystem is cited as the closest living example of money becoming a general-purpose social layer: payments, identity, investing, and communication in one stream. The "Open Finance" section extends this into a global template where investments in people become fungible across platforms.
- **Capitalism will evolve, not die.** "There is no socialism in the future." The framing treats institutional collapse scenarios (zombie-world chapters) as opportunities to reboot finance on better primitives, not to replace capitalism.

### Chapter 7 — OpenGovernment

Core claim: governance is a technology system and should be held to the same upgrade cycles, accountability standards, and distributed-power logic as good software. Key moves:

- **Power scales by distribution.** "You are most powerful as a leader when the people you are leading have the most possible power." An absolute ruler of a poor country is less powerful than an elected president of a rich country. Micromanagement destroys aggregate power.
- **Tech-first state.** Government should run all processes through computers first. The "head of IT" should outrank the head of police or military. Open data and real-time analytics are the accountability substrate. A village with servers and solar power can run a better government than a legacy bureaucracy.
- **Vote-funded salaries.** Novel electoral proposal: every vote cast is matched with money that goes directly to the elected leader's operating budget. Leaders paid at market-clearing rates are less likely to seek corrupt supplement. Low vote-share means a small budget; broad mandate means a large one.
- **Open data metrics for accountability.** Five-point rubric: (1) upgrade cadence, (2) accessible real-time analytics, (3) simplicity of access for citizens, (4) ability to expose leadership failures, (5) engineers at senior levels.
- **Leadership vs. administration.** Rules work until they don't; leaders exist to break rules when breaking them is the right call. The text treats this not as corruption but as the necessary surplus beyond what rule-systems can encode.
- **Plunder framing.** Governments maximize collective prosperity partly through historically coercive means. The text treats this bluntly: resource underutilization is implicitly a provocation. This framing is legacy and should be read as genealogy for how civilizational-expansion language entered later wiki material, not as endorsed doctrine.

### Relation to current wiki framing

The economics/governance lane from The Dark Empress predates the current system's constraint-first and nominalist framing. It should be read alongside [[the-dark-empress]] for placement, and through [[legacy-speculative-frameworks]] for context. It is genealogically upstream of the wiki's current interest in open-finance primitives, distributed-authority design, and energy-as-information arguments.

The current wiki's interest in distributed authority and open-finance primitives is narrower than the manuscript's broader dominion and collapse-reorganization rhetoric. Do not inherit the latter as current doctrine by default.

## Leviathan v3.2 Governance Genealogy

Leviathan v3.2 specifies the governance layer in concrete institutional terms beyond the bootpack sketches. Key components:

- **Delegated Proxy Voting.** Votes are delegated up through multiple layers to an apex leader; the base retains veto powers at a 60% dissent threshold enforced by smart contract. Delegation is logged on blockchain and revocable at will. Described in the source as the central primitive that makes everything else work.
- **Progressive Land Value Tax (LVT).** Power-law rates on unimproved land value (1–5%), paid in SBC/CRC/ECC, fund coops and Paladin centers. Rates are set by delegated proxy voting with base-level veto. Extends Georgist LVT with progressive tiers and multi-currency payment options.
- **Paladin System.** Four specialized civic roles — Science, Guardian, Civic, Engineering — with multi-classing permitted for compounded community impact. Paladins are trained at dedicated centers and validated by P2P audits, earning role-specific currencies.
- **Trolley Car Solution.** Communities pre-set moral rules through delegated proxy voting rather than making case-by-case decisions. Leaders edit rules; enforcement is automatic. Vetoes and public social-credit-score scrutiny prevent abuse without concentrating moral authority.
- **Three-currency layer.** SBC (Social Benefit Currency — service-based), CRC (Cooperative Resource Currency — goods/assets), ECC (Ecosystem Contribution Currency — land, shares, HOA rights). Each currency is earned primarily by teaching and service delivery. New supply is minted via Mirror rank advancement, not by fiat.
- **OpenGovernment integration.** Leviathan OS provides AI-mediated governance: intent-understanding interfaces for voting and LVT proposals, self-organizing preference aggregation, blockchain logs for real-time accountability.

These v3.2 institutional specifics make governance vocabulary in later wiki pages interpretable: LVT, SBC/CRC/ECC, and Paladin references all trace here. For the full document overview see [[leviathan-framework]]. For the wider civilizational-build context see [[the-dark-empress]].
