# Field Wide Compression Probe Contract

## Claim ceiling

This page is a contract for future formal/probe/falsifier packets about field-wide compression geometry.

It does not prove the model, admit a physics result, solve gravity, close Axis0, replace physics, or promote a ToE identity claim. It only says what a later finite witness packet must name before any readout or invariant can become load-bearing.

The owner-kernel wording remains source authority:

> "it compresses acrosss its whole field and space."

> "the geometry and all its domain is like a big compression algorithm that ironically expands as it runs."

This contract protects that line from becoming either a polished ontology claim or an ordinary local update in disguise.

## When to use this page

Use [[field-wide-compression-geometry]] for the conceptual finite bookkeeping model.

Use [[axis0-physics-source-teeth-map]] before a packet formalizes old/current physics source deltas, candidate perspective identities, standing kills, decisive finite tests, or route splits.

Use this page when a worker starts turning that model into a formal packet, probe plan, falsifier list, or result-ready schema.

Allowed work here:

- source-processing;
- finite object definitions;
- probe/readout contracts;
- invariant and anti-invariant candidates;
- kill conditions;
- future packet shape.

Blocked work here:

- sim execution;
- repo code mutation;
- proof/admission language;
- empirical gravity or dark-energy claims;
- final Axis0 theorem claims;
- collapse of politics-first, consciousness-first, physics-first, and ToE-first readings.

## 1. Finite witness contract

Every candidate packet must define a finite witness at step `n`:

```text
W_n = (X_n, P_n, E_n, H_n, Q_n, R_n, V_n)
```

where:

```text
X_n       = finite support field
P_n(x)    = finite admissible possibility set at each x in X_n
E_n       = finite relation/dependency data across support cells
H_n       = finite history, provenance, and graveyard bookkeeping
Q_n       = finite probe family
R_n       = finite readout registry
V_n       = finite variant ledger
```

Required declarations:

| Field | Required declaration |
|---|---|
| `X_n` | carrier type, support count, and what a support cell means. |
| `P_n(x)` | finite cardinality or explicit finite bound for every represented cell. |
| `E_n` | relation type and edge/relation count, including cross-cell/domain edges. |
| `H_n` | what counts as killed, merged, preserved, or unresolved. |
| `Q_n` | named probes available at this witness. |
| `R_n` | readouts requested from this witness and their named objects. |
| `V_n` | live reading lanes: physics-first, politics-first, consciousness-first, ToE-first. |

Growth is allowed only across witness steps:

```text
finite W_n -> finite W_{n+1} -> finite W_{n+2}
```

No packet may treat one witness as a completed infinity. "All possibilities" must mean "all possibilities represented inside the finite witness or finite bound currently declared."

Finite witness pass:

- `X_n` is finite;
- every `P_n(x)` is finite;
- every readout names a finite object;
- the transition into the next witness is bounded or explicitly pending.

Finite witness blocker:

- a packet uses unbounded possibility language without a finite bound;
- a carrier is named but no support cells are enumerated or bounded;
- growth across steps is confused with completed infinity at one step.

Finite witness kill:

- any load-bearing readout requires actual infinite support at `n`;
- any cell has an unbounded live `P_n(x)`;
- the packet hides an infinite generator behind "etc.", ellipsis, or undefined continuation.

## 2. Whole-field dependency contract

A field-wide compression claim must define:

```text
C_n : B_n -> B_{n+1}
```

and, for each new support cell `y`, a dependency set:

```text
D_n(y) subseteq X_n
```

The packet must show that `C_n` uses cross-cell or cross-domain data through `D_n`, `E_n`, `H_n`, or `Q_n`.

Required controls:

| Control | Question |
|---|---|
| Local-only baseline | Can the same output be computed from each cell alone? |
| Relation ablation | Does removing cross-cell/domain edges change the readout? |
| Shuffle/relabel control | Does randomizing relation order or support labels destroy the claimed structure? |
| Product/null control | Does a product, disconnected, or no-relation witness produce the same result? |
| Cross-domain ledger | Which domain or variant lane supplied the dependency, and which object carries it? |

Whole-field pass:

- at least one load-bearing output depends on cross-cell/domain relation data;
- local-only and relation-ablated baselines are separated by a named probe;
- the dependency is visible in the finite object, not only in explanatory prose.

Local-only kill condition:

If every load-bearing output can be computed as:

```text
for each x: output(x) = f(x, P_n(x))
```

with no change under relation ablation or product/null controls, the packet is not a field-wide compression packet. It may still be a local update packet, but it cannot carry the owner-kernel line.

## 3. Compression-vs-expansion contract

Compression here does not mean monotone shrinkage of support size.

A packet must track at least two finite quantities:

```text
support_size     S_n = |X_n|
possibility_mass M_n = sum_{x in X_n} |P_n(x)|
```

and at least one compression quality:

```text
A_n = ambiguity / distinguishability / contradiction / unsupported-branch
      measure under named probes
```

Expansion pass:

- `S_{n+1} > S_n`, or
- `M_{n+1} > M_n`, or
- a support/possibility split increases represented resolution.

Compression pass:

- ambiguity decreases under a named probe;
- unsupported branches move into graveyard bookkeeping;
- probe-equivalent branches merge into a quotient;
- contradiction or constraint violation count decreases;
- readout comparability improves under an explicit finite criterion.

The strongest owner-kernel reading is the joint case:

```text
represented support or resolution expands
while ambiguity, contradiction, or unsupported continuation clutter compresses
under explicit probes.
```

Compression blocker:

- a packet only counts more cells and calls that compression;
- a packet only deletes cells and calls that field-wide expansion;
- a packet cannot name the ambiguity or quotient being compressed.

## 4. Readout contract

Readouts are not bare identities. Each readout must name:

```text
readout_name
finite object
probe family
pass condition
kill condition
```

| Readout | Finite object | Minimum probe | Pass condition | Kill condition |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Entropy | `P_n`, `w_n`, quotient classes, branch histories, or cut states. | finite distinguishability, branch entropy, state entropy, graph/cell entropy, or cut entropy. | The entropy value changes or separates cases under a named finite probe and control. | A scalar called entropy is asserted without object, partition, distribution, or probe. |
| Time | `H_n`, update order, support refinement order, or global step ledger. | order/reversal/shuffle probe; refinement or history asymmetry. | Reversal or shuffle controls fail differently from forward/order-preserving updates. | Time is only the index `n`, or the readout survives arbitrary relabeling without explanation. |
| Gravity | bridge/cut object such as `Xi`, `rho_AB`, synchronization relation, or binding-consistency ledger. | finite cut-consistency, binding, synchronization, coherent-information, or entanglement-information probe. | Candidate gravity readout separates bridge/cut witnesses from product/null/local controls. | "Gravity equals entropy" is asserted without bridge object, cut, probe, or negative control. |
| Dark energy | support growth, expansion ledger, scale/window growth, or resolution-growth object. | finite expansion/support-growth probe tied to `X_n`, `M_n`, or a named scale variable. | Expansion readout separates constrained field growth from unconstrained branch inflation. | Expansion is named as dark energy without finite support object or constrained-growth probe. |
| Entanglement-information | bipartition, cut state, correlation object, or branch/cut ensemble. | mutual information, coherent information, conditional entropy, entanglement witness, or correlation control. | Entangled/correlated cases separate from product/null/classical-only controls. | No bipartition or cut is named, or ordinary correlation is silently promoted to entanglement. |
| Chirality | orientation, handed frame, order asymmetry, left/right action, cell orientation, or cut orientation. | parity/orientation reversal, left-right action, frame flip, or chirality-preserving control. | Chiral asymmetry survives relabeling and fails under the appropriate reversal control. | The chirality disappears under relabeling or was only a metaphorical asymmetry. |

Readout pass does not admit the model. It only says the packet has named enough finite machinery to make the readout testable later.

## 5. Variant contract

The owner correction keeps four readings live. A contract packet must preserve them unless the packet explicitly states which lane is out of scope.

| Variant lane | What must remain visible | Failure to avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Physics-first | Spacetime, entropy, time, gravity, dark energy, entanglement-information, chirality, Axis0, JK fuzz. | Treating physics language as empirical admission or as the only source route. |
| Politics-first | 4-quadrant political compass, order/conflict structure, social feedback, constrained options. | Flattening politics into a decorative analogy after the physics story is written. |
| Consciousness-first | perception, unconscious symmetry, prediction, evolution-driven mirroring, self/object projection. | Promoting consciousness language into ontology proof or deleting it as nontechnical. |
| ToE-first | cross-field convergence and perspective-rotation target. | Collapsing all lanes into a single identity claim before bridge objects and probes exist. |

Variant pass:

- the packet states which lane or lanes it is using;
- each active lane names a finite object, route, or probe pressure;
- cross-field language is routed through [[cross-field-toe-genealogy]];
- synthesis remains conditional until all readout gates are explicit.

Variant kill:

- a packet uses politics, consciousness, or evolution only as analogy;
- a packet says physics-first is the default truth rather than the clearest current Axis0 geometry route;
- a packet says ToE-first without preserving the surviving splits and admission blockers.

## 6. Minimal candidate invariants

These are candidate invariants for future packets. None is admitted by listing it here.

| Candidate invariant | What it would preserve |
|---|---|
| Finite-witness integrity | every step has finite support and finite possibility sets. |
| Whole-field dependency | at least one readout changes under relation ablation or local-only replacement. |
| Order asymmetry | forward/order-preserving updates differ from reverse or shuffled updates. |
| Graveyard monotonicity | killed branches stay killed unless a packet defines an explicit restoration rule. |
| Probe-equivalence quotient | merged branches remain indistinguishable under the declared probe family. |
| Cut consistency | bridge/cut readouts respect the declared bipartition or cut object. |
| Chirality sign/orientation | a handed feature survives relabeling and fails under reversal. |
| Variant ledger integrity | live readings remain separated until a named bridge narrows them. |

## 7. Anti-invariants

Anti-invariants are patterns that should break or block the claim if they survive.

| Anti-invariant | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Local-only sufficiency | kills the field-wide compression claim. |
| Completed infinity at one witness | kills finite witness discipline. |
| Bare identity readout | turns a probe target into ontology prose. |
| Monotone shrinkage requirement | misreads compression as only deletion. |
| Unconstrained expansion | misreads expansion as adding branches without compression criteria. |
| Physics-only collapse | erases the owner-source genealogy. |
| Provider/model agreement | not evidence for the contract. |
| F01/N01 force-the-whole-model language | root constraints may structure a packet, but they do not prove all readouts or variants. |

## 8. Concrete falsifiers and kill conditions

Use these before promoting a future formalization packet:

1. `X_n` is infinite or unbounded at one witness.
2. Some `P_n(x)` is infinite or lacks a finite bound.
3. `C_n` decomposes into independent local maps with no load-bearing cross-cell/domain dependency.
4. Relation ablation, product/null controls, and local-only baseline all preserve the claimed readout.
5. Compression has no ambiguity, quotient, contradiction, or unsupported-branch measure.
6. Expansion only adds unconstrained branches with no merge, kill, weight, or probe relation.
7. A readout lacks object, probe, pass condition, or kill condition.
8. Gravity or dark energy is asserted without a finite bridge, cut, support-growth, or expansion object.
9. Entanglement-information lacks a bipartition/cut or confuses ordinary correlation with entanglement.
10. Chirality disappears under relabeling or is not tied to an orientation/order object.
11. Politics, consciousness, or evolution are flattened into analogy rather than preserved as variant/source routes.
12. ToE synthesis deletes the four live readings instead of stating their surviving split.
13. A packet treats owner-kernel wording, smooth prose, or LLM agreement as proof.
14. A packet runs a sim or cites a result as admission while still claiming to be this proposal-only contract.
