## 1. The Origin: Hume Applied to Plato

Entropic monism was derived by applying Hume's empiricism to Plato's metaphysics in the most literal way possible.

- Plato says reality is ideal forms; the physical world is a degraded copy
- Hume says only empirical observation counts; if you can't measure it, it isn't real
- **The synthesis:** search for what is actually the most fundamental empirical thing

**The insight:** nothingness is more than nothing.

- Spacetime itself is the base material of reality
- Physicists keep breaking matter down further (atoms → quarks → strings) looking for the base material
- The base material was always right there: **spacetime itself**
- It is the least thing that is still real and measurable
- It is called "no-thing" — yet it can be measured, it has properties, you can have more or less of it
- It is growing (expanding universe)
