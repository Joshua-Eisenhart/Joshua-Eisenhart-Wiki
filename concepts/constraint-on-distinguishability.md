# Constraint on Distinguishability

## Definition
The primitive relation is probe-relative equivalence:

s₁ ~_M s₂  iff  every measurement in M gives the same result on s₁ and s₂.

Current owner correction, 2026-06-07: this is the root form behind `a=a iff a~b` and the entropic-monism frame. F01, N01, admissibility, and quotient construction are constraint expressions of this distinguishability pressure, not deeper primitives that replace it.

The quotient S/~_M is the ontology at resolution M.

The associator is the same root applied one level up: `(AB)C = A(BC)` is not assumed; the live question is whether `(AB)C ~_M A(BC)` under an associator/bracketing probe. Current receipts place non-associativity as R3 carrier/bracketing pressure unless a future discriminator shows it changes R1/R2 admissibility itself. See [[projects/codex-ratchet/foundation-root-distinguishability-and-associator-rebuild-2026-06-07]].

## Mathematical core
- Finite carriers and finite measurement families
- Density operators and Bloch-vector representations
- Operational distinguishability via trace distance

- Fiber/base loop structure and Berry phase
- Weyl chirality and bipartite density bookkeeping

## Why it matters here
This is the lower-layer statement that makes the rest of the stack meaningful: the system only admits what can be distinguished under the allowed probes.

It is also the bridge between the constraint surface and the geometric/operator layers used later in the wiki.

More concretely:
- raw difference is not enough
- measured difference is not enough
- admissible difference is not enough
- the distinction must survive the active support/manifold/probe constraints before it becomes load-bearing for later layers

That is why this page should steer the harness, not just the math pages. If an LLM assumes that any observed separation already has causal, ontological, or explanatory force, it is already drifting off this foundation.
