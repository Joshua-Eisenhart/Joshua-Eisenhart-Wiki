# FEP Distinguishability and Operational Identity

FEP helps because its particular-physics route begins with distinguishable things. Codex Ratchet is stricter: identity is probe-relative, finite, and gate-governed.

## Crosswalk path

```text
Markov blanket / particular partition
  -> statistical distinguishability
  -> operational identity question
  -> finite probe quotient
  -> possible ratchet cut
```

Every arrow is a question, not an admission.

## Comparison

| Source language | Codex Ratchet local question | Required local evidence |
|---|---|---|
| thing distinguishable from other things | distinguishable under which probe? | finite probe/readout set |
| internal/external partition | which states are hidden or exposed? | state partition and readout map |
| blanket-mediated independence | what boundary blocks direct coupling? | independence/blocked-readout test |
| information geometry | what changes are distinguishable? | metric/divergence or invariant |
| persistence | what survives update pressure? | transition, invariant, negative case |

## QIT relation

This page can route to QIT when the local object is actually a quantum-information readout, density, channel, distinguishability measure, or finite carrier. Otherwise it stays external support.

## Blocked use

- Do not call primitive identity solved by FEP.
- Do not use "thingness" as a substitute for probe equivalence.
- Do not infer QIT from the word information.
- Do not identify Markov blanket, finite quotient, and Axis0 cut.
