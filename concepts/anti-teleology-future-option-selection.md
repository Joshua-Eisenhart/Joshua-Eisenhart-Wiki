# Anti-Teleology and Future-Option Selection

## Purpose
This page gives the wiki a clean concept surface for the user's anti-teleology / basin-teleology idea.

The core claim:

Many possible futures can pressure present selection without any one future becoming privileged, destined, or metaphysically final.

This is not ordinary teleology. It is anti-teleology: future-option pressure without final-cause privilege.

## Plain statement
A present state is selected partly by the viable continuations available from it.

That does not mean a future state reaches backward as a chosen destiny. It means the present is constrained by a field of possible continuations, and the admissible present is the one that survives those continuation constraints.

## Basin teleology
The attractor basin has a kind of directedness, but not goal-destiny.

A basin can pull trajectories without being a privileged future object. Its directedness is distributed across:
- constraint surfaces;
- viable continuations;
- negative evidence;
- perturbation stability;
- repeated convergence;
- topology of the state space;
- selection among many possible futures.

This is why the basin must emerge from wide exploration. If the basin is named first, the LLM will turn it into a final cause or a story center.

## What this inverts in LLM behavior
Normal LLM bias:
- choose a plausible endpoint;
- narrate backward from that endpoint;
- smooth alternatives into a single destiny;
- treat future-facing language as intention or goal;
- mistake thematic convergence for basin evidence.

Anti-teleology process:
- keep many futures live;
- throw many trajectories at the constraint field;
- preserve killed/open/survived branches;
- let repeated survival reveal basin structure;
- only then compress into a route, MMM, or doctrine page.

## Research support lanes
This page should draw from several research neighborhoods:

### Viability and reachability
Present states can be constrained by the set of safe/admissible future trajectories.

Useful terms:
- viability kernel;
- reachable set;
- invariant set;
- safe trajectory;
- constraint over time.

### Dynamical systems
Attractors and basins give a non-mystical account of directedness.

Useful terms:
- basin of attraction;
- perturbation;
- stability;
- bifurcation;
- invariant manifold;
- phase portrait.

### Active inference / planning-as-inference, fenced
Useful only as a support analogy:
- policies range over future trajectories;
- expected future states influence present action selection.

Fence:
- do not make FEP/active inference the root ontology;
- do not let one expected future become privileged essence.

### Quality diversity / novelty search
Supports the process rule that wide exploration is necessary under strong constraints.

Useful terms:
- novelty search;
- quality diversity;
- MAP-Elites;
- exploration/exploitation;
- fitness landscape.

### Cybernetics / teleonomy / process philosophy
Useful for directedness without metaphysical final causes.

Use as pressure lenses, not authority replacements.

- No future has privilege until a gate makes it current.

## Overclaim fences
Do not say:
- the future causes the present;
- the basin has an intended goal;
- provider agreement proves the basin;
- a dashboard proves attractor structure;
- a speculative Holodeck bridge is live engine evidence.

Safer language:
- future-option pressure;
- viable continuation constraints;
- basin-like directedness;
- attractor candidate;
- survived this probe;
- killed by this boundary;
- current status remains SPEC / exists / runs / passes local rerun / canonical by process as applicable.
