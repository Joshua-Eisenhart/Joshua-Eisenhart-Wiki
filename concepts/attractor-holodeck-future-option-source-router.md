## Owner kernel
These ideas are worth preserving as live support/route material, with evidence labels kept narrow:

### 1. Holodeck as falsifiable projection system
The Holodeck source is explicitly `SPEC — Awaiting SIM Evidence`, but it carries a strong kernel:
- claims need KILL conditions;
- projection surfaces can be described through Hopf/S³/S² language;
- density-matrix world modeling needs validity checks;
- FEP/surprise language is a mapping layer, not root proof;
- synthetic density-matrix validation is not live engine validation.

### 2. Attractor basin dashboard as evidence-indexing pattern
`DR_attractor_basin_dashboard.md` is strongest as an implementation/data-lake source:
- schema-aware indexing of heterogeneous sim results;
- evidence ledger parsing;
- token-level PASS/KILL/run facts;
- dashboarding Hopf coordinates and basin-like telemetry.

It should not be read as doctrine that an attractor basin has already been proved.

### 3. Entropic monism / Hopf as constrained emergence
`DR_entropic_monism_hopf.md` carries a strong math-alignment warning:
- information-to-geometry is not automatic;
- random CPTP dynamics tend to mix without selection;
- mutual-information distance needs regularization;
- Hopf/S³ geometry is natural for qubit pure-state geometry, but its system use must be earned.

### 4. Negative sims as basin-edge makers
`DR_negative_sims_kill_tests.md` supports the basin/emergence process:
- negative sims are not failure noise;
- KILL tests expose impossible or cheating math;

### 5. Mass sim generator as wide exploration machinery

## Anti-teleology / future-option selection
This cluster strengthens the anti-teleology frame:

- many possible futures can pressure present selection;
- no single future option is privileged;
- future references become current only when triggers/gates are satisfied;
- negative sims and KILL conditions define the basin boundary;
- mass exploration throws trajectories at the constraint field;
- basin-like direction emerges from repeated survival, not from a destined endpoint.

A future option is allowed to shape the present only as one viable continuation among many, never as the owner of the present.
