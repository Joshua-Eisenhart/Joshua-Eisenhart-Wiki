# Claim Ceiling Policy

## Purpose

This document standardizes the policy for establishing and maintaining "claim ceilings" across all wiki documents. A claim ceiling is an explicit statement of the current status and limitations of a concept, model, or theory within the project's evolving doctrine. Its primary purpose is to prevent premature promotion ("overclaim") of ideas that have not yet met the required evidentiary gates, ensuring integrity and precision in our documentation.

## Core Principles

1.  **No Upward Inference:** Claims are not inferred upward based on their presence in high-level conceptual documents. All promotions of claims (from candidate to admitted, or from source-processing to proven physics/geometry) require explicit, independently earned evidence (e.g., sim results, UNSAT proofs, formal derivations) documented elsewhere.
2.  **Explicit Fencing:** Every document presenting candidate theories, speculative models (e.g., owner physics models), or concepts awaiting formal validation MUST include an explicit "Claim Ceiling" or "Authority Boundary" section. This section clearly states what the document DOES and DOES NOT admit or promote.
3.  **Source-Processing vs. Admitted:**
    *   **Source-Processing / Frame-Loader:** Content in this category serves to preserve a model, concept, or idea for future consideration or routing. It outlines the structure or pressure of a model without asserting its validity as admitted physics, mathematics, or formal geometry. Such content frequently points to required evidence for future promotion.
    *   **Admitted:** A claim is "admitted" only when it has passed all specified evidentiary gates, typically involving formal proofs, successful simulations, or established experimental validation. Admitted claims will have direct links to the evidence that supports their status.
4.  **Specific Blocking:** Claim ceilings should specifically block promotion of concepts like "final geometric constraint manifold M(C)", "Axis0", "Xi/Phi0", "gravity solutions", "dark energy proofs", "final cosmology", or "QIT engines" unless direct, earned evidence from the project's build program supports their admission.
5.  **Referential Authority:** When discussing concepts subject to a claim ceiling, documents should always refer to the specific page that defines the ceiling (e.g., `[[sequential-universe-spinor-network-physics-model]]` for the owner's physics model kernel) before making any further claims or routing decisions.

## Enforcement

*   **Audit Requirement:** All new conceptual documents and updates to existing documents proposing or discussing theories/models must be audited for compliance with this policy.
*   **Correction Protocol:** Any detected overclaim will result in a BLOCKED status until explicit fencing is added or corrected to align with the earned evidentiary status.

## Guidance for Writing Claim Ceilings

When crafting a claim ceiling for a document, consider these questions:

*   **What is the core idea this document is preserving?** (e.g., "The owner's physics model kernel.")
*   **What is the current status of this idea?** (e.g., "Candidate," "Owner Voice," "Proposal-facing," "Source-processing only.")
*   **What specific, higher-level claims DOES THIS DOCUMENT NOT PROMOTE?** (e.g., "It does NOT admit final physics, gravity, or cosmology.")
*   **What evidence is required for future promotion of these ideas?** (e.g., "Requires formal derivation from constraint surface.")
*   **Where should workers route NEXT after reading this document for further context or evidence?**
