# LLM Constraint Harness Wiki

## Overview
This wiki is being shaped as a constraint harness for an LLM: a curated graph of current-docs-aligned pages, older legacy provenance, and explicit framing pages that bias readers toward the user's operating style without pretending every support page is a front-door controller contract.

The harness works best when the current spine is paired with an evidence-backed research inventory and a separate overlay layer of external formal traditions. That keeps the wiki from flattening into generic philosophy while still adding context where the current stack needs it.

## Main points
- The wiki is not just storage; it is a routing and instruction-support surface.
- Current-docs-aligned pages should be read before legacy or speculative material.
- Legacy pages remain useful as provenance, but they are not automatic authority.
- A longer-term goal is a nominalist wiki version that helps readers stay in the user's retrocausal, anti-Platonic framing.
- The harness should force a distinguishability-first reading: raw difference, measured difference, admissible difference, and stable difference should not be silently collapsed.
- The harness should load the broader anti-primitive rule set too: identity, equality, geometry, time, probability, and causality should not be treated as front-door primitives just because later pages use those words.
- The harness should reject presumed causality as a primitive explanation. Constraint-survival, support admissibility, and probe-relative resolution come first; causal language is only a later admitted description if the active layer earns it.
- The harness should load support-first doctrine early enough that object-first and operator-first narration do not get a head start. `runs on` is a bounded sim question, not a rhetorical convenience.
- The manifold/support layers should clean object-first and causal drift at each level. A page that loads later structure should not quietly reintroduce primitives that lower layers already rejected.
- The research overlay layer should point to exact support traditions rather than vague thematic labels.
- The wiki should keep evidence-backed research separate from harness-language pages even when the subjects overlap.
- The harness should preserve the two root constraints in routing and review language:
  - F01 → finite, bounded, witness-backed packets
  - N01 → exact order preservation, no opportunistic reordering
- The harness should preserve the deeper nominalist constraint-engineering rule:
  - if a claimed separation does not survive the active probes, support layer, and manifold constraints, it does not yet count as load-bearing distinction
- The harness should preserve the graveyard-first scientific read:
  - killed/open/negative evidence is real output
  - the system is not only a winner-selection machine
  - status language should not flatten `open` or `killed` into `pass/fail/proven`
- The harness should bias models away from "nearby useful work" when that work violates requested process order, without pretending this page itself is the load-bearing authority surface.

## Runtime / controller enforcement
- Keep role separation explicit:
  - recon/advocacy surfaces are not admission surfaces
  - sim runners emit artifacts; they do not interpret or promote
  - enforcement/admission surfaces should stay candidate-blind where that is the job
- Preserve fail-closed status discipline:
  - do not upgrade `exists` into `runs`
  - do not upgrade `runs` into `passes local rerun`
  - do not upgrade local success into broader admission without the required process/tool/depth checks
- Preserve no-inference / no-repair / no-smoothing behavior when the active task is enforcement rather than exploration.

## Sim enforcement
- Treat sims as contracts, not vibes:
  - declare role/resolution/tools/artifacts/negatives/promotion status
  - keep claim ceilings tied to the declared resolution and artifact surface
  - missing negatives or missing artifacts should block promotion rather than get narratively repaired
- Design sims carrier/probe first, not bridge/story first.
- Sim geometry early on the same carrier rather than treating geometry as a late decorative layer.
- Treat stack order as something to test, not something to freeze from preference.
- Keep negative controls and graveyard output as first-class deliverables.
- Interpret survivors as not-yet-killed structures under the active constraints, not as timeless proof or closure.
