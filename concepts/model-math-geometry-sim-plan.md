# Model, Math, Geometry, and Simulation Plan

## Overview
Synthesis layer tying the owner model to the research stack. Does not declare canon by itself. Proposes how math and geometry should be simulated, tested, and promoted. Narrower docs outrank this one.

## Core Correction
Do not treat entropy as the only target. Do not treat any proposed axis math as admitted until simulated. Do not treat geometry as one thing. The system should test the full family of allowed math and geometry from the start, then let constraints narrow it.

That narrowing should be read as a support-first dependency chain, not just a family sweep. Candidate supports, candidate `runs on` relations, classical baselines, and negative variants all belong inside the same ratchet program. See [[support-first-constraint-manifold-dependency-chain]].

## Owner-Model Alignment
Current thesis: primitive substrate = [[constraint-on-distinguishability-full-math|constraint on distinguishability]]; probes are fundamental to identity; entropy is a later admissible measure; density matrices are natural base object; geometry is constraint compatibility on that carrier; correlation, entanglement, and operator structure are not decorative.

Right question: "Which families of math survive simulation at each admissible layer?"

## Base Mathematical Primitives
Likely fundamental objects: density matrices/density operators, probes/admissible probe families, channels/CPTP maps, partial traces, reduced states, correlation tensors, graph and cell-complex carriers, Pauli and Clifford generators.

Working hypothesis: density matrices and probes are probably the first stable language. Geometry appears as admissible structure on top. Axes are families of constrained operations on those objects.

## What Must Be Simmed Before Promotion
Every proposed math family or axis family must be simulated first: entropy families, correlation measures, geometry families, operator families, axis families, bridge families, kernel families. Only "real" after surviving: base carrier, geometric constraint manifold, negative controls, cross-representation bridge checks, same-state/different-probe tests.

## Geometry From Step One
Test all allowed geometry candidates early: spherical/Hopf, torus/nested torus, cell-complex/graph, Clifford/spinor, operator-transport, bridge geometry. Point is to see which geometries are actually admissible under the constraints, not to pick one first.
