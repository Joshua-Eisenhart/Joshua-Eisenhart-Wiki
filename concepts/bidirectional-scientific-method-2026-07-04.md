## Run the method in reverse

The standard scientific method runs inductive: observe, then theorise. The owner's addition is to run it in reverse to build new theories.

Owner, verbatim: "In order to create theories, the method has to be operated in reverse. Data is created before the experiment, and the scientist searches for experiments to generate the data they want. This is how Faraday and Einstein created their new paradigms."

In the reverse direction, the theory starts as a story — elegant, emotionally resonant, symbolically coherent (Faraday's visual field lines, Einstein's thought experiments). Owner: "Empiricism can't tell stories nor imagine cause." But the story must still be built out of a massive body of prior empirical experiments — it is not invention from nothing.

## Two axes, held apart

1. **Inductive-Humean** ("Observe everything, assume nothing"): universals are never assumed, causality is never presumed, only correlations; experiments tighten correlations, they do not establish cause. Morality and conscience sit at the centre of this empirical nominalism. Owner: "Your deepest emotions are more real than thoughts and reason. Reason is innately a rationalization of one's feelings, AND ALWAYS SHOULD BE SO."
2. **Deductive-Rationalist** ("Imagine first, then verify"): theories begin as stories, built from empirical material, then tested.

The owner is not anti-rationalism. He trained himself in both maximal rationalist and maximal empiricist skepticism. The system aims to save rationalism by regrounding it — merging rationalism and empiricism via Hume and Jung as the mechanism, with empiricism first in process order. Owner: "reason is not real" in the sense that to make reason work you must understand how it actually operates in the mind, not treat it as a metaphysical ideal.

## Observer in the box

Leviathan itself is framed as "a scientific box to put the scientist inside... along with their observations, experiments, theory building, and model verification. It controls for all the variables." The owner's diagnosis: this is the only way human science actually works, because without it people "burn the science" on humans — the observer's own variables (bias, incentive, model attachment) go unmeasured.

## Popper expanded: semantic AND numeric failure

Standard Popperian falsification checks numeric failure only. The owner's expansion requires predictions to fail on two axes: numerically, and semantically — legible, useful, emotionally aligned.

Owner: "Failure is not rejection, but signal — a route back into inductive discovery." And: "Modern uses of Popper rarely to never actually use this key step. They hold on to things and don't let them burn."

The two traps, named as law: "Rationalists hold on to pet models. Empiricists deny models." Each side is trained in the other's hygiene — rationalists trained to release dead theories, empiricists trained to honour genius — as the institutional cure for both unpaired-pole death modes.

## Science as moral practice

Owner: "Science must improve the lives of its participants — or it is invalid." And: "Our moral senses are the foundation of science and not an abstraction." The "Ugly Mirror" is the device that confronts model addiction, ego, and narrative rigidity directly.
