# Support Spaces and Process Classification

## Overview
A major thread-level claim of the project is that the kind of process that can occur depends on the support space that hosts it. This page gives that claim a cleaner mathematical form.

A support space is not just a container. It helps determine:
- what objects can exist there
- what local transformations are admissible
- what global organization is possible
- what invariants or obstruction classes matter

## Core idea
A process type can be classified by:
1. its ambient support space
2. the admissible local directions / objects / operators on that support
3. the compatibility data needed to assemble local structure globally
4. the invariants or obstructions that survive change of presentation

This is broader than one specific mathematical formalism. Depending on the problem, the support may be:
- a manifold
- a stratified space
- a bundle or tower of bundles
- a moduli/classifying space
- a support space of admissible subspaces

## Why it matters here
This project keeps returning to questions like:
- which graph paper is the right one?
- what can run on what?
- which structures survive deeper constraints?

Those are all support-classification questions.

## Support-first reading of the stack
The support-first reading is:
- identify candidate support spaces
- test what local structures can live there
- test what global compatibility is possible
- compare support families by what they admit, obstruct, or force

This is a stronger formulation of the thread’s support-first dependency-chain framing, and it helps separate:
- a support space
- a local object family
- an operator family
- a coupling rule
- an emergent quantity
