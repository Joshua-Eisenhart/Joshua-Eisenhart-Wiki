# F01 and N01 Root Constraint Basin Pressure

## What this page is

This page is the first educational wiki surface for the corrected formal-sim goal:

`F01_FINITUDE` plus `N01_NONCOMMUTATION` are not background slogans. They are the root pressure that may or may not force the system's layered finite noncommutative geometry.

The live question is not “which beautiful geometry do we like?”

The live question is:

> If only finitude and noncommutation are admitted at the root, what geometry, operators, topology, entropy behavior, and basin structure survive?

That is why manifold layers, G-structures, Hopf/S3 carriers, quaternion/SU(2)/Spin carriers, cellular carriers, ring-checkerboard models, gauge structure, and terrain operators must all be treated as candidate survivors rather than primitives.

## The two roots in plain language

### F01 — Finitude

F01 says no foundation-level claim gets to use an actually infinite carrier, unbounded witness set, unconstrained registry, or infinite-gradation continuum as a primitive.

A claim must show a finite or bounded surface such as:

- finite carrier
- finite witness set
- finite registry
- finite cell complex
- finite graph / hypergraph / simplicial complex
- bounded tensor-network scout
- bounded probe surface
- finite receipt-bearing computation

This does not mean “small” or “trivial.” It means every admitted witness must be bounded enough to be inspected, rerun, registered, or finitely represented.

### N01 — Noncommutation

N01 says order matters at the root.

Two operations, probes, updates, or constraints do not automatically commute. If applying `A` then `B` differs from applying `B` then `A`, the difference is not noise. It may be the first sign of time, direction, chirality, ratchet behavior, or basin boundary.

A claim must show finite witnesses of order-sensitive composition, such as:

- `AB != BA`
- different outcomes under order shuffle
- nonzero commutator
- path-dependent update
- nontrivial holonomy around a loop
- chirality-sensitive operator behavior
- irreversible receipt ordering
- update sequence that cannot be freely permuted

## Why these roots matter for attractor basins

An attractor basin is not just a cluster of repeated motifs.

Under this system's root logic, a basin has to be a finite, order-sensitive survivor region:

- finite/bounded enough to be witnessed under F01
- order-sensitive enough to avoid commutative collapse under N01
- stable enough to have an invariant or recurring boundary behavior
- fragile enough that negatives can kill fake explanations

A basin claim therefore needs more than similarity. It needs a state space, update rule, boundary, invariant, and escape cases.

## What the roots pressure into existence

The root pair pressures the search space in a specific way:

| Candidate structure | Why F01 matters | Why N01 matters | What must be tested |
|---|---|---|---|
| finite cell complex | avoids infinite gradation | cells compose/glue in order-sensitive ways | cell-vs-continuous ablation |
| graph / hypergraph carrier | finite relation carrier | edge/path composition may be noncommuting | order-shuffle and null graph controls |
| Hopf/S3 carrier | bounded compact carrier candidate | phase/fiber loops can carry holonomy/order | Hopf removed/transplanted controls |
| SU(2)/Spin/quaternion carrier | finite-dimensional algebraic carrier | multiplication is noncommuting | quaternion-vs-complex ablation |
| G-structure tower | finite admissibility ladder on frames | reductions may be order/obstruction sensitive | obstruction and reduction-order checks |
| ring-checkerboard model | finite symbolic/cellular carrier | ring transitions and checkerboard alternation can be order-sensitive | ring-off/checkerboard-off ablation |
| Lindblad/operator dynamics | finite matrix/operator witness | commutator plus dissipator order matters | Hamiltonian sign/order controls |
| entropy readout | finite spectra/registries | entropy changes under update order | pressure-off and symmetric-flux controls |

None of these is granted by resemblance. Each must survive root tests.

## What would count as a failure

The root-basin program can fail in several ways:

- F01-only gives finite structures, but no genuine order-sensitive geometry.
- N01-only gives noncommuting algebra, but no finite bounded carrier that can serve as an admitted surface.
- Both roots hold locally, but they do not force layered geometry.
- The apparent basin is just clustering or repeated motif, not a basin with boundary and invariant.
- A gauge choice creates the illusion of structure, but gauge-invariant observables do not preserve it.
- A continuous model works, but finite cellular/ring-checkerboard witnesses fail.
- A tool stub prints success without load-bearing proof or graph/tensor evidence.
- A classical baseline explains the phenomenon without manifold/basin structure.

These failures are useful. They prevent the wiki from turning candidate geometry into doctrine too early.

## Educational intuition

Think of F01 as the demand that the system can always point to a bounded witness.

Think of N01 as the demand that order cannot be erased.

Together they ask:

> What finite structures remember order?

That question naturally points toward finite noncommutative geometry, ordered receipts, graph/cell complexes, spin/Clifford witnesses, holonomy, chirality, tensor-network dynamics, and ratchet-like basin behavior.

But “points toward” is not “proves.” The current formal sim campaign is meant to prove or kill the stronger claim.
