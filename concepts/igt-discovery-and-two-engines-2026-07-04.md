## 1. What IGT is

Irrational Game Theory. Owner-original. It differs from classical game theory by
refusing two of its presumptions:

- It does not privilege winning. Losing strategies are equally valid.
- It does not privilege rationality. Strategies are affective states (4F), not
  utility computations. "Irrational" is Jung's technical sense: the perceiving
  functions — perception as a form of intelligence that computes, differently
  from reason, and is not passive intake (owner 2026-07-04).

Casing convention: CAPS (WIN/LOSE) = maximize / big; lowercase (win/lose) =
minimize / small. Casing is loop level, not position.

## 2. The inversion of classical game theory (owner 2026-07-04)

Classical decision theory computes four corner criteria on a payoff matrix:
maximax, maximin, minimax-regret, minimin. The four IGT quadrants correspond:
WinWin=maximax, WinLose=maximin, LoseWin=minimax-regret, LoseLose=minimin.
Classical theory recommends only the security corner (maximin; minimax for
adversaries) and its core algorithm — iterated elimination of dominated
strategies — deletes minimin by construction.

IGT keeps all four corners and crowns minimin ("the king of strategy, where
classical has no use for it" — owner). Same matrix, nothing added, pruning
refused, ranking inverted.

Fences: this is chart grammar plus owner doctrine, not proved dynamics. The
"quantum game theory" branding is explicitly rejected (owner: leads to endless
BS); any QIT connection must arrive later through the mechanism surface, not by
bolting superposition onto matrices.
