# Platonic Nominalism

## Overview

"Platonic nominalism" names the owner's stance. Forms survive probes, so they are real. Forms are not separate from matter, so they are not Platonic in the classical sense. The term holds both commitments at once without collapsing one into the other.

This page exists because the label was present only as a frontmatter tag before 2026-04-17. Worker agents could read about nominalism and about forms without ever encountering the composite name, and the tension the label carries was being lost.

## What the label fixes

Two failure modes the composite label is meant to block:

- pure nominalism read: "forms are names, not real" — collapses the fact that surviving patterns are what the system tracks
- pure Platonism read: "forms live in a separate realm" — collapses the fact that survival is material, probe-scoped, and finite

Platonic nominalism holds the middle: forms are admitted structure under a probe family, not names and not separate abstracta.

## Relation to the root axiom

The root axiom `a = a iff a ~ b` (see [[15_root_axiom_card]] and [[nominalism-in-this-system]]) gives the mechanical content. Identity is derived from probe-relative indistinguishability. A "form" in this system is an equivalence class under `~_M` for an active probe family M — a survivor, not a primitive.

This is why the label is "nominalist" on the anti-abstract side: there is no form prior to probes. It is "Platonic" on the realism side: the survivor class is a real pattern, not a linguistic convenience.

## Operational consequences

- do not write "the form X exists" without a probe family and an admissibility context
- do not write "X is just a name" — survival under repeated probes is stronger than naming
- when a classical default would import either a Platonic or a pure-nominalist shorthand, restate with probe + admissibility scope

See [[03_language_discipline]] and [[17_pre_emit_audit]] for the discipline that enforces this.

## Why it matters for the engine

The QIT engine and the coupling program assume forms are material survivors. If the stance drifts to classical Platonism, the engine becomes a lookup of pre-existing objects. If it drifts to pure nominalism, the engine becomes label manipulation. Platonic nominalism keeps the engine doing what it is built to do: narrow branch sets under active constraints and report which patterns survive.
