# FEP Particular Physics and NESS

This page routes Friston's particular-physics language without letting it close local physics or Axis0 claims.

## Useful source idea

The particular-physics route describes distinguishable "things" through statistical independencies, Markov blankets, and nonequilibrium steady-state structure. It is useful because it links persistence, boundary, inference, and information geometry in one external vocabulary.

## Local vocabulary

| Term | External role | Wiki use | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|---|
| NESS | nonequilibrium steady-state | persistence/self-organization language | must not become proof of survival without local dynamics |
| particular partition | partition into internal, external, sensory, active states | boundary/cut support | not automatically a Codex cut |
| thingness | distinguishability under a partition | neighbor to operational identity | not primitive identity |
| solenoidal flow | circulation on iso-density contours in FEP formulations | open math neighbor | critique-sensitive and assumption-heavy |
| dissipative flow | gradient-like descent on surprisal | prediction-first support | not all dynamics are shown to be this |

## Fit to Codex Ratchet

The fit is strongest at the language level:

```text
statistical distinguishability
  -> boundary/thingness vocabulary
  -> operational identity questions
  -> finite cut/probe questions
```

The fit is weakest when the page is used to leap directly into physics metaphysics.

## Blocked use

- Do not write `particular physics = gravity solved`.
- Do not use NESS language as a substitute for a local carrier, transition, invariant, or result.
- Do not claim Axis0 bridge closure from Markov blankets or particular partitions.
- Do not skip the critique pages when using solenoidal-flow or generality claims.
