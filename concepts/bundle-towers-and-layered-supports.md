# Bundle Towers and Layered Supports

## Overview
If the project’s phrase “topologies running on topologies in constrained order” is to become mathematically sharper, bundle towers are one of the strongest candidates.

A fiber bundle already separates:
- a base space
- fibers over each point
- transition/gluing data

A bundle tower or iterated fibration turns this into a staged support architecture.

## Why this matters here
The project repeatedly needs a language for:
- one support constraining another
- local structure varying over a broader support
- ordered buildup where later structure depends on earlier survivals

Bundle language gives a precise version of that idea without forcing everything into one flat manifold.

## Support-space interpretation
In project language:
- the base can encode a coarse support or slower structural layer
- the fiber can encode a local state family or local admissible object family
- sections encode globally coherent realizations
- transition/gluing data encode compatibility conditions

An iterated tower then models layered support in which one level depends on another in order.

## Why this is close to the ratchet
This framework naturally fits the ratchet as dependency chain:
- lower-level compatibility can block higher-level realization
- local structure may exist without a global section
- obstructions can kill one branch of development while leaving another alive

That is much closer to the project’s actual style than a generic “all structure lives on one plane” reading.
