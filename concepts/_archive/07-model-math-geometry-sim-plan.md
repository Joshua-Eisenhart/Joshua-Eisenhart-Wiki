# Model, Math, Geometry, and Simulation Plan

## Overview
Synthesis layer tying the owner model to the research stack. Proposes how the math and geometry should be simulated, tested, and only then promoted. Does not declare canon by itself. Narrower docs outrank this one.

## Core Correction

Do not treat entropy as the only target. Do not treat any proposed axis math as admitted until simulated. Do not treat geometry as one thing. Test the full family of allowed math and geometry from the start, then let constraints narrow it.

## Owner-Model Alignment

Primitive substrate = constraint on distinguishability. Probes are fundamental to identity. Entropy is later, not primitive. Density matrices are natural base object. Geometry is constraint compatibility on that carrier.

## What Must Be Simulated Before Promotion

Every proposed math family or axis family must be simulated first. A family only becomes "real" after it survives: (1) relevant base carrier, (2) geometric constraint manifold at that layer, (3) negative controls, (4) cross-representation bridge checks, (5) same-state/different-probe tests.

## The Simulation Ladder

| Layer | What to test |
|---|---|
| 0: Constraint/probe admissibility | what counts as probe, distinguishability, which representations violate base rules |
| 1: Carrier admission | finite carrier candidates, density matrix representability, positivity/trace/normalization |
| 2: Geometry candidates | Hopf/sphere/torus, graph/cell complex, Clifford/spinorial, operator-transport, bridge geometry |
| 3: Operator family admission | Pauli, Clifford, local vs joint, channels, left/right action asymmetry, composition order |
| 4: Bipartite structure | partial traces, joint density matrices, MI, coherent information, concurrence, Schmidt |
| 5: Entropy families | Shannon, vN, Rényi, Tsallis, min/max, conditional, MI, coherent info, entanglement entropy |
| 6: Axis families | Axis 0 polarity, Axis 6 left/right action asymmetry |

## Bottom Line

Build as: constraint first, admissible carrier and probe families next, all allowed geometries simulated early, all proposed math tested on same states, only then narrow to surviving operators and measures.
