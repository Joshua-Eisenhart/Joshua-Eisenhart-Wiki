# Cross-View Attractor And Strong Nominalism Ledger - 2026-07-10

Status: researched possibility space plus a proposal-only Ratchet ledger. This
page is not canon.

## Core Target

The proposed unification is not that an electron, organism, mind, program, and
company are literally the same surface object. It is that they may be
probe-relative, scale-relative quotient objects of one finite
constraint-survival process.

For a domain `d` with probe family `Pi_d`:

```text
x ~_d y  iff  every p in Pi_d gives indistinguishable outcomes on x and y.
X_d = U / ~_d.
```

Strong nominalism adds a crucial restraint: the attractor is not a hidden
Platonic thing behind these views. It is the invariant overlap that survives
translation, reconstruction, ablation, and intervention across independently
built views.

## What Would Make Two Views One Object

For a core evolution `T`, domain projection `Q_d`, and independently specified
domain evolution `T_d`, require a measured semiconjugacy:

```text
Q_d(T(x)) ~= T_d(Q_d(x)).
```

This must not be installed by defining `T_d = Q_d T Q_d^-1`. A valid bridge
needs:

1. independently built core and domain dynamics;
2. a declared finite probe family and quotient in each view;
3. a shared invariant not copied into both implementations;
4. a prediction transported from one view to another;
5. a paired intervention that breaks the structure in both.

The evidence ladder is:

```text
analogy
< structural match
< shared invariant
< semiconjugate dynamics
< cross-domain prediction
```

Only the final two justify "the same underlying attractor."

## Mathematical Instruments

### Noncommutative Transport

Carlen and Maas prove that finite-dimensional detailed-balance quantum Markov
semigroups are gradient flows of relative entropy in a particular
noncommutative transport metric. This can connect a terrain Lindbladian to a
dynamical geometry, but only when its ergodicity and detailed-balance
hypotheses are checked. It does not automatically apply to every installed
GKSL terrain: [Carlen-Maas](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.01254).

BKM geometry probes local state distinguishability. The Carlen-Maas metric
instead constrains admissible movement generated by a semigroup. Their relation
is a real research question, not an identity to assume.

### Tilted Histories And Quantum Doob Transforms

Large-deviation methods construct an alternative Markovian open-system process
whose typical trajectories realize rare long-time trajectory classes of the
original process: [Carollo et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.10951).

This supplies rigorous machinery for finite history weighting and conditioned
survivor dynamics. It does not prove teleology, future causation, or zero
message capacity. Those must be separate gates.

### Coalgebraic Behavior

Coalgebra treats transition and dynamical systems through observable behavior;
bisimulation and coinduction support identity by behavior rather than internal
essence: [Rutten, Universal coalgebra](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/courses/cs6861/2024sp/Handouts/Rutten.pdf).

This is a promising CS view of probe-relative objecthood. Importing a full
coalgebra at the floor would still assume set/function structure not yet earned.

### Sheaf Contextuality

Abramsky and Brandenburger formalize contextuality as an obstruction to a
global section over compatible local measurement contexts:
[primary paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1102.0264).

This is useful for local views that need not glue into one privileged global
description. The Ratchet's existing Peres-Mermin result supports one narrow
fixed-cover obstruction; its later context-indexed wrappers were correctly
caught as carrier-smuggling or tautological. Sheaf language must not restore
those rejected claims.

## MSS And Uniqueness

Let `A <= B` mean that `A` presumes no more structure than `B`, and let
`Surv(C)` be executable, persistent, evolvable candidates under constraints
`C`. MSS targets the minimal elements of `Surv(C)`, but there may be several
incomparable minima.

The nominalist uniqueness target is therefore:

```text
all A,B in MSS(C) remain indistinguishable under the complete admissible
cross-domain probe family Pi_*.
```

That would earn uniqueness up to admissible distinguishability. It must not be
assumed as a premise.

## Association Correction

The weakest floor is association-unspecified, not positively
nonassociative. It records partial bracketed composition without assuming an
associative law. Genuine nonassociativity requires a defined triple with

```text
(ab)c != a(bc).
```

Finitude, MSS, and witnessed noncommutation can come first. Octonionic or other
nonassociative structure enters only when a nonzero associator and its required
work are independently measured.

## First Executable Cards

1. **Carlen-Maas terrain discriminator:** identify which current terrains
   satisfy detailed balance; derive the transport metric only for those; match
   stationary state and entropy while breaking detailed balance as the kill.
2. **Tilted-history/Doob gate:** construct one finite trajectory observable,
   derive the transformed CPTP process, and separately test controllable
   signalling, one-history collapse, and erased tilt.
3. **Cross-view semiconjugacy instrument:** build a neutral finite fixture that
   can reject a bad projection; classify it as instrument-only until applied to
   independently sourced domain dynamics.
4. **Lev behavioral bridge:** freeze equal model/context/tool budgets; compare
   raw and engine-assisted behavior; mutate receipts, quorum, idempotency, and
   terrain labels; return evidence to Ratchet without runtime state mutation.
5. **FEP/open-reaction bridge:** define a finite viability kernel and boundary
   probe family before using consciousness or life language.
