# Research Support Bibliography

Audited 2026-04-11 for schema compliance and harness routing.

## Overview
This page collects external support sources that explain why the current wiki keeps using relation-first, geometry-first, process-first, and prediction-first language around distinguishability, control, correlative thinking, mathematical explanation, recursive science method, and world-coupled memory/perception. It is not a formal proof page. It is a routing page for the support layer behind [[current-research-overlays]], [[chinese-philosophy-reference]], [[process-philosophy-and-relational-physics]], and the newer holodeck / Leviathan / QIT integration cluster.

It is also downstream of [[current-research-overlays]] and [[source-notes]] rather than a provenance source on its own: use it to route support material, not to establish primary evidence.

## Definition
A support bibliography is a controlled list of outside references that help interpret the system’s current architecture without becoming the architecture itself. The sources here support the reasoning style of the wiki: explanation by structure, by admissibility, by correlative relation, and by geometric constraint.

## Cluster 1: Mathematical explanation
The SEP entry on mathematics explanation is useful because it separates two questions that the wiki also separates:
- can mathematics explain physical phenomena?
- can mathematics explain internal mathematical structure?

That split matters here because the system often asks whether a quantity is merely descriptive or genuinely load-bearing. The relevant lesson is that mathematical structure can explain without being causal in the ordinary mechanical sense.

The tennis-racket dynamics paper by Turrin and Broucke is useful as a classic example of explanation through geometry and motion constraints. It fits the wiki’s insistence that structure, symmetry, and stability can be explanatory without reducing the result to a simple cause-effect story.

## Cluster 2: Correlative thinking and yin-yang support
The yin-yang / correlative-thinking sources support the page [[chinese-philosophy-reference]] and its distinction between process language and substance language.

Main idea:
- reality is read as mutual conditioning
- distinctions arise in patterned relation
- opposition can be complementary rather than absolute
- naming is a discipline, not a metaphysical guarantee

These sources are strongest when used as support for non-substance, relation-first description. They are not substitutes for the formal operator math used elsewhere in the stack.

## Cluster 3: Chinese philosophy support pages
The Kongzi site material and the SFU PDF are useful as background support for Confucian and Daoist framing, especially where the wiki needs to keep:
- rectification of names separate from formal proof
- correlative process separate from causal mechanism
- transformation language separate from static substance claims

This cluster is especially relevant when the wiki discusses [[process-philosophy-and-relational-physics]], [[nominalism-in-this-system]], and [[i-ching-axes-rosetta]].

## Cluster 7: Axis0 / ratchet / future-option pressure support

The 2026-06-05 Axis0 and entropic-monism route needs a support lane that does not force the model into ordinary causal, teleological, or physics-first language. This cluster should support the questions and vocabulary, not promote the model.

Useful support traditions:

- **Viability theory and reachability** — for finite admissible continuations, safe sets, survival under constraints, and the distinction between possible trajectory and admitted trajectory.
- **Nonlinear dynamics and basin stability** — for perturbation, return, basin boundary, multistability, and the difference between a basin-like survivor region and a single final attractor.
- **Cybernetics and autopoiesis/enactivism** — for world-coupled feedback, structural coupling, and process identity without primitive object essence.
- **Active inference and path-integral control** — for finite future-policy/path weighting language, fenced away from completed-infinity path integrals and from final-cause privilege.
- **Quality diversity and novelty search** — for wide exploration under constraints before compression, which supports the rule that strong constraints require a broad graveyard rather than one early winner.
- **Operational probabilistic theories and QIT geometry** — for state/effect/probe language, distinguishability, density/spinor readouts, and geometry as support/readout rather than primitive container.

The key fence: research alignment can make the model easier to state and test, but it does not change status labels. Support literature gives pressure lenses and candidate formal languages; only finite witnesses, controls, and result/proof surfaces can admit a narrower claim.

## Key structures
The external sources in this bibliography reinforce seven recurring structures in the wiki:
1. explanation by geometric or structural constraint
2. explanation by relational comparison rather than essence
3. explanation by correlative pattern rather than linear causation
4. explanation by admissibility and survivability rather than Platonic certainty
5. explanation by prediction-first model correction rather than passive input processing
6. explanation by recursive empirical feedback rather than one-shot theory assertion
7. explanation by future-option pressure as viability/admissibility structure rather than teleology or backward-causal privilege
