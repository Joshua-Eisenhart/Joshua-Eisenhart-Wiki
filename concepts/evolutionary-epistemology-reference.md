# Evolutionary Epistemology and Universal Darwinism: Formal Reference

## Overview
Reference doc covering the actual traditions of evolutionary epistemology, universal Darwinism, niche construction, major transitions, and constructor theory. Not the system's application — the source material.

Source-mixing fence: the evolutionary epistemology reference is external/source-reference material; the Dark Empress linkage is legacy genealogy only and does not promote system claims.

## Three Programs of Evolutionary Epistemology

**EEM (Lorenz, 1941-43):** How cognitive capacities evolved. What is ontogenetically a priori (innate) is phylogenetically a posteriori (evolved). Our categories of space, time, causality are adaptations shaped by environment, not features of pure reason. They are fallible — selection weeds out grossly maladaptive, not subtly wrong.

**EET (Popper/Campbell):** Evolutionary models as descriptions of how knowledge grows. Formula: P1 -> TT -> EE -> P2 (Problem -> Tentative Theory -> Error Elimination -> New Problem). Knowledge never terminates — each solution generates new problems.

**Third program:** Historical emergence of abstract entities (number, value) preceding individual acquisition.

## Popper's Evolutionary Epistemology

Knowledge growth structurally identical to biological evolution: conjectures = variation (bold, unjustified hypotheses), refutations = selection (severe critical tests), survivors provisionally retained. Falsifiability = demarcation: scientific iff in principle falsifiable. Generalized to all problem-solving from amoeba to Einstein. Einstein's advantage: he lets theories die in his stead.

## Campbell's BVSR (1960)

Blind Variation and Selective Retention: three components — blind variation (candidates not pre-adapted to solution, emission independent of environmental conditions), selective retention (differential fitness), retention and propagation. Superordinate process encompassing perception, learning, creativity, cultural evolution. Vicarious selectors: nested hierarchies where higher-level processes (thought experiments) substitute for lower-level ones (physical trial and error) at lower cost.

## Universal Darwinism

Evolution as substrate-independent algorithm. Three conditions: variation, selection, retention (heredity). Dawkins (1983): replicator/vehicle framework — replicators are copied with fidelity, fecundity, longevity. Dennett (1995): natural selection as "substrate-neutral, mindless algorithm" — the single best idea anyone has ever had. Hull: replicator/interactor distinction, more general than Dawkins' "vehicle." Candidates: genes, memes, theories, technologies, neurons, words, programs, firms, antibodies, institutions.

## Neutral Theory and Drift (Kimura, 1968)

At molecular level, majority of changes driven by random drift of neutral mutations, not selection. In finite populations, neutral alleles reach fixation by chance. Nearly neutral theory (Ohta): mutations with |s| < 1/2Ne behave as effectively neutral. Neutral networks (Wagner): many genotypes produce the same phenotype, forming connected networks — populations drift along neutral networks to arrive near different fitness basins without crossing valleys. Robustness and evolvability linked through neutral network topology.

## Evolvability

Modularity: semi-independent modules reduce pleiotropy. Facilitated variation (Kirschner & Gerhart, 2005): conserved core processes weakly linked, combinatorially versatile — novel phenotypes from rewiring existing robust components, not inventing new mechanisms. Developmental bias: development channels variation toward functional variants because development itself is evolved.

## Major Transitions (Maynard Smith & Szathmary, 1995)

Eight transitions: replicating molecules to protocells to chromosomes to DNA/protein to eukaryotes to sexual to multicellular to societies to language. Common pattern: previously independent entities lose independent replication, become parts of larger whole. A RATCHET — once the transition occurs, components cannot revert. Two defining features: transition in individuality, and novel inheritance system.

## Niche Construction (Odling-Smee, Laland, Feldman, 2003)

Organisms systematically modify environments; modifications change selection pressures on constructor and others. Reciprocal causation: not one-way arrows. Ecological inheritance: second inheritance system alongside genetic (beaver kit inherits dam). Key implications: systematic bias on selection pressures, positive feedback loops, time-lagged effects, co-evolutionary dynamics.

## Extended Evolutionary Synthesis

Adds beyond Modern Synthesis: developmental bias, phenotypic plasticity (plasticity-first evolution inverts standard sequence), inclusive inheritance (Jablonka and Lamb's four dimensions: epigenetic, behavioral, ecological, symbolic), niche construction, evolvability, multilevel selection. Core shift: organism as active participant constructing its own trajectory, reciprocal causation.

## Constructor Theory (Deutsch & Marletto, 2013-2015)

Laws as statements about which tasks are possible vs impossible. Constructor: entity that can cause a task while retaining ability to cause it again. Constructor theory of information: information medium = physical system with distinguishable attributes. Superinformation media (quantum) = copy-all impossible, all transformations reversible. Constructor theory of life (Marletto, 2015): any laws permitting information media must permit replicators. Knowledge in constructor theory: information that tends to cause itself to remain instantiated — a physical characterization connecting to Popper.
