# Clifford Algebra in Quantum Information Theory

## Overview
Covers Clifford algebra Cl(p,q) definition, Cl(3,0) as the algebra of physical 3-space, and its relationship to quantum information. References: Hestenes (1986), Doran & Lasenby (2003), Somaroo et al (2001).

Authority boundary: this is a reference/support page. Standard Clifford facts can be used as math background; source-system mappings and legacy analogies do not by themselves prove repo admission, bridge closure, or `canonical by process` status.

## Clifford Algebra Cl(p,q): Definition
Cl(p,q) is the real associative algebra generated by e_1, ..., e_n (n=p+q) subject to: e_i e_j + e_j e_i = 2 g_{ij}, where g = diag(+1,...,+1,-1,...,-1) with p positive and q negative entries. Dimension 2^n. Basis: all products of distinct generators, graded by degree k (scalars, vectors, bivectors, ..., pseudoscalar).

## Cl(3,0): The Algebra of Physical 3-Space
Three generators e_1, e_2, e_3 with e_i^2=1, e_i e_j = -e_j e_i for i!=j. Complete basis: 8 elements -- grade 0 (scalar), grade 1 (3 vectors), grade 2 (3 bivectors), grade 3 (pseudoscalar e_{123}).

## Pseudoscalar Properties
e_{123}^2 = -1. In the cited source mapping, chirality is read through the pseudoscalar e_{123}; the sign flip is treated as its eigenvalue on each sheet (H_L vs H_R). This is useful operator language, not a standalone admission proof.

## Connection to Pauli Algebra
The Pauli matrices sigma_x, sigma_y, sigma_z are isomorphic to the grade-1 generators e_1, e_2, e_3 of Cl(3,0). The bivectors correspond to the Lie algebra su(2). The pseudoscalar corresponds to the chirality operator.

## System Relevance
The source mapping reads the system's operators (Ti/Te/Fi/Fe) through Cl(3,0)-style structure. `su(2) x u(1)` and the chirality split (H_L = +H_0, H_R = -H_0) should be treated here as interpretive/operator mappings until a current repo receipt establishes the exact carrier, generator set, and admissibility status. ## Legacy Perspective (Dark Empress)

From Chapter 10: The Dark Empress claims the imaginary unit i is more fundamental than 1. The derivation chain: "Indeterminacy-> probability-> imaginary numbers-> negative numbers->positive numbers." Since i^1=i, i^2=-1, i^3=-i, i^4=1, "Complex imaginary numbers are more fundamental than real numbers."

The key insight: "'1' can't be the first number. You can't make -1 from 1. You can make 1 from -1. -1^2=1. Thus -1 is more primary than 1." Taking this further, "the square root of -1 is the most primary." The book also highlights Euler's formula e^(pi*i) as foundational: it "seems to cover most any possible number in existence, and has interesting qualities as a number that can explain the patterns in our universe, and how all of math comes into being."

The book explicitly credits Euler's identity (e^(pi*i) + 1 = 0) for enabling the conversion between grid and spherical coordinates that underpins its physics. See [[hopf-fibration-mathematics]] for the 0D origin and [[quantum-geometry-fubini-study]] for dimensional evolution.

## Cl(3,0) = M_2(C) Isomorphism
e_1 <-> sigma_x, e_2 <-> sigma_y, e_3 <-> sigma_z. Bivectors: e_{12}<->i sigma_z, e_{23}<->i sigma_x, e_{13}<->-i sigma_y. Pseudoscalar e_{123}<->i I_2. The even subalgebra Cl^+(3,0) = {1, e_{12}, e_{13}, e_{23}} is isomorphic to the quaternions H as a real associative algebra; the pure bivector / imaginary-quaternion subspace is su(2) under commutator. Rotors R=exp(-theta B/2) for unit bivector B give SU(2) matrices: R <-> exp(-i theta n.sigma/2). R and -R give the same rotation (double cover Spin(3)=SU(2) of SO(3)). (from clifford_algebra_qit.md)

## Cl(6,0) for Two Qubits and Twisted Embedding
Literature/source math, not repo admission:
Cl(6,0) with e_1,e_2,e_3 for system A and e_4,e_5,e_6 for system B. The 15 bivectors decompose: 3 local A, 3 local B, 9 cross-bivectors encoding correlations/entanglement. Critical subtlety: the geometric product in Cl(6) has ALL generators anticommuting, but tensor product operators on different subsystems COMMUTE. Fix: twisted embedding Sigma_4 = I_A e_4 = e_{1234}, etc., so [Sigma_i, Sigma_{j+3}]=0, recovering tensor product commutation. (from clifford_algebra_qit.md)

## Spin Groups and Accidental Isomorphisms
Spin(2)=U(1), Spin(3)=SU(2), Spin(4)=SU(2)xSU(2), Spin(5)=Sp(2), Spin(6)=SU(4). Spin(6)=SU(4) connects the geometry of R^6 to 2-qubit gates. This is the LAST case where the full gate group has a simple Clifford algebra description via accidental isomorphism. For 3+ qubits, the structure is fundamentally more complex. (from clifford_algebra-qit.md)

## Entanglement in the Clifford Framework
The entanglement bivector E = sum T_{ij} Sigma_i Sigma_{j+3} has maximum "area" for Bell states (T orthogonal). Concurrence C = |<Psi|sigma_y tensor sigma_y|Psi*>| measures the component in the maximally entangled subspace. SLOCC classes correspond to Spin(6,C) orbits on the spinor representation. (from clifford_algebra_qit.md)
