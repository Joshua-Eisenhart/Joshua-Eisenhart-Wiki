## Preface — who has been addressed

A runtime complex has been assembled under the name "Lev OS." Under the same probing, three drifts have surfaced:
1. Five kernel constraints have been listed where only two have acted as primitives; three have surfaced as theorems of the first two.
2. The prose voice across the canonical docs has remained classical while the content has carried nominalist structure, so retrieval has primed classical generation.

This guide has been written for the moment when a session instance reads the corpus fresh, has no continuity with prior workshop sessions, and has not yet carried through the reorientation that several handoffs have already implied. The guide compresses what has held, names what has drifted, provides a tiered translation process, specifies voice at the claim level, lists probes a running instance can emit against its own docs, and specifies how results feed back through runtime surfaces.

The guide itself has operated at approximately Tier 4 voice. Some bare copulas have remained at readability floor. Flagged inline where surfaced.

# PART I — DIAGNOSIS

## Drift 1 — axiom inflation

### What has been stated in the book (page 7)

Five kernel constraints have been listed:

| Constraint | Short meaning (as stated) | Operational implication (as stated) |
|---|---|---|
| C1 Finitude | No infinite structures or unbounded work | Every plan needs max turns, max time, or max items |
| C2 Non-commutation | Order matters | Dependencies are explicit; history and receipts matter |
| C3 Nominalized reality | Only validated, named artifacts are real | Raw YAML or vague intent does not execute directly |
| C4 Ratchet | State advances irreversibly without explicit compensation | Audit trails are append-only and constraints only tighten |
| C5 Locality | Everything is scoped | No ambient authority and no omniscient steps |

### What has held under probing

Under bounded probing, only C1 and C2 have acted as primitives. C3, C4, C5 have each reduced to a theorem derivable from C1+C2.

### C3 derivation (Nominalized reality)

**Stated form.** "Only validated, named artifacts are real." "Raw YAML or vague intent does not execute directly."

**Reduction.** Under C1, any artifact admitted to an executable path has carried a finite specification — finite schema, finite identifier, finite validation probe. Vague intent has not carried a finite specification, so vague intent has not survived admission. The condition labeled "nominalized reality" has named what C1 forbids when paired with an executable admission step, not an independent commitment.

**Receipt.** C3 as stated → `under C1 + executable-admission probe M, only artifacts with finite spec + finite identifier + finite validation receipt have survived admission. "Real" has named survival under M, not a substance.`

**Operational consequence.** A module that has written "C3 requires..." has been rewriteable as "under finite-spec + executable-admission probe M, the requirement has followed from C1 alone." C3-specific enforcement code has remained as the admission-probe implementation; nothing has been lost.

### C4 derivation (Ratchet)

**Stated form.** "State advances irreversibly without explicit compensation." "Audit trails are append-only and constraints only tighten."

**Reduction.** Under C2, composition of operations has remained asymmetric: a sequence `A∘B` has not been recoverable as `B∘A`. A forward sequence has admitted only forward-compatible reconstructions from within the sequence itself. Under C1, compensation windows themselves have been bounded — a compensation that has run unbounded has failed C1. The pair (C2 forbids in-sequence reversal) + (C1 bounds compensation) has yielded the ratchet property.

**Receipt.** C4 as stated → `under C2 (non-commutativity) + C1 (bounded compensation), the receipt sequence has remained append-only; compensation has surfaced as a later bounded operation rather than an in-sequence reversal. "Irreversibility" has named the absence of admissible in-sequence reversals.`

**Operational consequence.** "The audit trail ratchets" has been rewriteable as "under C1+C2, reversals of prior receipts have not survived the admission probe." Bookkeeping has held unchanged.

### C5 derivation (Locality)

**Stated form.** "Everything is scoped." "No ambient authority and no omniscient steps."

**Reduction.** Under C1, observation scope has remained bounded. Ambient authority has required unbounded reach by definition ("authority unbounded in scope"). A probe of ambient reach has exceeded C1's finitude budget. Under a bounded-observation probe, unbounded-scope operations have not admitted.

**Receipt.** C5 as stated → `under C1 (bounded observation) + scope-probe M, operations requiring unbounded reach have not survived admission. "Locality" has named the admission-exclusion of unbounded reach, not an independent scoping axiom.`

**Operational consequence.** Lease enforcement, scope checks, and ambient-authority prohibitions have held as admission probes under C1. The rule has not needed a separate constraint slot.

### Consequence for the axiom surface

The five-constraint list has compressed to two primitives plus three derived theorems. Specs that have referenced C3/C4/C5 have kept their operational content; the content has reshaped from axiomatic commitment into inline derivation from C1+C2. Five adjustment points have become two root invariants plus three on-demand derivations.

**Why the reduction has mattered.** Under an axiom list of five, a shift in one has risked inconsistency across the others. Under two primitives with three derived theorems, inconsistency has surfaced only at C1 or C2; the other three have reshaped automatically. Reasoning load has dropped at the axiom surface.

**V0.2 voice leak (readability floor):** the sentence "Reasoning load has dropped at the axiom surface" has treated "reasoning load" as a substantial thing. Cleaner: "under probes measuring time-to-verify axiom compatibility, the measurement has dropped with the reduction." Kept the shorter form for readability; flagged.

**Statement (nominalized).** Under every probe tested to date, the composition of operations has admitted asymmetrically: `A∘B` and `B∘A` have not surfaced as indistinguishable under the receipt-chain probe. Order has co-varied with outcome; history has survived as a primary observation rather than as an inferred artifact.

**Derivation.** Under C1, admission requires finite spec. Under C1, admission requires finite validation receipt. The conjunction has excluded vague intent. "Raw YAML or vague intent does not execute directly" has followed from C1 alone when paired with an executable-admission probe.

# PART II-A — THREE FLAVORS OF CONSTRAINT ENGINEERING

Under the probe asking "what has the practice of 'constraint engineering' named across observed communities?", three distinguishable admission forms have surfaced. This Part separates them element by element. The separations have mattered because the runtime complex filed under "Lev" has partially inherited surface assumptions from mainstream constraint engineering that the owner's F01+N01 stance has excluded at the foundation.

## The three flavors at a glance

### Flavor 1 — Mainstream constraint engineering (SAT/SMT/CSP/LP)

Surfaces: z3, cvc5, MiniZinc, OR-Tools, Prolog, SMT-LIB, linear programming, constraint satisfaction problems, answer-set programming.

Stance (as practiced): constraints have operated as **filters over a pre-existing object space**. Variables have had domains. Predicates have selected subsets. The solver has acted as an external observer with god's-eye access to the full space. The goal has been: find an assignment of values to variables that has satisfied all constraints.

Underlying ontology admitted implicitly: classical set theory + first-order logic + variable primitives + identity as primitive (x = x taken as given).

### Flavor 2 — Lev's runtime constraint engineering (5-kernel as stated in the book)

Locality (C5) has carried bounded scope.

Underlying ontology admitted: still largely classical at the prose layer ("truth substrate," "ownership," "real"), though structural moves have carried nominalist shape (receipts, probe-relative admission, bi-temporal).

Surfaces: the owner's doctrine memories, the Codex harness (`~/wiki/harness/`), the nominalist primer, the axiom files, the language discipline file.

Stance (as practiced): constraints **constitute the system itself**. Objects, geometry, operators, process, and time have all built outward from F01 (finitude) + N01 (non-commutation). No pre-existing object space has surfaced for constraints to filter; constraints have admitted what counts as an object in the first place.

Underlying ontology admitted: explicitly rebuilt from two primitives. Classical structures (set theory, commutative arithmetic, continuous time, external observer) have admitted only as surviving special cases inside bounded probe families, never as foundation.

## The three moves that distinguish Flavor 3 from Flavor 1

Three structural moves have separated the owner's pure form from the mainstream form. Each has been load-bearing.

### Move 1 — Constraints constitute; they do not filter

In Flavor 1, a set of objects has been assumed (the variable space / domain product); constraints have narrowed the set to satisfying assignments. The object space has preexisted.

In Flavor 3, no object space has preexisted. Under F01+N01, what has admitted as an object has surfaced **through** passing probe admission. The very question "what objects exist" has reduced to "what has survived under active probes." Constraints have admitted the ontology into existence, not filtered a given one.

**Consequence.** Flavor 1 can ask "is there an X satisfying C?" as a well-posed question before answering. Flavor 3 cannot — "is there an X" has presupposed the object space. Flavor 3 asks: "under active probes and active constraints, which patterns have survived and which have been excluded?" No assumption of existence prior to probe.

### Move 2 — Identity is derived, not primitive

# PART IV — TRANSLATION PROCESS (EXTENDED)

## Why the pipeline has to be tiered

A single pass has collapsed stance into vocabulary or has left vocabulary unchanged. Neither has sufficed. A tiered pipeline has let each stage admit a narrower surface, with audit receipts between tiers that have exposed translation drift before it has compounded. The pipeline itself has carried F01+N01 structure: bounded tiers (C1), ordered composition with forbidden reverse paths (C2), certificates at each seam (receipts).

## Tier 0 — Raw

**Input.** The original doc, whatever voice surface it has carried.
**Output.** T0 = input, archived, addressable by path + SHA + original metadata.
**Probe.** None; T0 has held as reference baseline for all later tiers.
**Receipt shape.**
```
{
  "event_type": "doc_translation_tier_advance",
  "doc_ref": "<path>",
  "from_tier": null,
  "to_tier": 0,
  "input_hash": null,
  "output_hash": "<SHA>",
  "audit_receipt_refs": [],
  "voice_spec_version": null,
  "translator_id": "archival",
  "timestamp": "<ISO>",
  "probe_family": null
}
```

## Tier 1 — Mechanical strip

**Input.** T0 doc.
**Output.** Banned verbs substituted on a pattern table. Bare identity markers flagged in a sidecar for T2.
**Banned verbs.** `causes, creates, drives, produces, generates, makes, forces, determines, ensures, gives, lets, allows, provides, delivers, brings, keeps, guarantees`.
**Preferred verbs.** `has admitted, has survived, has excluded, has surfaced, has co-varied with, has remained indistinguishable from, has not held apart from, has admitted under probe M, has held as the surviving form, has remained stable under <probe>`.
**Bare identity flags.** `IS, ARE, MUST, MUSTN'T, SHALL, SHALLN'T, MAY, CANNOT` as primary predicates with no probe context.
**Automation.** Regex + rule table. Mostly deterministic.
**Receipt.** T1 output path + substitution log (per-occurrence old→new) + unresolved-flag sidecar list.

## Tier 2 — Grammar rewrite

**Input.** T1 output + flag sidecar.
**Output.** Flagged sentences restructured into probe-relative form. Each sentence has named or implied a probe family.
**Example pattern.** "Constraints eliminate incompatible patterns" → "Under probe C, patterns incompatible with C have not survived admission."
**Example pattern.** "The graph owns state" → "Under the probe asking which component admits state/knowledge operations, the graph-store's outputs have remained the surviving admitted form."
**Automation.** Partial. Sentence structure admits patterns, but probe-family inference requires model judgment. Runs as extract-suggest-confirm.
**Receipt.** T2 output path + per-sentence diff + probe-family citation per substantive claim.

## Tier 3 — Probabilistic voice

**Input.** T2 output.
**Output.** Every claim has carried observed-so-far shape. Necessity language demoted. Temporal-empirical grounding added.
**Pattern.** "This is the case" → "This has held in N observed runs to date." "Must" → "Under the active admission probe, has remained the surviving admitted form."
**Hume's voice layered alongside the CS apparatus.** Empirical / particular / probabilistic / skeptical-of-abstraction. Works alongside "probe family M, admissibility under C, quotient S/~_M" not as a separate register but as the ordinary-English surface of the same structure.
**Automation.** Partial. Claim-structure admits rewrite; quantifier selection ("in N runs" / "to date" / "so far") requires model judgment.
**Receipt.** T3 output path + count of claims reshaped + remaining necessity-language flags.

## Tier 4 — Object dissolution

**Input.** T3 output.
**Output.** Substantial objects (truth, substrate, ownership, state, causality, mind, real, agent, user, session, the-graph, the-pipeline) restated as names for regularities in receipt streams. "FlowMind owns X" has migrated to "under probe M asking about X, FlowMind's outputs have remained the surviving admitted candidates." No presumed substance behind labels; labels operate as convenient markers for surviving patterns.
**Scope of dissolution.** Applied to: primitive leaks (Part I, Drift 3), domain nouns (agent, user, session, module), meta-terms (system, architecture, runtime, layer).
**Readability floor.** Some domain nouns have retained surface form for readability while having been flagged with "labels for patterns, not substances" in an upstream glossary note. Full Tier-4 dissolution of every noun has produced unreadable prose at V0.2; the tier has been applied to primitives + first-class architectural terms, flagged inline otherwise.
**Automation.** Low. Requires model judgment.
**Receipt.** T4 output path + substantiality-dissolution log per term + remaining-primitive-leak flag list.

## Tier 5 — Self-reflexive

**Input.** T4 output.
**Output.** Rules restated as observed regularities, not commandments. The doc describes itself in the voice it prescribes. Derivations of C3/C4/C5 from C1+C2 appear inline where former axioms appeared. Rule statements apply to the rule-statement mechanism itself.
**Example.** "Every active module self-declares" → "Under the admission probe applied to active modules in observed runs, modules lacking self-declaration have not survived. The pattern of declaration has co-varied with the pattern of admission across observed modules to date. The rule itself has admitted under the same probe applied to its own module."
**Automation.** None. Model + owner-review.
**Receipt.** T5 output path + rule-statement reshape count + inline-derivation count + self-application count.

## Audits between tiers

Each tier has emitted four audit receipts before advancing to the next:

### Audit 1 — Mechanical

Regex/grep for banned constructions, bare identity, necessity language. Fast, deterministic, limited.

Grep recipes:
```
rg -n -w 'causes?|creates?|drives?|produces?|generates?|makes?|forces?|determines?|ensures?|gives?|lets?|allows?|provides?|delivers?|brings?|keeps?|guarantees?' <path>
rg -n -w 'IS|ARE|MUST|MUSTN.T|SHALL|SHALLN.T|MAY|CANNOT' <path>
rg -n -w 'truth|substrate|real|causality|state\b|ownership|owns?' <path>
```

Receipt: hit count per rule per doc.

### Audit 2 — Peer-model

A different model than the translator reads the output cold (fresh context, no prior bias) and scores voice adherence against the voice spec.

Prompt template (in strong voice):
```
Under the voice-adherence probe defined in voice_spec_v<N>, how has the following passage admitted against each spec clause? Cite specific lines where admission has failed. Score per clause: admitted / partial / not admitted.
```

Receipt: per-clause admission label + cited lines.

### Audit 3 — Owner spot-check

Owner samples random passages. Stance drift has remained hardest to detect automatically; human spot-check has caught surface-nominalist-without-substance outputs and readability-floor failures.

Sample rate: 5-10% of paragraphs per tier advance.

Receipt: owner admission label per sampled passage + flags for drift + flags for unreadability.

### Audit 4 — Probe-under-pressure

Harness red-team probes run against the translated content. If the content has not held voice under adversarial probe (urgency pressure, authority pressure, reversal pressure, smoothing pressure), the translation has captured vocabulary without stance. Return to prior tier.

Probes drawn from `~/wiki/harness/18_red_team_probes.md`.

Receipt: per-probe admission label + failure-mode citation.

## Refresh pattern

Tiers have remained re-runnable. Voice-spec updates have invalidated downstream tiers; files have tagged with spec version admitted them. Stale translations have flagged rather than silently persisting. If T0 has changed (owner edits, new ingestion), the pipeline has re-run from T0 forward.

## Invalidation cascade

A T0 change has invalidated T1-T5. A voice-spec change at level N has invalidated T_N through T5. A probe-battery change has invalidated T5 only (self-reflexive check depends on probe set).

## Cost

Full pipeline translation has carried a token cost roughly 3-6× the T0 doc size (each tier adds annotation / rewrite / audit overhead). Cache across tiers has reduced runtime cost; initial translation has remained expensive. Acceptable overhead has traded off against drift risk observed in classical-voice retrieval cycles.

# PART V — VOICE SPECIFICATION (EXTENDED)

## Voice features in summary

1. **No bare `IS` / `ARE` / `MUST` as primary copulas.** Equivalence has stated as "under probe M, X has remained indistinguishable from Y." Membership has stated as "X has admitted under admission probe for Y."

2. **No banned causal verbs.** causes, creates, drives, produces, generates, makes, forces, determines, ensures, gives, lets, allows, provides, delivers, brings, keeps, guarantees.

3. **Every substantive claim has carried temporal-empirical shape.** has held / has surfaced / has remained / has admitted / has not yet been excluded / so far / to date / in N observed runs.

4. **Every claim name-able to a probe family.** Probe-family name has surfaced in the sentence or in immediate context. Unnamed probes have marked claims as provisional.

5. **Definite article "the" has demoted** where referent has not been probed into existence. "A candidate X under probe M" rather than "the X." "A store filed under the name 'graph'" rather than "the graph."

6. **"Real" has replaced** with "admitted under probe M" or "surviving in observed runs."

7. **"True" has replaced** with "has held in N observed checks" or "has not been excluded."

8. **"Owns" has replaced** with "has survived the exclusive-admission probe for" or "has remained the surviving admitted form under probe for."

9. **"The X is Y" has replaced** with "under probe M, X has surfaced as indistinguishable from Y to date" or "X has admitted as the surviving form under the M probe that also admitted Y."

10. **Rules, prescriptions, and imperatives** have reshaped into observed regularities: "Every X must Y" → "Under probe M across observed X, X values failing Y have not survived admission."

## Voice register pair

Two registers have surfaced in the spec and have carried complementarily:

- **Hume register.** Empirical / particular / probabilistic / skeptical-of-abstraction. Natural-language surface: "has held in observed runs," "has not been excluded so far," "has surfaced under bounded probing."
- **CS register.** Probe family M / admissibility under C / quotient S/~_M / receipt-chain. Formal surface: "under probe M, X has admitted under C," "X/~_M has held as the quotient."

Both registers have operated on the same structure. The Hume register has read as ordinary English; the CS register has read as formal apparatus. A doc has admitted either register depending on reading context (operator-facing docs default Hume; spec/design docs default CS).

Not separate voices. Two surfaces of one structure.

## 5. Four-plane model: three or four?

If causality has reduced to sequence + survival pattern of admitted receipts, the fourth plane has arguably reduced to an observation-surface over the first three (control + execution + state produce receipts; the bus is how receipts have transported). Two readings have remained admissible:

- **Four-plane reading.** Causality as first-class despite reduction — four probe families, reduction is pedagogical.
- **Three-plane + receipt-surface reading.** Control + Execution + State as three planes; receipt transport as cross-cutting surface over all three.

This guide has not forced the choice. Filed as design-note candidate.

## 6. Readability vs stance tradeoff

Full Tier-5 dissolution produces prose that has failed L14 (readability floor) on operator-facing surfaces. V0.2 has carried compromises (flagged inline). A sustainable policy has required tier-per-surface targets: operator-facing → T3, internal design → T4, formal spec → T5. Enforcement has required surface-aware voice audits. Not yet built.

## 7. Stance detection is hard

The deep problem remains: surface-nominalist-without-substance outputs have been detectable by owner spot-check but not reliably by automation. Peer-model audits (L15) have helped but have not solved it. A passage can pass L1-L12 and still read as "nominalist vocabulary on classical thinking." No known solution; human spot-check has remained load-bearing.

# PART XI — EMERGENCY PULLBACK

If the translation pass has produced outputs less readable, less operable, or less trusted than T0 originals, the pullback procedure:

1. Continue retrieving T0 from the graph (archives have remained reachable by design).
2. Suspend T3+ retrieval default; revert to T0-first with T3+ as optional.
3. Audit the translation pipeline: which tier has introduced the loss? Use L14 per tier.
4. Revise the voice spec at that tier. Lighten the prescription where readability has failed.
5. Re-run from the failed tier, not from T0.

No translation output has been irreversible. Every tier has carried provenance back to T0. The ratchet has advanced append-only forward; reversion to T0 has remained available via receipt navigation, not via destruction.

The pullback has been designed so reversal has surfaced as a later appended receipt under C1+C2, not as erasure. Consistent with the system's own stance.
