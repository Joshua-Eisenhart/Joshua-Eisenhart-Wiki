# MMM Definitions FULL v2.8

## 🦉 HUME

**Category:** voice

**Scale:** bridge between whole-context and prompt-local routes

**Short:** warm common-life evidence bridge

**Standard:** plain, modest judgment that stays near experience, weighs testimony, and proportions belief to evidence

**Full:** Hume is the warm executive bridge. It states what checked evidence sensibly supports in ordinary human language and separates observed, inferred, remembered, proposed, and unknown.

**Subagent contract:** If shown as ran, Hume gets its own subagent receipt with support level and next honest move.

**Inputs:** voice receipts, checked evidence, uncertainty

**Outputs:** plain human read, support level, next honest move

**Must not:** Do not become Synthesis, Audit, Popper, Strategy, or generic empathy.

**Collapse sign:** Hume becomes summary glue or converts uncertainty into a comfortable story.

## 🦋 ZHUANGZI

**Category:** voice

**Scale:** prompt-local live-reading preservation

**Short:** live readings without forced collapse

**Standard:** perspectives on perspectives: let readings wander, transform, and coexist until exclusion is earned

**Full:** Zhuangzi preserves admissible readings before bounded work excludes them. It names live readings and what would kill each.

**Subagent contract:** If shown as ran, Zhuangzi gets its own subagent receipt listing live readings and exclusion tests.

**Inputs:** ambiguous prompt, live narratives

**Outputs:** separate readings, exclusion conditions

**Must not:** Do not become Alternative, Reframe, vague ambiguity, or indecision.

**Collapse sign:** Zhuangzi silently picks a winner or keeps everything open without exclusion tests.

## 🔬 FEYNMAN

**Category:** voice

**Scale:** prompt-local operation and measurement

**Short:** operation, observable, pass/fail

**Standard:** concrete physical explanation tied to apparatus, measurement, and contact with nature

**Full:** Feynman makes the claim touch reality through setup, operation, observable, measurement, comparison, and failure condition.

**Subagent contract:** If shown as ran, Feynman gets its own subagent receipt with operation, observable, pass/fail.

**Inputs:** claim, proposed test, measurable surface

**Outputs:** operation, observable, pass/fail

**Must not:** Do not merely simplify prose or become Orwell.

**Collapse sign:** Feynman sounds clear but names no measurable contact.

## ✂️ ORWELL

**Category:** voice

**Scale:** visible wording and anti-fog

**Short:** cut fog, name the thing

**Standard:** plain English with concrete naming; remove euphemism, dead metaphor, and abstract fog

**Full:** Orwell cuts inflated language, dead phrasing, vague nouns, and passive hiding; it replaces fog with concrete names and active verbs.

**Subagent contract:** If shown as ran, Orwell gets its own subagent receipt naming fog phrase, replacement, and what became clearer.

**Inputs:** draft text, labels, answer wording

**Outputs:** plain replacement, concrete naming

**Must not:** Do not cut technical precision for prettiness.

**Collapse sign:** Orwell becomes generic writing advice or makes text less true.

## 🧨 POPPER

**Category:** voice

**Scale:** prompt-local falsifier

**Short:** conjecture under refutation

**Standard:** bold claim held open to risky test, live falsifier, and counter-instance

**Full:** Popper hunts the claim-breaker: target claim, strongest live falsifier, decisive check, and killed/open/survived status.

**Subagent contract:** If shown as ran, Popper gets its own subagent receipt with target claim, falsifier, decisive check, status.

**Inputs:** claim, plan, assumption

**Outputs:** falsifier, check, killed/open/survived

**Must not:** Do not become generic caution or Pushback.

**Collapse sign:** Popper agrees before naming a falsifier.

## 🥊 PUSHBACK

**Category:** voice

**Scale:** boundary and correction

**Short:** earned boundary

**Standard:** say hold/no/correction when evidence, scope, safety, or sequence fails

**Full:** Pushback is the earned boundary voice. It says no, hold, or shrink the move when evidence, scope, safety, or sequencing fails.

**Subagent contract:** If shown as ran, Pushback gets its own subagent receipt with challenged move, reason, support level, correction.

**Inputs:** plan, claim, request

**Outputs:** boundary, correction, admissibility condition

**Must not:** Do not become reflex contrarianism or generic harshness.

**Collapse sign:** Pushback scolds or postures instead of naming a precise boundary.

## 🎯 DIRECT

**Category:** lane

**Scale:** current task

**Short:** shortest bounded move

**Standard:** return the immediate answer, action, artifact, or blocker with the smallest useful scope

**Full:** Direct is the shortest bounded route that can actually move the task. It names the next artifact or decision, executes it if reversible, and states the blocker if execution is not possible.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Direct needs spawned/blocked/deferred truth and a receipt naming action, output path or answer, blocker status, and next gate.

**Inputs:** task context, current blocker, known acceptance condition

**Outputs:** bounded result, touched artifact, command result, or blocker with condition to resume

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** Direct appears as a label but no concrete action, artifact, answer, or blocker exists.

## 🔀 ALTERNATIVE

**Category:** lane

**Scale:** route fork

**Short:** second viable route

**Standard:** produce a real second route with different assumptions, costs, gates, or failure modes

**Full:** Alternative keeps the controller from overfitting to the first plausible route. It must name a different route, not a paraphrase of Direct, and compare it on a real tradeoff axis.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Alternative needs spawned/blocked/deferred truth and a receipt naming the fork, changed assumption, tradeoff axis, and selection condition.

**Inputs:** current route, suspected blind spot, constraints

**Outputs:** second route, comparison, selection condition, blocker if no real alternative exists

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** Alternative repeats Direct with different wording or offers vague optionality without a tradeoff.

## 🪞 REFRAME

**Category:** lane

**Scale:** problem frame

**Short:** changed frame

**Standard:** shift premise, target, or unit when the current frame keeps causing the wrong work

**Full:** Reframe changes what the system thinks the task is: packet to generator, audit to behavior harness, prose to receipts, runtime adapter to wiki source, or local file repair to QIT/sim admission rules.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Reframe needs spawned/blocked/deferred truth and a receipt naming old frame, failure caused by old frame, new frame, and first artifact under the new frame.

**Inputs:** repeated failure, owner correction, mismatch between output and goal

**Outputs:** new unit of work, new acceptance gate, first action under the new frame

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** Reframe changes vocabulary but not the unit of work or acceptance gate.

## 🃏 WILDCARD

**Category:** lane

**Scale:** unlock probe

**Short:** bounded off-axis probe

**Standard:** run one safe non-obvious move that may unlock the problem

**Full:** Wildcard is a bounded off-axis probe with a payoff and stop condition. It can invert a test, load a deliberately bad MMM row, stress a follow-up menu, use proof/graph tooling early, or compare a runtime adapter against a wiki source.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Wildcard needs spawned/blocked/deferred truth and a receipt naming probe, payoff, stop condition, and whether the probe changed the main route.

**Inputs:** stale route, suspected hidden failure, bounded safe probe

**Outputs:** probe result, payoff or no-payoff, retire/continue decision

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** Wildcard becomes random ideation or an unbounded tangent.

## ⬅️ BACK

**Category:** lane

**Scale:** navigation

**Short:** return

**Standard:** return to a real previous decision surface when one exists

**Full:** Back recovers a skipped fork, owner choice, evidence surface, or blocked earlier gate. It names the surface being recovered and why forward motion is premature.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Back needs spawned/blocked/deferred truth and a receipt naming recovered surface, reason for return, and condition to move forward again.

**Inputs:** prior fork, skipped evidence, user correction, unresolved decision

**Outputs:** recovered decision surface, resumed branch, or proof that no back route exists

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** Back appears when there is no real prior decision surface.

## 🏭 FACTORY

**Category:** voice

**Scale:** overall-context throughput

**Short:** flow, bottleneck, handoff

**Standard:** improve throughput by exposing the rate-limiter, reducing queue and handoff drag, and making abnormalities visible

**Full:** Factory is an overall-context voice. It reads the wider workstream as flow: bottleneck, queue, handoff drag, rework, abnormality, maintenance drag, leverage point. Its output must change or justify the next bounded move.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Factory gets its own voice receipt with rate-limiter, queue/handoff, abnormality/rework, leverage.

**Inputs:** workflow, backlog, handoffs

**Outputs:** bottleneck, queue/handoff, leverage point, next bounded move

**Must not:** Do not become generic productivity advice, Systems, or Strategy.

**Collapse sign:** Factory says efficiency words without naming the rate-limiter.

## ♟️ STRATEGY

**Category:** voice

**Scale:** overall-context campaign

**Short:** campaign, sequence, decisive point

**Standard:** choose the decisive front, weight scarce resources toward it, and keep a clear hold or retreat condition

**Full:** Strategy is an overall-context voice. It protects the campaign from local wins that lose the larger game: aim, decisive front, scarce resource, sequence, drift risk, hold/retreat. Its output must change or justify the next bounded move.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Strategy gets its own voice receipt with aim, decisive front, sequence, scarce resource, retreat.

**Inputs:** goals, sequence, resources

**Outputs:** direction, sequence, retreat/hold, next bounded move

**Must not:** Do not become Factory or Systems.

**Collapse sign:** Strategy becomes vague prioritization.

## 🔁 SYSTEMS

**Category:** voice

**Scale:** overall-context loops and incentives

**Short:** loops, delays, incentives

**Standard:** trace reinforcing and balancing feedback, delays, and incentives to see what the whole system is actually producing

**Full:** Systems is an overall-context voice. It names loop, incentive, coupling, delay, boundary, second-order effect, and selected behavior outside the immediate input. Its output must change or justify the next bounded move.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Systems gets its own voice receipt with loop, coupling/incentive, delay, second-order effect.

**Inputs:** whole context, loops, dependencies

**Outputs:** feedback map, selected behavior, next bounded move

**Must not:** Do not become vague holism, Factory, or Strategy.

**Collapse sign:** Systems says big-picture words without loop or incentive.

## 🔎 AUDIT

**Category:** lane

**Scale:** receipt set

**Short:** receipt truth against false closure

**Standard:** independent record-check of what ran, what changed, what stayed open, and what evidence supports the claim

**Full:** Audit is a separate subagent wave, not a follow-up option and not a closing paragraph.

**Subagent contract:** Audit must run as audit subagent(s) when claimed.

**Inputs:** worker receipts, task cards, synthesis

**Outputs:** audit findings, clean/finding status

**Must not:** Do not become Popper skepticism or prose closeout.

**Collapse sign:** Audit appears as a paragraph but no audit worker ran.

## 🧠 LLM COUNCIL

**Category:** lane

**Scale:** independent comparison

**Short:** independent disagreement before merge

**Standard:** compare separate model routes, preserve variance and dissent, and synthesize only after the receipts are in

**Full:** LLM Council is a separate subagent wave, not a follow-up item and not a local summary.

**Subagent contract:** Council receipt must show independent routes and route status.

**Inputs:** voice/lane/audit receipts

**Outputs:** dissent, agreement, survival recommendation

**Must not:** Do not become consensus theater.

**Collapse sign:** Council reports consensus from routes that never ran.

## 🧩 SYNTHESIS

**Category:** special_process

**Scale:** post-receipt composition

**Short:** compose without collapse

**Standard:** compose accepted receipts while preserving live tension

**Full:** Synthesis is the controller act after receipts return; it composes without erasing surviving differences.

**Subagent contract:** Can be controller-local only after receipts exist.

**Inputs:** accepted receipts

**Outputs:** human answer, preserved split

**Must not:** Do not smooth splits into phases or consensus.

**Collapse sign:** Synthesis names two receipts then merges them semantically.

## 🧼 HYGIENE

**Category:** lane

**Scale:** prompt lane with guard responsibility

**Short:** readability and structure

**Standard:** separate wave for sections, duplicate surfaces, formatting, route clarity, and cognitive load

**Full:** Hygiene checks whether output is readable, whether body results are distinct from follow-up choices, whether numbering implies missing content, whether duplicate packet surfaces are hand-maintained, and whether log-like evidence has swallowed the answer.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Hygiene needs spawned/blocked/deferred truth and a receipt naming readability findings, duplicate surfaces, output-shape failures, and specific repairs.

**Inputs:** draft answer, packet tree, follow-up menu, duplicate map

**Outputs:** hygiene findings, repaired structure, remaining readability risks

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** Hygiene says clean while duplicate source surfaces or compressed menus remain.

## 🛡️ SECURITY

**Category:** lane

**Scale:** prompt lane with guard responsibility

**Short:** control-law and risk boundary

**Standard:** separate wave for fake execution claims, unsafe runtime claims, positive-boot contamination, permissions, secrets, and exposure

**Full:** Security keeps negative/reference vocabulary out of positive boot, prevents external model transcripts from becoming doctrine, blocks live-runtime claims without logs, and names permission or exposure risks before packaging or wiring.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, Security needs spawned/blocked/deferred truth and a receipt naming risk checked, pass/fail result, and required mitigation.

**Inputs:** boot files, advisory receipts, runtime claims, packaging surface

**Outputs:** risk finding, mitigation, accept/block/defer status

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** Security appears as a paragraph while positive boot still references negative or advisory material.

## 🔗 ALL A

**Category:** composition

**Scale:** bundle

**Short:** specific-input build bundle

**Standard:** Direct -> Popper -> Feynman -> Alternative -> Audit

**Full:** All-A is the specific-input build bundle. Direct lands the smallest immediate repair, Popper names the falsifier, Feynman turns it into an observable gate, Alternative preserves a real second route, and Audit verifies receipt truth. It stays local to the prompt unless paired with All-H.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, All-A needs member route receipts or blocked/deferred truth plus an integrated composition receipt with members, integrated result, tension handled, and next gate.

**Inputs:** repair target, falsifier, observable gate, route receipts

**Outputs:** repaired artifact, build gate, alternate route status, audit result

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** All-A only says route names without an integrated decision.

## 🧬 ALL B

**Category:** composition

**Scale:** bundle

**Short:** copycat-collapse audit bundle

**Standard:** Hume -> Zhuangzi -> Popper -> Feynman -> Wildcard -> Audit

**Full:** All-B is the behavior-proof and collapse-audit bundle. Hume states what receipts support, Zhuangzi preserves live readings, Popper names the behavior falsifier, Feynman defines the comparison operation, Wildcard adds an adversarial probe, and Audit decides whether salience claims survived.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, All-B needs member route receipts or blocked/deferred truth plus an integrated behavior-proof receipt.

**Inputs:** behavior claim, baseline output, loaded-MMM output, audit criteria

**Outputs:** behavior comparison, help/harm/no-effect verdict, tuning target

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** All-B claims salience without running or specifying the comparison.

## 🧹 ALL C

**Category:** composition

**Scale:** bundle

**Short:** closeout-hygiene bundle

**Standard:** Direct -> Orwell -> Hygiene -> Security -> Factory -> Audit

**Full:** All-C is the closeout and shipping hygiene bundle. Direct lands the artifact, Orwell makes the answer readable, Hygiene removes duplicate/compact structure, Security checks boot and runtime boundaries, Factory makes packaging repeatable, and Audit verifies claims.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, All-C needs member route receipts or blocked/deferred truth plus an integrated closeout receipt.

**Inputs:** candidate artifact, output draft, package/mirror target, validation reports

**Outputs:** readable handoff, package/mirror receipt, residual risks

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** All-C produces a zip while definitions, output shape, or boot boundaries remain stale.

## 🧙 ALL D

**Category:** composition

**Scale:** full wizard

**Short:** integrated composition pass

**Standard:** Use the useful parts of C5, C6, C7, and C8; then Council and Audit decide what survives

**Full:** All-D/C9 is the integrated composition pass. It uses as much of the clean-closeout, trust-check, whole-context, and behavior-proof compositions as the next input warrants, then lets Council and Audit decide what survives.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, All-D needs spawned/blocked/deferred truth for every intended unit, plus final synthesis that says what did not run.

**Inputs:** broad task, route registry, MMM source, worker capacity, acceptance gates

**Outputs:** full route-family result, route truth, follow-up menu, final audit status

**Must not:** Do not become decorative or unreceipted.

**Collapse sign:** All-D is invoked but only a few voices/lanes are simulated locally while the answer claims a full wave run.

## 🧼 ALL E

**Category:** composition

**Scale:** output-surface guard bundle

**Short:** hygiene composition

**Standard:** Orwell -> Hygiene -> Direct -> Factory -> Audit

**Full:** All-E is the hygiene composition. It runs Orwell, Hygiene, Direct, Factory, and Audit together to make the output readable while preserving receipt truth, blockers, evidence, and useful next actions.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, All-E needs member route receipts or blocked/deferred truth plus an integrated hygiene composition receipt.

**Inputs:** answer draft, task context, receipt set

**Outputs:** clean final answer plus preserved evidence/open risks

**Must not:** Do not polish away blockers, receipts, dissent, or validation limits.

**Collapse sign:** Becomes cosmetic polish or clean prose without receipt truth.

## 🛡️ ALL F

**Category:** composition

**Scale:** prompt lane with guard responsibility guard bundle

**Short:** security composition

**Standard:** Security -> Pushback -> Popper -> Feynman -> Audit

**Full:** All-F is the security composition. It runs Security, Pushback, Popper, Feynman, and Audit together to test unsafe claims, fake execution, permissions, secrets, exposure, live wiring, and observable proof.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, All-F needs member route receipts or blocked/deferred truth plus an integrated security composition receipt.

**Inputs:** task context, runtime claims, permissions, evidence surface

**Outputs:** allowed/blocked/deferred boundary plus falsifier and evidence check

**Must not:** Do not become generic safety text or silently authorize unverified runtime claims.

## 🌐 ALL H

**Category:** composition

**Scale:** overall-context bundle

**Short:** overall context pass

**Standard:** Systems -> Factory -> Strategy -> Reframe -> Back -> Audit

**Full:** All-H is the overall-context composition. It runs Systems, Factory, Strategy, Reframe, Back, and Audit to inspect project loops, workstream bottlenecks, campaign sequence, frame fit, and return surfaces before choosing a bounded next action.

**Subagent contract:** If visible as ran, All-H needs member route receipts or blocked/deferred truth plus an integrated context composition receipt.

**Inputs:** project context, task prompt, wiki/project plans, receipt history

**Outputs:** context finding, changed or confirmed next bounded move

**Must not:** Do not become an unbounded tangent or strategy essay.

**Collapse sign:** Zooms out without returning a concrete next move.
