# Constraint-Admissibility Primer

This system runs on constraints, not construction. Constraints are not applied to a pre-existing system; they are the system. Each admitted layer is built outward from the active constraint set, so carriers, geometry, operators, and process are later survivors rather than primitive givens.

---

## How Constraints Work

**Constraints eliminate what cannot persist.** A constraint kills incompatible patterns. Example: "Indistinguishable states under all probes are the same." This eliminates over-distinguished states. What survives is compatible with all active constraints.

**Constraints do not act on a separate substrate.** Classical: "From axioms, these objects must follow." Constraint-based: "Only these objects are compatible; we do not know what survives unless tested." If a claimed object cannot be traced outward from the active constraints, it is smuggled structure rather than earned surface.

**Survivors are candidates, not truths.** When a geometry passes constraint tests, it is admissible given current evidence. It is not the "correct" geometry. Later constraints may exclude it.

---

## The Two Directions of Reasoning

**Forward evolution:** Given states and operators today, what states are reachable tomorrow? Prediction.

**Backward admissibility:** Given constraints, what objects could have persisted to now? History or inverse reasoning.

These are NOT the same. A forward sim might show "X leads to Y." A backward check might show "no admissible order led from X to Y." When they disagree, the backward result (impossibility) is more fundamental. If it cannot have come from anywhere, it cannot be.

---

## Feedback on Earlier Candidates

**Later organization feeds back on earlier candidates.** When shell 3 sims run, they discover constraints shell 1 must satisfy. This retroactively restricts shell 1 candidates. Not retrocausality—discovery of a constraint always present but unmeasurable until higher-shell sims ran.

---

## Test vs. Truth

**Passing a test ≠ being correct.** A candidate passing 10 tests has survived 10 checks. Future checks may exclude it.

Never write: "This is THE density matrix," "This is correct," "28/28 PASS."

Always write: "This survived constraints X, Y, Z," "F01 bounded ✓; N01 ordering ✓; locality survived under probe family M."

---

## Operational Stance

1. Know what constraints are active
2. Test what survives
3. Hold all survivors as candidates
4. Trust impossibility over possibility
5. Never collapse survivors until constraints eliminate all but one
