# S9 Transport - Standard Math

Scope: standard mathematical facts for connection/transport families. This is
corpus material, not an MMM head.

## Connections, Chern Class, And Holonomy

The ordinary one-form candidates use `A=dphi+f(eta)dchi`. Their curvature is
`F=dA=f'(eta)deta wedge dchi` in the pinned chart. The first Chern class records
normalized total curvature, but it does not determine the connection, curvature
density, holonomy spectrum, or annular flux vector.

Holonomy is the transport readout around loops. For abelian U(1) rows,
curvature integrals and Stokes-type boundary comparisons are strong local
tools once the loop, chart, and lift convention are fixed. For non-abelian or
matrix-valued transport, path ordering is load-bearing because loop factors may
not commute.

## Same-Chern, Different-Holonomy

Families can share `c1=1` while changing density or selected holonomies. A
small bump that vanishes at endpoints preserves total Chern class but changes
curvature distribution. A leaf-match family can match one or two holonomy
samples while changing off-anchor leaves or annular flux.
