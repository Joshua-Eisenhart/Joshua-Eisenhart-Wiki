# S9 Transport - Negatives

Negatives are first-class controls for the S9 discriminator.

## Kill Tests

- Equal `c1` does not imply equal connection or transport. It is only a
  topological co-survival row.
- A small endpoint-preserving bump is not alias. It is designed to keep total
  Chern class while changing curvature density and annular flux.
- One-leaf and two-leaf holonomy matches are not global equality. They must be
  killed or left open by expanded leaf spectra and off-anchor annuli.
- Curvature density perturbations cannot be hidden by topology language. If
  `F` changes, alias fails even when `c1` survives.
- Path ordering cannot be dropped for noncommuting transport. If loop factors
  do not commute, ordinary coefficient comparison is not the transport object.
  The designed check is `U_a U_b` versus `U_b U_a`, or equivalently the loop
  commutator gap against identity under the scoped convention.

## Designed-Fail Controls

The future battery should include same-Chern density bumps, single-leaf matches,
two-leaf matches, and a noncommuting loop pair. These controls keep topology,
local curvature, sampled holonomy, annular flux, and path-ordered transport
separate.
