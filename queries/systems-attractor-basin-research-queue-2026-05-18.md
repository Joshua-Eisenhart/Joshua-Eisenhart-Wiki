# Systems / Attractor-Basin Research Queue — 2026-05-18

## Purpose
This queue adds actual research support for [[systems-philosophy-attractor-basin-inversion]] and [[topology-carrier-tool-lane]].

It is not just a restatement of the user's correction. It gives the wiki research lanes that can support the claim that strong constraints require wide exploration and that attractor basins emerge from trajectories under constraint pressure.

## Core research claim to support
The wiki/harness process should be modeled as a constrained dynamical and epistemic system:

- wide exploration supplies trajectories;
- constraints define exclusions and viability conditions;
- repeated survival patterns define basins;
- topological and hypergraph tools expose basin shape;
- MMMs compress the discovered basin into language, nouns, examples, and tool roles.

## Non-arXiv foundation lanes
Some required foundations are books/fields/classic literatures, not only arXiv papers.

### Dynamical systems and attractors
Use for: basins of attraction, stability, phase portraits, bifurcation, invariant sets.

Key names / anchors:
- Poincare: qualitative dynamics and phase-space thinking.
- Smale: dynamical systems and structural stability.
- Strogatz: nonlinear dynamics and synchronization pedagogy.
- Wiggins / Guckenheimer-Holmes: invariant manifolds and nonlinear systems.

### Complex systems and self-organization
Use for: emergence, order under constraint, many interacting local trajectories.

Key names / anchors:
- Ashby: requisite variety and regulator logic.
- Kauffman: self-organization and adjacent possible.
- Holland: complex adaptive systems.
- Prigogine: far-from-equilibrium order.
- Kelso / Haken: synergetics and coordination dynamics.

### Viability and safe trajectories
Use for: constraints over time, admissible trajectories, safe sets.

Key names / anchors:
- Aubin: viability theory.
- Nagumo: tangent cone / invariance conditions.
- control theory: invariant sets, reachability, safety filters.

### Topological data analysis and shape of dynamics
Use for: persistent features, basin shape, attractor reconstruction, time-series topology.

Key names / anchors:
- Edelsbrunner, Letscher, Zomorodian: persistent homology.
- Carlsson: topology and data.
- Ghrist: applied topology and sensor/network systems.
- Takens: attractor reconstruction from time series.

### Higher-order networks and cell complexes
Use for: multi-way interactions, hypergraphs, simplicial/cell dynamics.

Key names / anchors:
- Battiston / Bianconi / Petri / Torres: higher-order network dynamics.
- Hodge Laplacians and cellular sheaves: signals on simplicial/cell complexes.

### Search and quality diversity
Use for: why wide exploration is not waste but required to discover basins under constraints.

Key names / anchors:
- Lehman and Stanley: novelty search.
- Mouret and Clune: quality diversity / MAP-Elites.
- evolutionary search and open-endedness literatures.

### Active inference / cybernetics / enactivism
Use carefully for: self-maintaining systems, attractor dynamics, feedback and model updating.

Fences:
- do not let FEP/active inference replace the constraint-first mathematical spine;
- use it as a support/resonance lane for attractor and self-maintenance language.
