## Anti-teleology / basin teleology research lanes

This needs more than one research tradition.

### A. Dynamical systems and basins
Use for: basins of attraction, invariant sets, perturbation, stability, bifurcation.

Already queued in [[systems-attractor-basin-research-queue-2026-05-18]].

### B. Viability / reachability / control
Use for: many possible futures under constraints; admissible trajectories; safe sets; present selection by future-reachable sets.

Important because it gives a non-mystical reading of “future options select the present”: present states are constrained by the set of viable continuations.

### C. Active inference / policy selection with fences
Use for: possible future policies affecting present action selection.

Fence:
- do not promote active inference into root ontology;
- use it as a computational support analogy for future-option pressure.

### D. Path-integral / optimal control / planning-as-inference
Use for: distributions over future trajectories shaping current controls.

Fence:
- no privileged final state;
- many possible futures weight present selection.

### E. Evolutionary search / quality diversity
Use for: wide exploration under constraints; basin discovery by many trials; novelty as necessary input.

### F. Philosophical anti-teleology / immanent finality
Use carefully for: non-destined directedness, final-cause critique, teleonomy, and organismic/process accounts.

Good supporting names/fields:
- Aristotle/final cause as contrast, not authority;
- Kant's purposiveness without determinate purpose as a useful caution lens;
- cybernetics / teleonomy as goal-like behavior without metaphysical destiny;
- Deleuze / Simondon / process philosophy as immanent individuation and metastability;
- enactivism/autopoiesis as self-maintaining directedness without external telos.
