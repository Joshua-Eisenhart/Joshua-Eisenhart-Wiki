# Constraint Core: Prior-Art Positioning, External-Contact Fuel, and Expert Shortlist

## TL;DR
- **The great majority of Constraint Core's technical "earned results" on single-qubit GKSL dynamics, channels, geometric phases, and QM reconstruction are established textbook or active-research material** — the two-sector spectral/eigenvector split, non-unitality as an invariant (L(I)≠0), phase-covariance of amplitude damping/dephasing, Bargmann/Pancharatnam phases as eigenvector-sector meters, and Szilard/Landauer engine accounting are all published and should have novelty claims withdrawn before an expert sees them.
- **A few framings are genuinely less-charted and worth foregrounding**: the specific packaging of "identity as operational indistinguishability" as a *root axiom* to motivate the *qubit specifically* (not general QM), the Hadamard-involution "pair duality" as an organizing device, and the constraint-first assembly narrative — but each borders closely on known work (quantum reference frames, MUBs, reconstruction programs) and must be positioned humbly.
- **The strongest external-contact opportunities** are cloud quantum hardware for Bargmann-invariant/geometric-phase circuits (IBM, photonics), superconducting-qubit Maxwell-demon/Landauer experiments (Murch, Pekola, Cottet), and phase-covariant channel tomography; the entropic-gravity and MBTI/Jungian overlays are the highest-risk arms and should be quarantined from the pure-math ledger shown to experts.

## GOAL A — PRIOR-ART POSITIONING

### A1. Reconstruction of QM from operational/information axioms; the qubit from finiteness; identity as indistinguishability
**Verdict: ACTIVE RESEARCH AREA, heavily populated — reconstruction is a mature subfield.** The canonical reconstructions are Hardy's "Quantum Theory from Five Reasonable Axioms" (2001, arXiv:quant-ph/0101012); Chiribella–D'Ariano–Perinotti "Informational derivation of quantum theory" (Phys. Rev. A 84, 012311, 2011; arXiv:1011.6451), built on the purification postulate; Masanes–Müller (2011); Dakić–Brukner (2011, "Quantum theory and beyond: is entanglement special?"); Höhn "quantum theory from rules on information acquisition" (2017). Dakić–Brukner explicitly use "limited information capacity of elementary systems" — i.e., a finiteness-of-distinguishability axiom — as a core principle. **Verdict on "deriving the qubit specifically from finiteness of distinguishability": partially anticipated.** The reconstructions derive general finite-dimensional QM, of which the qubit is the elementary case; the "one bit of information capacity" idea is precisely how Brukner–Zeilinger and Dakić–Brukner motivate the elementary system. So the *spirit* is published; a *dedicated* constraint-first derivation targeting the qubit as the object may be a novel packaging but is not conceptually new. (Note: constructor theory [Deutsch–Marletto], relational QM [Rovelli], and QBism [Fuchs] are adjacent interpretive programs, not reconstructions in the Hardy/CDP sense; treat them as motivational neighbors, not prior derivations of the qubit.)

**"Identity as operational indistinguishability" as a foundational axiom: ACTIVE in philosophy of physics, NOT as a QM-reconstruction axiom.** The relevant literature is French & Krause, *Identity in Physics* (OUP, 2006); the Stanford Encyclopedia entry "Identity and Individuality in Quantum Theory"; Muller & Saunders (2008) and Muller & Seevinck on weak discernibility; Adam Caulton "Discerning indistinguishable quantum systems" (Phil. Sci. 80, 2013; arXiv:1409.0249); and the Leibniz Principle of Identity of Indiscernibles (PII) debate. **This is the single most defensible "genuinely uncharted" angle** — using operational indistinguishability under admissible probes as a *generative* axiom for the carrier, rather than as a *descriptive* debate about particle individuality, does not appear to have a direct published counterpart. Position it carefully: the metaphysics is well-trodden, the *axiomatic-generative use* is the fresh move.

### A2. Two-sector split: spectral/eigenvalue functionals vs eigenvector/frame; unitary conjugation invisible to spectral invariants
**Verdict: KNOWN/TEXTBOOK, distributed across several literatures.** That all spectral functionals (eigenvalues, entropy, purity) are invariant under unitary conjugation ρ→UρU† is elementary linear algebra / textbook quantum information (Bengtsson & Życzkowski, *Geometry of Quantum States*). The "eigenvector/frame" information that spectral functionals cannot see is exactly the content of the **quantum reference frames and superselection literature**: Bartlett, Rudolph & Spekkens, "Reference frames, superselection rules, and quantum information," Rev. Mod. Phys. 79, 555 (2007; arXiv:quant-ph/0610030), which formalizes "unspeakable" information (frame/direction/phase information) as opposed to speakable information; and Gour & Spekkens, "resource theory of quantum reference frames" (NJP 10, 033023, 2008). **The two-sector split is therefore standard**; the value Constraint Core can add is only in the specific packaging. Novelty claim should be withdrawn.

### A3. Parity/XOR structure requiring two complementary readouts (entropy meter × phase meter)
**Verdict: PARTIALLY KNOWN; the specific "axis parity = dynamics XOR frame" framing has no exact match found, but its components are standard.** The idea that some information is unreadable by any single scalar functional and requires multiplicative/complementary combination is the essence of complementarity and of the speakable/unspeakable divide (A2). There is a directly relevant technical result: work in the non-Markovianity literature exhibits a qubit dynamics for which the **trace-distance (Helstrom) non-Markovianity measure is nonzero while all entropic measures are exactly zero** (i.e., one meter sees what another is blind to) — this concretely realizes the "one scalar functional is blind" claim. No published "axis parity" or "charge-parity readout requiring multiplication of an entropy meter and a phase meter" was found under that name; treat as a plausibly novel *framing* of standard complementarity, not a new theorem.

### A4. Bargmann invariants / Pancharatnam phases as eigenvector-sector instruments; mixed-state geometric phases
**Verdict: ACTIVE RESEARCH AREA, 2020–2026, well-populated — this is a strength to align with, not claim.** Foundational: Bargmann (J. Math. Phys. 5, 862, 1964); Pancharatnam (Proc. Indian Acad. Sci. A44, 247, 1956); Mukunda & Simon kinematic approach; Rabei, Arvind, Simon & Mukunda "Bargmann invariants, null phase curves, and a theory of the geometric phase" (Phys. Rev. A 67, 042114, 2003). Modern measurement circuits: Oszmaniec, Galvão et al. "Measuring relational information between quantum states" / cycle tests (arXiv:2109.10006; NJP 26, 2024); "Quantum circuits for measuring weak values, Kirkwood–Dirac distributions and state spectra" (arXiv:2302.00705); quantum-switch measurement of unitary invariants (PRL 130, 170201, 2023; arXiv:2508.02345); Fernandes, Wagner, Novo & Galvão "Unitary-invariant witnesses of quantum imaginarity" (PRL 133, 190201, 2024); "A Survey of Bargmann Invariants" (arXiv:2601.01858, 2026). Mixed-state geometric phases: Uhlmann phase (Rep. Math. Phys. 24, 229, 1986); Sjöqvist et al. interferometric phase (PRL 85, 2845, 2000); Tong et al. kinematic mixed-state phase (PRL 93, 080405, 2004); unified framework + first Uhlmann measurement (Zhu et al., arXiv:0710.5804). **A "χ₂ eigenvector-sector meter" is essentially a Bargmann-invariant/Pancharatnam-phase instrument; this must be presented as an application of a hot existing toolset.**

### A5. Non-unitality of Lindblad generators as classification invariant (L(I)≠0)
**Verdict: KNOWN/TEXTBOOK.** That L(I)=0 iff the channel is unital, and that non-unitality is characterized by a traceless Hermitian "drift" operator independent of Kraus representation, is standard (see "Phase space contraction and quantum operations," arXiv:quant-ph/0508190; and the unital↔normal-Lindblad-operators result). The parametrization separating unital (rotational) from non-unital (affine translation) parts of the GKLS generator is explicit in the "Analysis of quantum semigroups with GKS-Lindblad generators" literature and the Lie-wedge/Lie-semialgebra work. The "purity-decreasing iff unital" theorem is also published. **Non-unitality as "the exact invariant splitting dissipative from unitary generators" is textbook; withdraw novelty.**

### A6. Phase covariance of amplitude damping and dephasing; order-sensitivity vanishing on the rotation axis
**Verdict: KNOWN/TEXTBOOK — canonical phase-covariant channel theory.** Phase-covariant qubit channels are defined precisely by commutation with z-rotations: e^{iεσz}ℰ[ρ]e^{-iεσz} = ℰ[e^{iεσz}ρe^{-iεσz}]. Canonical references: Holevo, "Covariant quantum Markovian evolutions" (J. Math. Phys. 37, 1812, 1996); Lankinen et al., Phys. Rev. A 93, 052103 (2016); Smirne et al. PRL 116, 120801 (2016); "Phase-covariant mixtures of non-unital qubit maps" (arXiv:2206.10742, 2022). Amplitude damping, dephasing, and emission/absorption are the standard generators of this class, all covariant under z-rotation. **That composition of a phase-covariant channel with a z-rotation is order-insensitive on the z-axis is a direct corollary of covariance — a known result, not new.**

### A7. Hadamard involution swapping z-pinching↔x-pinching and x-rot↔z-rot; MUB duality
**Verdict: KNOWN components (MUBs, complementary channels), but the specific "single Hadamard-involution pair duality" organizing device may be a novel presentation.** The Hadamard W=(σx+σz)/√2 exchanging X and Z bases is the elementary statement that these are mutually unbiased bases; pinchings onto the two abelian (diagonal-in-X, diagonal-in-Z) subalgebras are the two canonical complementary conditional expectations. That these are exchanged by the Hadamard is immediate. No single well-known paper frames "complementary channel pairs conjugate under one involution" as a named result, so the *packaging* may be presentable as a modest observation — but the mathematical content is standard MUB/complementarity theory. Do not overclaim.

### A8. Hopf/Bloch geometry in dissipative qubit dynamics; swept-area chirality; H=±H₀ Weyl pairs
**Verdict: KNOWN/TEXTBOOK for the geometry; ACTIVE for driven-dissipative geometric-phase chirality.** Bloch-sphere/Hopf-fibration structure of the single qubit is textbook (Bengtsson–Życzkowski). Uhlmann phase under isotropic decoherence: Tidström & Sjöqvist (Phys. Rev. A 67, 032110, 2003). Geometric phases as topological order parameters at finite temperature (Uhlmann vs interferometric) are actively studied (e.g., Kitaev-chain analyses; Phys. Rev. B 107, 165415, 2023). Swept-area/geometric-phase signatures of chirality in driven open qubits are an active experimental area (e.g., recent complex-Berry-phase work in superconducting circuits). Opposite-handedness H=±H₀ pairs map to time-reversal/chirality conjugates — a standard symmetry operation. **The geometry is textbook; the chirality-signature application is a real research frontier to engage.**

### A9. Octonions, Cl(0,6), G₂, three-qubit structure
**Verdict: ACTIVE (niche) RESEARCH PROGRAM, well-defined and directly on point.** The specific fact that octonionic left-multiplication generates the Clifford algebra Cl(6) acting on ℂ⁸ (with SU(3)⊂G₂=Aut(O)) is the central construction of Furey's program: Furey, "Standard model physics from an algebra?" (arXiv:1611.09182); "Three generations, two unbroken gauge symmetries, and one eight-dimensional algebra" (Phys. Lett. B 785, 84, 2018; arXiv:1910.08395); Furey & Hughes (Phys. Lett. B 827/831, 2022). Predecessors: Günaydin & Gürsey (J. Math. Phys. 14, 1651, 1973); Dixon, *Division Algebras* (1994); Gresnigt (braids); Stoica Cl(6) (2018); Todorov & Dubois-Violette (exceptional Jordan). The **three-qubit ℂ⁸ = octonionic Hopf fibration** link is exactly Mosseri–Dandoloff (qubit Hopf fibrations) extended to octonions, and appears in recent work "The Standard Model Symmetry and Qubit Entanglement" (arXiv:2512.17328). **This is established (if speculative) territory — align with Furey et al.; do not claim the algebraic facts as new.**

### A10. Quantum Szilard engine, Landauer bound, measurement-feedback work extraction
**Verdict: KNOWN/TEXTBOOK + strong experimental record.** Canonical: Szilard (1929); Landauer (1961); Bérut et al., "Experimental verification of Landauer's principle," Nature 483, 187–189 (7 Mar 2012) — using a single colloidal particle in a modulated double-well, they established that **"the mean dissipated heat saturates at the Landauer bound in the limit of long erasure cycles,"** the first experimental confirmation of the kBT ln2 bound after ~50 years. Also: Toyabe et al. (Nat. Phys. 6, 988, 2010); Koski et al. single-electron Szilard engine (PNAS 111, 13786, 2014); **Naghiloo, Alonso, Romito, Lutz & Murch, "Information gain and loss for a quantum Maxwell's demon," PRL 121, 030604 (17 July 2018)** — working at Washington University (St. Louis), they "use continuous weak measurements of a driven superconducting qubit to experimentally study the information dynamics of a quantum Maxwell's demon," showing information gained can be "converted into work using quantum coherent feedback" and verifying a quantum fluctuation theorem with feedback (arXiv:1802.07205); Cottet et al., "Observing a quantum Maxwell demon at work," PNAS 114, 7561 (2017); single-atom Landauer (Yan et al., PRL 120, 210601, 2018). Reviews: Parrondo, Horowitz & Sagawa, "Thermodynamics of information," Nat. Phys. 11, 131 (2015). **The engine-cycle accounting is textbook quantum information thermodynamics; the model must match these, not reinvent them.**

### A11. Entropic/emergent gravity; gravity from (∇S)² of distinguishability fields
**Verdict: ACTIVE but EMPIRICALLY CONTESTED research area.** Jacobson, "Thermodynamics of spacetime: the Einstein equation of state," PRL 75, 1260 (1995); Verlinde, "On the origin of gravity and the laws of Newton," JHEP 04 (2011) 029; Verlinde, "Emergent gravity and the dark universe" (2016); Van Raamsdonk, "Building up spacetime with quantum entanglement" (Gen. Rel. Grav. 42, 2323, 2010); Faulkner–Guica–Hartman–Myers–Van Raamsdonk "Gravitation from entanglement in holographic CFTs" (JHEP 03, 051, 2014); Maldacena–Susskind ER=EPR (2013). Empirical status: **Brouwer et al., "First test of Verlinde's theory of emergent gravity using weak gravitational lensing" (MNRAS 466, 2547, 2017; arXiv:1612.03034), studying 33,613 isolated central galaxies (KiDS×GAMA), report that "the prediction from EG, despite requiring no free parameters, is in good agreement with the observed galaxy–galaxy lensing profiles in four different stellar mass bins. Although this performance is remarkable, this study is only a first step."** Later cluster-scale tests (MNRAS 487, 3734, 2019) and criticisms (Kobakhidze, "gravity is not an entropic force") are less favorable. **Any (∇S)²-of-entropy toy must eventually face the Brouwer lensing benchmark; presently entropic gravity is not empirically established. This is a high-risk arm — present only as a toy analogy.**

### A12. Homochirality/Frank model; "self-mirror + noncommutation forces a chirality pair"; SMT/solver-verified physics lemmas
**Verdict: MIXED — Frank/Soai chemistry is KNOWN; the formal "noncommutation forces chirality" proof and solver-verified physics lemmas appear NOVEL but unvalidated.** Frank, "On spontaneous asymmetric synthesis" (1953); Kondepudi & Nelson; Soai et al. (Nature 1995) as the sole clean experimental Frank-model realization; Blackmond reviews (Cold Spring Harb. Perspect. Biol. 2019; Chem. Rev. 2020, acs.chemrev.9b00557); parity-violation-energy-difference plausibility (Nat. Chem. 2019, s41557-019-0321-y — finds PVED likely too small to be the symmetry-breaking cause). **The chemistry/amplification is textbook.** No published result stating "self-mirror plus noncommutation forces a chirality pair" as a formal theorem was found; nor a standard practice of using Z3/CVC5 SMT solvers to verify physics lemmas (autoformalization efforts target Lean/Isabelle — see A16). Treat the formal-chirality claim as a genuinely novel but *unrefereed* proposition that needs an independent proof check.

### A13. Quantum free energy principle / active inference in fully quantum terms
**Verdict: ACTIVE, VERY RECENT, small identifiable group — align directly.** Fields, Friston, Glazebrook & Levin, "A free energy principle for generic quantum systems" (Prog. Biophys. Mol. Biol., 2022; arXiv:2112.15242); Fields, Friston, Glazebrook, Levin & Marcianò, "The free energy principle induces neuromorphic development" (Neuromorph. Comput. Eng. 2, 042002, 2022); Fields et al., "Control flow in active inference systems" (2023, arXiv:2303.01514). Underlying classical FEP: Friston "A free energy principle for a particular physics" (arXiv:1906.10184); "The free energy principle made simpler but not too simple" (Phys. Rep. 1024, 2023). Markov blankets as finite cuts and belief updating as CPTP maps with data-processing-inequality monotones is precisely the language of this program. **The quantum-FEP framing is genuinely recent (2021–2024) and not textbook; the model can make honest contact here, but the group is small and specific.**

### A14. Jungian/MBTI formal or dynamical-systems models; academic standing
**Verdict: MBTI is PSYCHOMETRICALLY WEAK and academically disfavored; quantum/mathematical personality models exist only at the fringe. HIGH REPUTATIONAL RISK.** The canonical critique is Pittenger, "Cautionary Comments Regarding the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator": **"Across a 5-week re-test period, 50 percent of the participants received a different classification on one or more of the [MBTI] scales... the test fails to meet standards of test-retest reliability"** (retest disagreement ranges ~39–76% depending on interval). This instability arises largely because the MBTI dichotomizes near-continuous traits at their midpoints — notably, the MBTI Extraversion scale correlates ~0.74 with Big Five Extraversion (McCrae & Costa 1989), i.e., it measures real variance but forces unstable binary cuts. Much supportive research is published in-house (Journal of Psychological Type / CAPT), and the instrument is widely deployed commercially (~88% of Fortune 500 companies have used it) despite the psychometric weakness. Isolated attempts to model Jungian functions as dynamical systems exist (e.g., "A Jungian based framework for Artificial Personality Synthesis," CEUR-WS Vol-1680). **No credible quantum model of personality typology exists in the mainstream literature.** Recommendation: **keep any MBTI/Jungian overlay entirely out of the pure-math proposition ledger shown to physicists** — it will actively damage credibility. If retained at all, frame it as speculative metaphor in a separate document.

### A15. Tomita–Takesaki modular theory used physically; thermal time; "the state generates its own dynamics"
**Verdict: KNOWN/ESTABLISHED PROGRAM.** Connes & Rovelli, "Von Neumann algebra automorphisms and time–thermodynamics relation in generally covariant quantum theories" (Class. Quantum Grav. 11, 1994) — the thermal time hypothesis: the modular automorphism group of a KMS state provides physical time (t = ħβs). Bisognano–Wichmann (modular flow = Lorentz boosts for a wedge); entanglement/modular Hamiltonians in QFT; recent "Spacetime from operator algebras" (arXiv:2606.10924) and "thermal time as an unsharp observable" (J. Math. Phys. 65, 032105, 2024). **"The state generates its own dynamics" is exactly the thermal time hypothesis — established, with active philosophical critique (Swanson, Chua). Align with this program; do not claim as new.**

### A16. AI-assisted math with verification harnesses; autoformalization; cross-substrate numerical validation
**Verdict: ACTIVE, FAST-MOVING (2022–2026); cross-substrate numerical agreement is good practice but not a formal-verification substitute.** Autoformalization with LLMs: Wu et al. (2022); **AlphaProof + AlphaGeometry 2 (Google DeepMind, Nature s41586-025-09833-y, published 12 Nov 2025) "scored 28 points" of 42 at IMO 2024 (silver threshold, one point below gold), "with AlphaProof solving three problems (P1, P2 and P6) and AlphaGeometry 2 solving one (P4)"** — proofs generated and checked in Lean; LeanDojo (Yang et al. 2023); DeepSeek-Prover / Goedel-Prover; PhysLean/HepLean and Lean-QuantumInfo libraries; MerLean "agentic framework for autoformalization in quantum computation" (arXiv:2602.16554). Surveys: "Autoformalization in the Era of Large Language Models" (arXiv:2505.23486). **Key calibration point:** machine-checked correctness means Lean/Isabelle/Coq kernel verification. **Cross-substrate numerical agreement (numpy/JAX/PyTorch/Julia agreeing to 1e-12) is a strong artifact-exclusion / reproducibility practice but is NOT machine-checked proof** — it excludes implementation bugs, not mathematical errors. Present it accurately as reproducibility engineering, and if formal-proof claims are made, route them through Lean/Isabelle to be credible.

## GOAL B — EXTERNAL-CONTACT FUEL

**B1. Bargmann-invariant / open-path geometric-phase circuits on real hardware.** The cycle-test and SWAP-test-generalization circuits of Oszmaniec–Galvão et al. (arXiv:2109.10006; NJP 26, 2024) and the constant-depth / quantum-switch variants (PRL 130, 170201, 2023) are directly runnable on **IBM Quantum** cloud devices and have been demonstrated on photonic processors (contextuality/coherence certification, Sci. Adv. 9, eadj4249, 2023). A "χ₂ eigenvector-sector meter" prediction is checkable by running third-order Bargmann-invariant circuits (Δ₃ = Tr[ρ₁ρ₂ρ₃]) and comparing the extracted Pancharatnam phase to the model's prediction. Single-qubit Lindblad simulation (amplitude damping + dephasing + rotation) is well within current fidelities on superconducting and trapped-ion cloud hardware. **This is the most concrete and low-cost contact point.**

**B2. Quantum thermodynamics experiments the engine cycles must match.** Bérut et al. 2012 (Landauer erasure, kBT ln2 saturation); Koski et al. 2014 (single-electron Szilard); Naghiloo–Murch 2018 (superconducting-qubit quantum Maxwell demon, PRL 121, 030604, WashU); Cottet et al. 2017 (PNAS 114, 7561); Yan et al. 2018 (single-atom Landauer). The model's Szilard/Landauer/Carnot accounting on the qubit must reproduce the kBT ln2 bound and the measured work/information trade-offs in these experiments — they constitute a hard quantitative benchmark.

**B3. Phase-covariant channel tomography / process tomography of amplitude damping + rotation.** Experimental process tomography of amplitude-damping and dephasing compositions is standard on IBM/ion hardware; the model's order-insensitivity-on-the-z-axis prediction is directly testable via process tomography of ℰ∘R_z vs R_z∘ℰ.

**B4. Verlinde entropic-gravity empirical benchmark.** Brouwer et al. 2017 weak-lensing test (MNRAS 466, 2547; 33,613 galaxies, parameter-free agreement flagged as "only a first step") and the 2019 cluster tests are the benchmarks any entropic-gravity toy must eventually face. Currently the evidence is mixed-to-unfavorable at cluster scales; this arm cannot be "validated" now.

**B5. "Gradient descent = dephasing terrain + pinching" as a testable claim.** Assessment: **this is the weakest bridge and is essentially not testable as stated.** There is a large gradient-descent-dynamics literature, but no established operational map from a dephasing-plus-pinching qubit channel to measurable optimizer trajectories. Recommend demoting this from "prediction" to "loose analogy" unless a concrete, falsifiable correspondence is constructed.

**B6. Homochirality amplification datasets.** The Soai reaction kinetic datasets (Blackmond's in-situ kinetics; Cold Spring Harb. Perspect. Biol. 2019) and Frank-model bifurcation data are the comparison points for the chirality arm. The PVED magnitude bound (Nat. Chem. 2019) is the quantitative constraint.

### Norms & realistic success paths for an independent researcher (2026)
- **arXiv endorsement (quant-ph / math-ph):** As of a January 2026 policy tightening (following a December 2025 change in Mathematics), automatic endorsement now generally requires BOTH an institutional email AND prior authorship in the same endorsement domain; new independent submitters typically must obtain a **personal endorsement** from an established arXiv author in that domain (find endorsers via the "Which authors of this paper are endorsers?" link on any abstract page). math-ph is governed by Physics rules. Endorsement is necessary but not sufficient — moderators can still reclassify/reject. This change was driven by a surge of low-quality/AI-generated submissions, so a clean, prior-art-aware manuscript matters more than ever.
- **Quantum (quantum-journal.org):** community-run, non-profit arXiv **overlay** journal; submit from an arXiv-posted (or cross-listed to quant-ph) preprint; no submission fee; conventional peer review by a large volunteer board. Good target for a rigorous single-qubit-dynamics ledger.
- **SciPost Physics:** diamond open access, **fully open peer review** (published referee reports, optional signed); "peer-witnessed refereeing" where registered contributors can also report. Strong fit for work that benefits from transparent scrutiny.
- **PhilSci-Archive (philsci-archive.pitt.edu):** free preprint server for philosophy of science (hosted by University of Pittsburgh) — the right home for the operational-identity-axiom / foundations material.
