## What the cache estate is saying, safely

### Repeated owner intent signals

1. **Process memory caches into a routed wiki estate, not a raw archive.** The recurring ask is to find intent, clarifications, provenance, and source splits, then make them retrievable.
2. **Keep method truth separate from theory truth.** A memory row can say how to work, what to check, or what the owner corrected; it cannot by itself prove a model claim.
3. **Preserve owner-originated kernels and later generated elaboration as different things.** Old owner sources, current owner corrections, assistant summaries, and model-generated closure language must not be merged.
4. **Make claims operational and bounded.** Read exact files, run probes, name support level, and keep `exists`, `runs`, `passes local rerun`, and `canonical by process` apart.
5. **Use multiple agents/models as variation and pressure, not as authority.** Hot/hallucinating lanes may be useful finders only when strict gates select on them.

### Repeated correction patterns

- The owner corrects premature promotion: scratch, memory, scout, table, or generated summary does not become canon.
- The owner corrects route mistakes: wrong repo root, wrong runtime, wrong carrier/backend, wrong build/audit role, wrong order.
- The owner corrects output shape: Wizard/Hermes output should reduce cognitive load, not add dense process walls.
- The owner corrects proxy substitution: Axis0, PEPS3D, entropy, forward dynamics, Bloch quotient, or other proxies must not replace the primary finite constraint/possibility object.
- The owner corrects hidden collapse: surviving alternatives and source-order variants must stay visible until bounded checks exclude them.
