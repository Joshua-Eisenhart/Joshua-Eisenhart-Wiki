Safe root wording:

```text
The primitive is constraint on distinguishability, locally formalized by finite probe-relative indistinguishability under an active probe family.
```

Identity is earned by probe-indistinguishability, not granted as primitive global equality:

```text
a = a iff a ~ b
```

Use this only as an attack on primitive identity. Do not convert it into a global equality axiom.

### 2. Three root expressions must stay separate

One relation appears at three levels:

| Level | Expression | Claim ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| Elements | quotient `S/~_M` | visible identity under probe family |
| Order | noncommutation `A∘B != B∘A` | sequence matters if witnessed on the active object |
| Grouping | nonassociativity / explicit bracketing | grouping is separate from order-swap |

Do not collapse order into bracketing, or quotient identity into carrier ontology.

### 3. Finitude and exclusion-first

Finitude is load-bearing across carriers, support sets, probe families, admissibility predicates, composition/bracketing witnesses, result artifacts, and receipts.


## MSS / weakest-survivor discipline

MSS means **Minimal Survivable Structure**. It is not Occam and not a root axiom. It is an admission meta-gate:

```text
Admit only the weakest structure that survives active constraints and can still carry / compose / continue / be killed under supplied operations.
```

Important caveat: “still evolves” smuggles dynamics if not fenced. Static finite probe data gives quotient structure. Update, time, transition, noncommutation, and history are supplied ingredients unless a sim explicitly earns them.
