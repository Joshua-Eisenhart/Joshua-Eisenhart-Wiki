## One-Page Bottom Line

The strongest honest connection now is not "FEP proves Axis0" or "holodeck proves physics." It is a shared grammar of finite, probe-relative, boundary-mediated readouts.

Josh's current Codex Ratchet pages place `F01` and `N01` as chosen root constraints, not derived truths. From there, the strongest source-grounded internal chain is:

1. finite admissible possibilities and finite probes;
2. probe-induced equivalence `S / ~_M`;
3. finite state-on-algebra representation, with density-matrix readout when the finite algebra is represented complex-linearly;
4. spinor/Hopf/Weyl lift as a way to preserve phase, fiber, sign, and handed path information that rho-only views can erase;
5. engine/operator/terrain placements as explicit finite maps and order-sensitive readouts;
6. Axis0 as an open bridge family from shell/history/cut/fuzz objects into bipartite states and entropy-like functionals;
7. holodeck/FEP/IGT/perception as downstream perception-policy grammars over prediction, boundary, interface, and strategy readouts;
8. cross-field ToE readings as live interpretive lanes, not one settled winner.

The external papers support pieces of this grammar:

- FEP/active inference supports prediction, Markov-blanket partitioning, expected-free-energy policy selection, and "as-if" internal representation.
- Hoffman-style interface theory is not directly anchored by a local paper in this pass, but the wiki's holodeck/IGT pages use it interpretively as a perception-interface warning: perception tracks action viability or fitness-interface value, not primitive truth.
- Hume/Jung/IGT is supported inside the wiki as a perception-policy grammar: reason/operator later, perception/regime first. The external evolutionary-game paper supports information geometry for game dynamics, not the Jung mapping.
- Hopf/qubit geometry and Berry/spin papers support the math vocabulary around Hopf maps, entanglement-sensitive fibrations, Berry curvature, spin transport, and chirality-sensitive controls.
- Constructor theory, resource-theoretic distinguishability, Rovelli, thermodynamic length, and viability theory support the broader discipline: possible/impossible transformations, distinguishability as a resource, relational state/value, entropy-induced geometry, and bounded viable sets under conflicting models.

The biggest open bridge remains Axis0: `Xi_fuzz -> rho_AB -> Phi_0` is a clear proposal/object grammar, but not a proved final cut, not a full gravity/dark-energy bridge, and not a consciousness derivation. The useful next work is to harden the matrix and probe contract, not to skip into larger simulations without explicit lower gates.

## Math/Geometry Status Section

### 1. Probe quotient and state-on-algebra

Status: observed/source-grounded.

Let `S` be a finite candidate state/support set and `M` a finite admissible probe family. The current wiki formula is:

```text
a ~_M b iff forall m in M: m(a) = m(b)
Q = S / ~_M
```

This is the cleanest local expression of "identity/equality are not primitive." Equality is replaced by indistinguishability under an explicit finite probe family. The quotient `Q` is therefore probe-relative and may refine when the probe family changes.

When the quotient/probe object is represented by a finite complex *-algebra, a state can be written as a positive normalized linear functional:

```text
omega(1) = 1
omega(x* x) >= 0
omega(M) = Tr(rho M)
```

The last line is a representation, not a root. It is valid when the finite algebra admits a density-matrix representation. Current ledgers repeatedly warn that "rho-first" means "probe-equivalence first, then rho as a stable representation," not "rho-only ontology."

### 2. Spinor/Hopf lift

Status: observed/source-grounded internally; local-paper-supported externally.

The current carrier formulas appear in the running guide and source ledgers:

```text
H = C^2
rho = 1/2(I + r_x sigma_x + r_y sigma_y + r_z sigma_z)

psi_s(phi, chi; eta)
  = [exp(i(phi + chi)) cos eta,
     exp(i(phi - chi)) sin eta]^T

rho_s = |psi_s><psi_s|
pi(psi) = psi^dag (sigma_x, sigma_y, sigma_z) psi
A_Hopf = -i psi^dag dpsi = dphi + cos(2 eta) dchi
```

The important claim ceiling is that the lift keeps data erased by rho-only views: fiber phase, spinor sign, 360/720 behavior, path class, and handedness candidates. The Mosseri Hopf paper supports S3-to-S2/Bloch and higher qubit Hopf geometry, including entanglement-sensitive S7 maps. It does not admit Josh's full nested engine.

### 3. Fiber/base paths and L/R Weyl signs

Status: observed/source-grounded internally; not final physics.

The current guide separates same-rho fiber motion from base-changing lifted path motion:

```text
gamma_fiber^s(u) = psi_s(phi0 + u, chi0; eta0)
rho_fiber^s(u) = rho_fiber^s(0)

gamma_base^s(u) = psi_s(phi0 - cos(2 eta0)u, chi0 + u; eta0)
A_Hopf(dot(gamma_base)) = 0
rho_base changes

H_L = +H_0
H_R = -H_0
dot r_L = 2 n x r_L
dot r_R = -2 n x r_R
```

This is enough to say there is an explicit handed path/sign grammar. It is not enough to claim chirality is admitted as physics. The guide's own control says chirality needs finite carrier, L/R distinction, same local rule with opposite handedness, nonzero order gap/commutator, erase-handedness control, observable change, and receipt.

Status: local-paper-supported for the external FEP pieces; interpretive as bridge to Ratchet.

- Markov blankets as internal/external conditional-independence partitions mediated by sensory/active states;

```text

  ~ boundary/cut/probe partition

expected free energy policy selection
  ~ admissible continuation weighting / policy readout

internal-external synchronization
  ~ holodeck prediction-confirmation grammar
```

Ceiling: this is support, not derivation. Friston's framework starts inside stochastic/probabilistic/continuous assumptions. Codex Ratchet must strip primitive probability, primitive time, and classical Bayesian priors before promotion.
