# Gradient digging and Ratchet drive — v0.4

> Constrained distinguishability is the root. A surviving distinction/entropy gradient supplies drive for a particular
> tooth. The representation of that gradient is proposed and tested at the gate; it is not fixed beforehand.

## The v0.3 mistake

v0.3 correctly remembered that a tooth needs a gradient, but then required one pre-licensed scalar functional and a
universal set of freeze/closure/injection/coupling controls before exploration. That could make the current language of
the model decide whether alternatives were allowed to be generated. It froze gradient digging.

It also assumed every valid gradient must vanish when an obligation closes. That contradicts hypotheses in this bundle
where a local demand closes while a larger or permanent gradient remains. Closure behavior belongs to the proposed
gradient's type, not to a universal process rule.

## Working rule

At each gate the Explorer generates a finite family of possible drive readings, for example:

- distinctions merged by the weaker presentation;
- prediction or reconstruction residual;
- unresolved path/order/grouping contrast;
- capacity or boundary contrast;
- partial-order or vector-valued drive;
- Shannon, von Neumann, relative-entropy, BKM, or other typed entropy after their prerequisites are available;
- exogenous-source models, explicitly labeled exogenous;
- deliberately injected and label-sensitive negatives.

For a proposed readout `V`, a finite candidate contrast can be written

\[
g_V(X\to Y)=V(X)-V(Y).
\]

This notation does not make `V` a scalar, an entropy, or canon. Every proposal declares its domain, direction, expected
coupling, and controls. Different types can coexist on the frontier.

## Strict drive gate

A drive proposal for one tooth must:

1. change across the proposed repair in the declared direction;
2. track the distinction the weaker structure lost;
3. survive admissible representation changes such as label permutation;
4. respond as predicted when its claimed source is changed;
5. remain distinct from MSS complexity preference and from an arbitrary reward;
6. be recorded with killed rivals and open attacks.

The controls are generated from the proposal. An order-deficit drive is tested by order erasure; a topology drive by
topology shuffle; a permanent-source drive by changing that source; an exogenous drive by source accounting. No single
control behavior is forced on every gradient type.

## No current witness means DIG

```text
NO SURVIVING DRIVE PROPOSAL
    -> generate more functionals, probes, histories, carriers, resolutions, and countermodels
    -> UNRESOLVED_GATE__DIG_CONTINUES
```

It does not mean the universe is flat, no gradient exists, or the project should stop. A finite run can end because its
budget is exhausted, but its open dig queue remains executable.

## Current executed result

`runs/root_history_run_v0_4.json` explored six possible drives at each of two gates. Two survived:

- `partition_conflict_mass`;
- `merged_distinction_pairs`.

Four were killed as drives at those gates:

- raw outcome Shannon entropy did not couple to either repair;
- representation cell count measured added structure rather than the lost distinction and survived the commuting
  counter-surface;
- an injected constant supplied no direction;
- a label-code score was an ornamental negative.

That result is deliberately narrow. It does not select a universal entropy formula. It demonstrates that gradient
digging can produce, test, kill, and retain candidate drives rather than requiring the answer in advance.
