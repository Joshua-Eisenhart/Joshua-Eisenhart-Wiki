# Pack lineage: 167 to 189

## Bottom line

Between 2026-07-16 and 2026-07-19 the owner ran a rapid-fire sequence of roughly 30 execution/reconciliation packs (167 through 189), each one auditing and partially superseding its predecessor. The throughline is a repeated failure mode — a pack narrows the project to fit what it can execute, loses fuel/provenance in the process, and the next pack has to repair the loss — followed by a real correction at 181 and genuine mechanism progress at 186–188. Pack 189 is the current synthesis: it names three coupled P0 priorities, ranks donors/competitors, and states a repo-wide rule that NumPy cannot carry a promoted scientific claim. This page is a lineage map only; no claim below rises above what its own source pack states.

## Pre-167 base estate

The two earliest audited archives are pack 53 (nested-manifold owner provenance: apple-notes dumps, `Ring_Checkerboard_Gradient.md`, the working math scaffold) and pack 125 (v0.3 process kernel: root/drive/MSS/gate/pawl separation, an executable `ratchet_kernel.py` receipt validator). Neither is governing authority — pack 53 is drafts, pack 125's validator checks receipt shape, not scientific truth (167_3-source `EARLY_RATCHET_LINEAGE_AUDIT.md`).

Midline packs 145 → 146 → 149 → 150 → 151 → 154 → 166 are a preservation chain, audited bytewise. 151 deleted 26 files that 150 had added (a real regression); 166 ("reconciled ratchet v0.11.7") restores them and is the best historical estate, but is explicitly **not** current authority — its `RATCHET_SPEC.md` conflates the root hypothesis with process authority, and its "one process, no branches" doctrine conflicts with the later plural-MSS model (167_3-source `MIDLINE_LINEAGE_AUDIT.md`). Pack 146 already carried a `no_numpy_gate.py` — the NumPy-exclusion rule that resurfaces in 189 has roots this far back.

## Pack 189's priorities (`02_PRIORITY_AND_EXECUTION_MAP.md`)

Ranked by immediate leverage on the whole program, not by truth or importance. P0/P1 lanes are meant to run concurrently, not sequentially:

- **P0 — State/pawl/conservation.** Append-only source map, correction ledger, raw receipts, replay/diff pack. Must not be substituted by a prose summary or a new "authority" file that overwrites older state.
- **P0 — Native whole-object vertical slice.** One `ManifoldState` semantics shared by Julia/JAX/PyTorch, one genuine new Ratchet tick, one Lev replay receipt. Must not be substituted by a Python-only composite or inherited green receipts.
- **P0 — Root Ratchet as a live selector.** A generated/competed candidate set with frozen requirements and a countermodel/deletion witness. Must not be substituted by predeclared "winner" labels or scalar scoring.
- **P1 — Coherent finite-history / record-cut weld.** Finite ordered/bracketed history carrier with coherent-vs-recorded-vs-phase-scrambled controls. Must not be a branchwise Kraus mixture.
- **P1 — Dynamic Axis-0 test.** A structured receipt retaining continuations, indistinguishability, amplitudes, basins, records, multi-tick interventions. Must not be `log d-S`, parity/XOR, or an authored schedule.
- **P1 — Holodeck/JEPA empirical line.** A body-gaze/action-conditioned object benchmark with held-out interventions. Must not be an image-embedding benchmark without actions.
- **P2 — Carrier and geometry tournament.** Hopf/spinor/QCA/tensor/Jordan/octonion candidates earned via deletion witnesses, not chosen for aesthetic fit.
- **P2 — Physics extraction.** Noncircular gravity/clock/dark-sector/no-signalling gates, only after the runtime keeps its own distinctions.

## The NumPy rule

Stated in three places in pack 189, consistently: "The current claim path is Julia + JAX + PyTorch + typed proof lanes. NumPy may remain a diagnostic/control utility, but cannot silently become evidence for a scientific promotion" (189, `00_READ_FIRST.md`); "No NumPy on promoted claim paths" (189, `03_INFLUENCES_AND_COMPETITORS.md`); "current scientific claim-path policy requires native Julia, JAX, and PyTorch rather than NumPy" (189, `07_EVIDENCE_STATUS_AND_SOURCES.md`). The same rule appears as far back as pack 146's `ratchet/no_numpy_gate.py` (per the 167_3-source `MIDLINE_LINEAGE_AUDIT.md`), and it is the specific reason 189 will not let the 186–188 line stand as the claim-bearing runtime: that line's active runner is Python/NumPy/SciPy-based.

## HELD OPEN — 188/189 vs system_v8 base-licensing tension

Pack 189's priority map licenses P0 work — the vertical slice, the live Ratchet selector, the state/pawl conservation — as the highest-leverage next step, and frames 186–188's whole-manifold, carrier-tournament, and axes-7–12 upper-engine-field material as valuable but not yet claim-bearing until ported natively. `system_v8/loop3_senses/LOOP3_FOUNDATION_CARD.md` (2026-07-19, same day as 189) states a different binding order for the live repo: a fail-closed visibility sanity gate (G1–G5) must pass **before any carrier scaling**, on the finding that Pack 188's recurrent body (`rho_{t+1}=F(rho,d,G)`) has no object argument and provably carries zero object information (`chi(O:rho)=0`), and that loop-2's "first senses" path is broken (full visibility performs near chance). See [[concepts/qit-engines-perception-program]] for the full v8 treatment.

These are not obviously the same claim stated two ways. 189's priority ladder is about which *executable slice* to build next across Ratchet/manifold/engines/Lev; the v8 foundation card is a narrower, sharper finding about one specific artifact — Pack 188's engine field — lacking perception by construction, and a gate meant to stop any further carrier work until that is fixed. It is possible these are complementary (v8's gate is a concrete instance of 189's P1 Holodeck/JEPA lane, and P0's "native vertical slice" would need to pass the same visibility gate to be honest). It is also possible they are in tension: 189 treats 186–188 as a laboratory worth porting into the native vertical slice at P0, while the v8 card treats Pack 188 specifically as a demonstrated dead end for perception (an "eraser" attractor) that should not be scaled further until senses are rebuilt from loop 1. This page could not fully resolve which specific claims conflict versus merely differ in scope, and per instruction that tension is held open rather than resolved here.
