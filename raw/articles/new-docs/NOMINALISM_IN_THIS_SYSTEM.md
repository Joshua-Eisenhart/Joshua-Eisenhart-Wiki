# Nominalism In This System

Date: 2026-04-05
Status: Working doc — thinking this through, not asserting conclusions

---

## What nominalism means in general

Nominalism says: only particular things exist. Universals, abstract objects,
categories, and kinds are names we give to groups of particulars — not real
things that exist independently of their instances.

The opposite (realism/Platonism) says: there really IS such a thing as
"redness" or "the number 3" or "the law of gravity" — existing independently
of any particular red thing, any group of three objects, or any particular
gravitational interaction.

Most of science and math is implicitly Platonist. When a physicist writes
F=ma, they treat "force," "mass," and "acceleration" as real abstractions
that exist independently of any particular interaction. When a mathematician
proves a theorem about "all triangles," they treat "triangle" as a real kind.

## What nominalism means in THIS system

This system's nominalism starts from one axiom: a=a iff a~b.

In this working doc, that means: a thing has identity only if it is distinguishable from something else, under a specific finite set of admissible probes. Identity is probe-relative distinguishability, not intrinsic essence.

Working this out:

### No identity without contrast

A thing by itself, with nothing to compare it to, has no identity.
"This apple" is meaningless without "not-this-apple" under some probe
that can tell them apart. Identity is relational, not intrinsic.

This kills: the idea that things have essences. An apple doesn't have
"appleness." It has a set of probe responses that currently distinguish
it from non-apples. Change the probes, change the identity.

### No universals without probes

"Redness" as an abstract universal requires that all red things share
something probe-independent. But a=a iff a~b says identity only exists
relative to probes. So "redness" is a name for a particular probe response
pattern (wavelength discrimination), not a real abstract thing.

This doesn't say redness is fake. It says redness is a probe response.
The probe (a retina, a spectrometer) is particular and material. The
response is particular and material. The "universal" is a name for the
pattern of responses. The pattern is real — but it's made of particular
material probe-responses, not of abstract essence.

### No laws without surviving patterns

"The law of gravity" in Platonism is a real abstract object that governs
particular interactions. In this system: gravity is a name for a
correlation pattern that has not been killed by any probe. It is a
particular pattern that has survived. Not a law that exists above
the particulars — a pattern among the particulars.

If a probe killed it (found a case where the correlation didn't hold),
the "law" would die. Laws are survivors, not rulers.

### No math without material

"The number 3" in Platonism exists in an abstract realm. In this system:
"3" is a name for a distinguishability class — three things that are
mutually distinguishable under some probe set. The number is the probe
result, not an abstract object.

Math is not discovered in a Platonic realm. Math is the structure of
constraint on distinguishability. It is material — made of the same
stuff as everything else (spacetime = constraint substrate).

## How this connects to the system's primitives

### Constraint on distinguishability (the one substance)

If a=a iff a~b, then the most primitive thing is: what makes
distinguishability possible. What is the substrate in which probes
can return "different" or "same"?

The system's answer: constraint on distinguishability IS the primitive
substance. Not matter, not energy, not information, not entropy. Those
are all downstream:

  constraint on distinguishability (primitive)
  ├── matter = constraint (what resists, what has structure)
  ├── energy = constraint flow (how constraint changes)
  ├── geometry = constraint compatibility (how constraints fit together)
  ├── dynamics = constraint-respecting transformation (what can change)
  └── information = constraint bookkeeping (what is recorded)

Entropy comes later — it is a measure of how much distinguishability
exists in a region. Not the primitive. A bookkeeping tool on the
primitive.

### Why this is anti-abstract but not anti-form

Plato said: forms exist in an abstract realm, separate from matter.
This system says: forms exist, but they are treated as the same material substrate as everything else, not as separate abstract objects.

The form of a triangle is real — it is a constraint-compatibility pattern in the substrate. But it doesn't live in a separate realm. It lives in the same spacetime as the particular triangles.

Nothingness (spacetime, the substrate of distinguishability) is treated here as the base material. The "nothing" between things is part of that substrate. Forms are patterns in that material. Not separate. Not abstract.

### Why statistics is prior to identity

If identity requires distinguishability (a=a iff a~b), and
distinguishability is a probe result, then identity is a statistical
statement: "under this probe set, these things are distinguishable
with these frequencies."

Statistical structure is not a description of pre-existing identities.
Statistical structure IS how identities exist at all. Without the
statistics (the probe results, the frequencies of distinguishability),
there are no identities.

So statistics is not applied to things. Statistics is prior to things.
Things emerge from statistical structure, not the other way around.

This is why "statistics IS causality" — not because statistics replaces
a separate thing called causality, but because what we call causality
(regular patterns of correlation) IS the statistical structure of
distinguishability. There is no separate causal layer underneath.

### Why subject and object are the same material

If identity requires a probe (a=a iff a~b), and the probe itself is
made of the constraint substrate, then the probe and the probed are
the same material.

A human looking at an apple: the human (probe) is constraint-structure.
The apple (probed) is constraint-structure. The boundary between them
is drawn by the probe — it's a particular distinguishability pattern,
not a feature of the substrate.

Without the human's probe (utility-projection: "is this edible?"),
the apple-boundary doesn't exist in the substrate. The substrate
doesn't have apple-shaped regions on its own. The apple is a
probe-relative identity, not a substrate-intrinsic one.

Mars has no objects because no probe is projecting utility-boundaries
onto it. The substrate is there. The distinguishability is there.
But no probe is drawing boundaries, so no identities emerge.

## What this means for the ratchet

The ratchet doesn't discover pre-existing truths (Platonic).
The ratchet tests what particular patterns survive particular
constraint checks.

"Survived" doesn't mean "is true." It means: this particular pattern
was not yet distinguished from M(C) by any probe applied so far.
It might be killed by the next probe. Nothing is permanently true.
Only not-yet-killed.

The graveyard (killed patterns) is more informative than the survivor
set because it narrows the boundary of M(C) by elimination. Each kill
is a particular probe result on a particular candidate. Not a universal
truth — a particular measurement.

## What this means for LLMs

LLMs are trained on text that is overwhelmingly Platonist:
- "The law of gravity says..." (law as real abstract thing)
- "This proves that..." (proof as discovering eternal truth)
- "The correct answer is..." (correctness as Platonic property)
- "X causes Y" (causality as abstract relation between types)

The nominalist equivalents:
- "The correlation pattern labeled 'gravity' has survived all probes..."
- "This pattern has not been killed by this constraint check..."
- "Under this probe set, this candidate is not yet distinguished from M(C)..."
- "X and Y co-vary; no probe has established their independence..."

But even these equivalents can become empty formulas (as I demonstrated
with "under probing" repeated 16 times). The real test is whether the
person/agent THINKS in terms of particular probe results on particular
candidates, or thinks in terms of abstract truths and just dresses
the language up.

The harness can enforce the language. It cannot enforce the thinking.
But enforcing the language makes the wrong thinking harder to express,
which is the best a finite constraint can do.

## Formal derivations from a=a iff a~b

Using the formal spine from DISTINGUISHABILITY_FORMAL_REFERENCE.md.

### Derivation 1: a=a iff a~b kills primitive identity

Formal version: identity is the equivalence class in the quotient S/~_M,
where ~_M is the indistinguishability relation induced by the admissible
probe family M (see: quotient structures in the distinguishability ref).

a=a (identity) only holds relative to a probe family M that can
distinguish a from at least one other thing b. Without M, no
equivalence relation exists, no quotient exists, no identity exists.

This is not epistemological ("we can't know identity without probes").
It is structural ("identity is constituted by the quotient structure
of a probe family"). The density matrix IS this: infinitely many pure
state decompositions exist, no POVM separates them, so the density
matrix IS the equivalence class. Identity at the quantum level is
literally the quotient under admissible measurements.

F01 adds: the probe family M must be finite. This means the quotient
has finite resolution. Identity is always coarse-grained to some degree.
Infinite-resolution identity (identity under ALL possible probes) is
forbidden by F01.

### Derivation 2: a=a iff a~b kills primitive equality

Two things are "equal" iff no probe in M distinguishes them.
This is operational equivalence (Spekkens): P1 ~ P2 iff for all
measurements and outcomes, p(k|P1,M) = p(k|P2,M).

"Equality" is not a fact about things. It is a fact about a probe
family's inability to distinguish them. Change M, change what counts
as equal. Equality is probe-relative, not absolute.

The data processing inequality (DPI) guarantees stability: if two
things are indistinguishable under M, they remain indistinguishable
under any processing of M's outputs. You can lose distinctions but
never gain them. Once equal, always equal under that probe family
and any coarsening of it.

### Derivation 3: a=a iff a~b kills primitive causality

Causality (A causes B) requires distinguishing "A happened then B
happened" from "B happened then A happened" or "B happened without A."
This requires a probe family that can:
1. Distinguish A from not-A
2. Distinguish B from not-B
3. Distinguish the sequence (A then B) from other sequences

All three are probe-relative (from derivations 1 and 2).
N01 says: composition order matters (AB ≠ BA). But N01 is about
the STRUCTURE of composition, not about temporal causation. The
"cause" is a compression of "this probe family distinguishes the
sequence AB from BA" into a narrative.

Formally: if the probe family M cannot distinguish the sequence
(A then B) from (B then A), then "A causes B" has no operational
content under M. Causality is the distinguishability of sequences
under a particular probe family. Not a primitive relation.

Statistical correlations (A and B co-vary under all probes in M)
are the probe-family-relative observation. "Causality" is the
narrative compression of that observation. This is Hume formalized:
constant conjunction (probe-relative co-variation) is all we observe.
The "necessary connection" (causation as primitive) is the narrative
added on top.

### Derivation 4: a=a iff a~b creates subject=object collapse

The probe and the probed are both elements of the same set S.
The probe family M acts on S. The quotient S/~_M defines identities.
But the probe itself is an element of S and subject to the same
quotient under other probe families.

There is no privileged "observer" position outside S. Every probe
is also a probed thing under some other probe family. The distinction
between "subject" (probe) and "object" (probed) is itself a
probe-relative distinction, not a structural feature of S.

Formally: there is no meta-probe-family that can distinguish "probes"
from "non-probes" in a way that survives all other probe families.
The subject/object boundary is in the quotient, not in the substrate.

### Derivation 5: a=a iff a~b connects to FEP

In FEP, the generative model is a parameterized family of
distributions {q_μ} (the probe family). Perception = updating μ
to minimize free energy = finding the quotient class that best
matches observations. Action = changing observations to match the
current quotient class.

Mapping:
- Probe family M = generative model parameters μ
- Quotient S/~_M = the organism's "world" (what it can distinguish)
- Updating M = perception (refining the quotient)
- Changing what's probed = action (active inference)
- a=a iff a~b = identity only exists relative to the generative model

The structural correspondence: FEP's "minimizing surprise" is
"maintaining a probe family under which the organism's identity
(its Markov blanket) persists in the quotient." The organism IS
its quotient class under its own probe family.

NOTE: This is a structural correspondence, not a derivation of FEP
from a=a iff a~b. The derivation would require showing that F01+N01
necessarily produce FEP-like dynamics, which is not yet done.

### Derivation 6: a=a iff a~b → power laws (attempt)

The derivation chain (attempted, not proven):

Step 1: Indistinguishability is the default state.
Under no probes (empty M), everything is indistinguishable.
S/~_∅ = {S} — one equivalence class containing everything.
This is maximum entropy / maximum symmetry / bell curve.

Step 2: Adding probes creates distinctions.
Each probe added to M refines the quotient — splits equivalence
classes. Some things become distinguishable that weren't before.

Step 3: Not all probes create equal refinement.
Some probes split large classes (high Fisher information at that
point on the statistical manifold). Others split small classes or
create no new distinctions. The Blackwell order ranks probes by
their discriminatory power.

Step 4: The distribution of refinement power across probes...

THIS IS WHERE THE DERIVATION BREAKS. I cannot formally derive that
the distribution of refinement power follows a power law from
a=a iff a~b alone. The claim that power laws arise from the
identity axiom requires additional structure (likely from N01:
the ORDER in which probes are applied matters, and some orderings
compound refinement multiplicatively, which could generate
scale-free distributions).

This is an open problem. The connection between a=a iff a~b and
power laws is asserted by the owner but not formally derived here.

### Derivation 7: a=a iff a~b → feedback loops (attempt)

The claim: negative and positive feedback are fundamental, and they
arise from the identity axiom.

Attempt:

Identity requires contrast (a=a iff a~b). Contrast requires
distinguishability. Distinguishability under a probe family creates
a quotient with BOUNDARIES between equivalence classes.

Near a boundary: small perturbations can move a thing from one class
to another (distinguishable → indistinguishable or vice versa).
This is SENSITIVITY — positive-feedback-like behavior. Small change
→ category change → cascading consequences.

Far from a boundary: perturbations don't change the class membership.
This is ROBUSTNESS — negative-feedback-like behavior. Perturbation
→ return to same class → stability.

So the quotient structure ITSELF generates regions of robustness
(negative feedback, homeostasis) and regions of sensitivity
(positive feedback, allostasis). The boundary between them is where
the identity structure is most fragile.

THIS IS SUGGESTIVE BUT NOT A FORMAL DERIVATION. The connection
between quotient boundary structure and feedback loop dynamics
requires additional formal work — possibly connecting to viability
kernel boundaries (where the system is near the edge of the viable
set) and the stochastic dynamics near those boundaries.

   distinguishability is the primitive" are the same claim —
   ATTEMPTED RESOLUTION: yes, they are the same claim stated from
   different angles. Statistics = the structure of probe results.
   Identity = the quotient under probe results. Constraint on
   distinguishability = what determines which probe results are
   admissible. All three describe the same formal structure
   (the probe family M, the equivalence relation ~_M, and the
   quotient S/~_M) from different perspectives.

6. Neg/pos feedback depth — PARTIALLY ADDRESSED in derivation 7.
