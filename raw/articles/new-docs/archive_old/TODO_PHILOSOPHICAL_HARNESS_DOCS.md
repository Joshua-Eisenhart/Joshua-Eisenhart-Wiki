## What's needed

### 1. Chinese philosophical variant

Confucianism and Mencius are highly aligned with nominalist themes.
There are academic studies correlating Chinese philosophy with Western
nominalism. Need:
- Confucian/Mencian positions on universals, names, rectification of names
- How these relate to and differ from Hume/Berkeley/Ockham
- The Daoist contribution (if relevant — names and the unnamed)
- Academic correlation studies between Chinese and Western nominalism
- Why the Chinese variant may be CLOSER to what this system does

### 2. The owner's specific twists on nominalism

The owner is NOT a simple nominalist. The system draws from nominalism
as one important thread but is not wholly nominalistic. Need to document:
- Where the system agrees with standard nominalism
- Where it diverges (e.g., anti-abstract but not anti-form — forms are
  material, not denied)
- The specific twists (statistics as causality, subject=object,
  constraint on distinguishability as primitive substance)
- What other philosophical traditions contribute (FEP, evolutionary
  epistemology, process philosophy, information-theoretic approaches)
- The subtle nuances — it's a whole school of thought, not one claim

### 3. The harness itself

The philosophical docs are not just reference. They need to become
an operational harness that constrains how agents think:
- What vocabulary is nominalist-safe vs nominalist-violating
- What framing patterns to avoid (Platonic identity, causal arrows,
  essentialist categories)
- What framing patterns to prefer (derived from the philosophy, not
  from mechanical "under probing" substitution)
- How to test whether an agent is actually thinking nominally vs
  just dressing Platonic thinking in nominalist clothing

### 4. Integration across traditions

The owner sees correlated things as the same thing from different
angles. The Western nominalist tradition and the Chinese philosophical
tradition may be two such angles on the same underlying position.
The integration doc should:
- Map the correlations between traditions
- Find the shared structure underneath
- Express it in the system's own terms (constraint on distinguishability)
- Not force one tradition to be the "real" one

## What this is NOT

- Not a survey of all philosophy
- Not an academic paper
- Not a defense of nominalism against realism
- It's a HARNESS — operational docs that constrain how agents
  working on this system must think about identity, universals,
  causation, and categories
