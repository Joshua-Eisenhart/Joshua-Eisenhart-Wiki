Date: 2026-04-05
Status: Reference doc — the actual mathematics, not the system's application

## Statistical Manifold

A smooth manifold M whose points are probability distributions. Given
parametric family {p(x;θ) : θ ∈ Θ ⊆ Rⁿ}, each parameter vector θ labels
a point on M.

A statistical manifold is a triple (M, g, C): manifold M with Riemannian
metric tensor g and totally symmetric cubic tensor C (Amari-Chentsov tensor).

"Nearness" is NOT Euclidean in parameter space. Two Gaussians with means
0 and 0.01 but variance 0.0001 are very FAR apart (nearly non-overlapping).
Same means but variance 100 are extremely close. The geometry tracks
distinguishability, not parameter distance.

## Fisher-Rao Metric

g_ij(θ) = E_{p_θ}[(∂_i log p_θ)(∂_j log p_θ)]

Under regularity: g_ij = -E_{p_θ}[∂_i ∂_j log p_θ]

The metric is the Hessian of KL divergence at zero separation:
D_KL(p_θ || p_{θ+dθ}) = (1/2) Σ_{ij} g_ij dθ_i dθ_j + O(|dθ|³)

Measures LOCAL DISTINGUISHABILITY of nearby distributions — how much
information one observation carries about which distribution generated it.

### Čencov's Uniqueness Theorem (1982)

The Fisher-Rao metric is, up to a positive scalar, the UNIQUE Riemannian
metric on statistical models invariant under sufficient statistics
(Markov morphisms).

A Markov morphism maps a model into a higher-dimensional one via a
stochastic matrix. Invariance: every such embedding acts as an isometry.

This elevates the Fisher metric from a convenient choice to the
CATEGORICALLY FORCED one. The geometry of distinguishability is not
arbitrary — it is determined by the requirement of invariance under
information-preserving maps.

## Amari's Alpha-Connections

One-parameter family of torsion-free affine connections:

Γ^(α)_{ij,k} = Γ^(0)_{ij,k} - (α/2) C_{ijk}

where C_{ijk} = E_{p_θ}[(∂_i log p)(∂_j log p)(∂_k log p)]

Special cases:
- α = 0: Levi-Civita connection (metric-compatible)
- α = 1: e-connection (exponential). Flat for exponential families.
- α = -1: m-connection (mixture). Flat for mixture families.

Duality: ∇^(α) and ∇^(-α) are conjugate with respect to Fisher metric.

Exponential families are e-flat (Γ^(e) = 0 in natural parameters).
Mixture families are m-flat (Γ^(m) = 0 in mixture parameters).
Both simultaneously → dually flat structure.

## Divergence Functions

### KL Divergence

D_KL(p||q) = ∫ p(x) log(p(x)/q(x)) dx

Non-symmetric. Not a metric. But induces Fisher metric via Hessian.

### Rényi Divergence

D_α(P||Q) = (1/(α-1)) log(Σ_i p_i^α / q_i^{α-1})

Limit α→1 recovers KL. Nondecreasing in α. Satisfies DPI for all α > 0.

### f-Divergences (Csiszár, Ali-Silvey)

D_f(p||q) = ∫ q(x) f(p(x)/q(x)) dx

for convex f with f(1)=0. Every f-divergence induces the Fisher metric
and an alpha-connection.

### Bregman Divergence

B_F(θ : θ') = F(θ) - F(θ') - (θ-θ')ᵀ ∇F(θ')

for strictly convex potential F. KL = Bregman generated by negative entropy.
Bregman divergences satisfy an EXACT Pythagorean theorem (not inequality).

Key: KL is the UNIQUE divergence that is simultaneously an f-divergence
AND a Bregman divergence (on finite alphabets).

## Exponential Families

Canonical form:
p_θ(x) = exp{Σ_i θ^i t_i(x) - F(θ) + k(x)}

- θ: natural parameters
- t(x): sufficient statistics
- F(θ): log-partition (cumulant) function
- k(x): base measure

Moment parameters: η_i = E[t_i(x)] = ∂F/∂θ^i

Fisher metric in natural parameters: g_ij = ∂²F/∂θ^i∂θ^j = Cov[t_i, t_j]

(θ, η) form dually flat coordinates. Bregman divergence B_F(θ:θ')
equals D_KL(p_{θ'} || p_θ).

## Projection Theorems

### e-Projection (I-projection)

p* = arg min_{p∈P} D_KL(p || q)

Mode-seeking (zero-avoiding in q's support).

### m-Projection (reverse I-projection)

p* = arg min_{p∈P} D_KL(q || p)

Mean-seeking (covers all of q's support).

### Pythagorean Theorem

In dually flat manifold with Bregman divergence, when e-geodesic from p*
to q is orthogonal to m-autoparallel submanifold P at p*:

B_F(θ_p : θ_q) = B_F(θ_p : θ_{p*}) + B_F(θ_{p*} : θ_q)

EXACT equality, not just inequality.

### Inference as Projection

- Maximum likelihood: m-projection of empirical distribution onto model
- Maximum entropy: e-projection onto moment constraint submanifold
- Variational inference: m-projection of true posterior onto tractable family
- EM algorithm: alternating e- and m-projections

## Quantum Information Geometry

Extension to density matrices ρ (positive semidefinite, trace 1).

### Quantum Fisher Information (QFI)

Symmetric logarithmic derivative L_μ defined by:
(ρL_μ + L_μρ)/2 = ∂ρ/∂θ^μ

QFI matrix: J_μν = tr[ρ(L_μL_ν + L_νL_μ)/2]

Quantum Cramér-Rao: Cov(θ̂) ≥ J⁻¹.

### Bures Metric

D_B(ρ₁,ρ₂)² = 2[1 - √F(ρ₁,ρ₂)]

QFI metric = 4 × Bures metric.

Spectral form: ds² = (1/2) Σ_{j,k} |⟨j|dρ|k⟩|² / (λ_j + λ_k)

### Petz Classification (1996)

UNLIKE classical case (Čencov: unique metric up to scale), the quantum
case admits INFINITELY MANY monotone metrics. Bijective correspondence:

{symmetric monotone metrics} ↔ {operator monotone functions f:(0,∞)→(0,∞)
                                 with f(t) = t·f(1/t)}

Special cases:
- f(t) = (1+t)/2 → Bures/SLD metric (SMALLEST monotone metric)
- f(t) = 2t/(1+t) → Kubo-Mori metric (LARGEST monotone metric)
- f(t) = √t → Wigner-Yanase metric

The non-uniqueness in the quantum case (vs classical uniqueness) is a
fundamental structural difference between classical and quantum information.

## Applications to Machine Learning

### Natural Gradient (Amari, 1998)

θ_{t+1} = θ_t - η I(θ_t)⁻¹ ∇L(θ_t)

Steepest descent on the Riemannian manifold, not Euclidean parameter space.
Reparameterization-invariant. Avoids plateaus from near-singular Fisher.

Computational challenge: N×N Fisher matrix for N parameters. Infeasible
for modern networks (N~10⁸). Approximations: K-FAC (block-diagonal),
empirical Fisher, diagonal.

IGO framework (Ollivier et al. 2017): natural gradient on manifold of
search distributions. Recovers CMA-ES as special case.

## Applications to Thermodynamics

### Thermodynamic Length (Crooks 2007, Sivak-Crooks 2012)

L = ∫ dt √(dλ/dt · g(λ) · dλ/dt)

where g(λ) is the friction tensor (related to equilibrium correlations).

Dissipated work bounded: W_diss ≥ L²/τ

Optimal protocols are GEODESICS of the thermodynamic metric. Move slowly
where friction is high, quickly where low.

### TUR as Cramér-Rao

The thermodynamic uncertainty relation:
ε² · ⟨Σ_tot⟩/k ≥ 2

is a SPECIAL CASE of the Cramér-Rao inequality where Fisher information
= total entropy production. Entropy production bounds current precision
exactly as Fisher information bounds parameter estimation.

## Applications to Neuroscience / FEP

Internal states parameterize variational densities q_μ(z), forming a
statistical manifold M_q with Fisher-Rao metric.

Free energy minimization = natural gradient flow on M_q:
dμ/dt = -∇_μ F[q_μ]

Perception is PROJECTION onto a submanifold — the same e-projection /
m-projection geometry. Predictive coding implements this gradient flow
through hierarchical message passing.
