# Process Philosophy and Relational Physics: Formal Reference

Date: 2026-04-05
Status: Reference doc — the actual philosophy and physics, not the system's application

## Whitehead's Process Philosophy

Alfred North Whitehead's Process and Reality (1929) replaces substance ontology
with an ontology of actual occasions — momentary, self-determining "drops of
experience." The key inversion: "Its 'being' is constituted by its 'becoming.'"
Becoming is metaphysically prior to being.

Core vocabulary:
- Concrescence: process by which an actual occasion integrates data from its
  past, filtering and combining until it reaches satisfaction (full determinacy).
  At satisfaction, the occasion perishes and passes to objective immortality —
  becoming available datum for future occasions.
- Prehension: the act by which one occasion grasps/feels another. Physical
  prehensions absorb data from past actualities; conceptual prehensions grasp
  eternal objects (pure potentials).
- Ingression: how an eternal object's potentiality is realized in an actual entity.
- Internal relations: occasions are constitutively shaped by their relations.
  An occasion IS its prehensions.

## Relation to Nominalism

Common ground: both deny fixed enduring substances as fundamental. Both are
anti-essentialist in the classical sense.

Key differences:
- Nominalism denies reality of universals. Only particulars exist.
- Process philosophy retains a role for universals via eternal objects —
  pure potentials that can ingress into actual occasions. Whitehead is a
  realist about potentials in a way strict nominalism forbids.
- Nominalism tends to be deflationary about structure; process philosophy is
  maximally structurally rich (internal relations, hierarchies, complex
  topologies of becoming).

Sellars developed arguably the first consistently nominalist AND naturalist
system using a process ontology — treating qualia as aspects of processes.

## Relational Quantum Mechanics (Rovelli, 1996)

Central claim: there is no observer-independent state of a physical system.
The quantum state is a relation between observed system and observing system.

Core commitments:
- All systems are equivalent — no fundamental observer/observed distinction
- There is no privileged "real" account of the state
- Different observers can have different, equally valid accounts
- Inspired by special relativity's lesson: physical descriptions are
  frame-dependent. RQM extends this to quantum states.
- Information (relative information between systems) is the bedrock concept

## Information-Theoretic Physics

### Wheeler's "It from Bit" (1989)

Every item of the physical world has "at bottom an immaterial source and
explanation." Reality arises from yes-no questions and equipment-evoked
responses. Existence is participatory.

### Zurek's Quantum Darwinism (2003)

Explains emergence of classicality via Darwinian selection. The environment
acts as witness, proliferating redundant copies of information about selected
pointer states. Classical robustness arises because only states surviving
environmental monitoring become accessible to observers.

### Deutsch and Marletto's Constructor Theory (2013-2015)

Reformulates physics in terms of which transformations (tasks) are possible
and which are impossible, and why. A constructor can cause a task while
retaining ability to cause it again. Laws become statements about
possible/impossible tasks rather than equations of motion.

Applied to information theory: derives all known qualitative differences
between classical and quantum information (no-cloning, complementarity,
objective unpredictability) from constructor-theoretic principles.

## Ontic Structural Realism (Ladyman, French)

Structure is ontologically fundamental. No underlying "stuff" bearing
properties — only structures. In the most radical form: relations without
relata, "relations all the way down."

Motivation: structure is preserved across theory change even when ontology
is not. Epistemic structural realism (Worrall) says we can only KNOW
structure; OSR says structure is all there IS.

Critique (Cao, Psillos, Chakravartty): "one cannot intelligibly subscribe
to the reality of relations unless one is committed to the fact that some
things are related." Ladyman and Ross respond by reconceptualizing objects
as nodes with no intrinsic identity independent of the structure.

## Causal Set Theory (Sorkin, Dowker)

Founding slogan: "Order + Number = Geometry."

Spacetime is fundamentally discrete — a collection of elementary events.
The only structure is a partial order: for some pairs x, y, we know
x precedes y. This partial order is the microscopic analog of macroscopic
causal structure.

Why it works: Hawking, King, and McCarthy showed that causal structure plus
volume information determines Lorentzian geometry up to conformal factor.
A causal set provides both: partial order encodes causal structure, number
of elements encodes volume.

Dynamics: Classical Sequential Growth models describe stochastic processes
where the causal set grows by continual birth of new elements, subject to
discrete general covariance and a Bell-type causality condition.

## Carlo Rovelli: Unified Picture

Sits at the intersection of several threads:

- Loop Quantum Gravity: background-independent quantization of GR. Space
  composed of discrete atoms — spin networks with quanta of area and volume.
  Dynamics via spin foams. No fixed background spacetime.
- Relational QM: quantum states are relational, not absolute.
- Thermal Time Hypothesis (with Connes): in a generally covariant theory
  with no preferred time, time EMERGES statistically. The thermal state
  picks out a flow on the algebra of observables. "When we say a variable
  is 'the time,' we are not making a statement about fundamental mechanics
  but about the statistical distribution."
- Information foundations: Shannon's relative information between physical
  systems can ground statistical mechanics and QM without subjectivism.

Deeply consonant with process philosophy: no fixed background, no absolute
states, relations and information as primary, time as emergent from process.

## Category Theory in Physics

### Categorical Quantum Mechanics (Abramsky, Coecke, 2004)

The primitive objects are physical PROCESSES and how they compose. Not
states of systems (substance-like) but processes and compositional structure.

Mathematical framework: dagger symmetric monoidal category. Sequential
composition = morphism composition. Parallel composition = tensor product.

A physical theory IS a process theory — a symmetric monoidal category
with additional structure. Explicit formalization of "composition replaces
substance."

### Baez's contributions

Higher category theory, n-categories, cobordism hypotheses, TQFTs.
Baez's "Rosetta Stone" paper connects category theory, physics, topology,
logic, and computation as different presentations of the same compositional
structure.

### Diagrammatic calculus

String diagrams / ZX-calculus derived from monoidal categories provides
complete graphical language for quantum computation. Proofs become
topological manipulations, not algebraic manipulations.

## Integrated Information Theory (Tononi, 2004)

Consciousness is identical to a system's integrated information (Phi).
A system is conscious to the degree it has intrinsic causal power — capacity
to causally influence itself in a way that is integrated (not decomposable)
and differentiated (many distinct states possible).

Two phenomenological axioms:
- Integration: every conscious experience is unified
- Differentiation: the space of possible experiences is vast

Phi measures minimum information generated by a system above and beyond
its parts. System with Phi > 0 has some consciousness.

Controversy: 2023 letter by numerous scholars characterized IIT as
potentially unfalsifiable. Predictions remain difficult to test empirically.

## Panpsychism and Panprotopsychism

Panpsychism: mentality is fundamental and ubiquitous. Fundamental entities
have some form of experience.

Panprotopsychism: fundamental entities have proto-conscious properties —
precursors to consciousness that can constitute consciousness in larger
systems without themselves being conscious.

Key arguments:
- Nagel (1979): if consciousness exists and is irreducible, and higher
  properties reduce to lower, then consciousness must have roots all the
  way down.
- Strawson (2006): radical emergence (consciousness from wholly
  non-conscious ingredients) is unintelligible. "Emergence can't be brute."
- Chalmers (1996): consciousness is irreducible, should be taken as
  fundamental. Both panpsychism and panprotopsychism are live options.

Connection to process philosophy: Whitehead's actual occasions have
"experience" at all levels. But Whitehead's experience is prehension —
proto-feeling, not human consciousness. Arguably closer to panprotopsychism.
