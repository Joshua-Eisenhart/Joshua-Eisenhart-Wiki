# Autopoiesis and Enactivism: Formal Reference

Date: 2026-04-05
Status: Reference doc — the actual frameworks, not the system's application

---

## Autopoiesis (Maturana & Varela, 1972/1980)

An autopoietic system is a network of processes of production of components
that: (a) continuously regenerate the network that produced them, and
(b) constitute the system as a distinguishable unity. The sole "product"
is the continuation of its own relational unity.

Contrast with allopoietic systems (factories) that produce something
other than themselves.

### Organization vs Structure

Critical distinction:
- Organization: relations between components that define the system as
  a system of a particular kind. INVARIANT — if it changes, the system
  ceases to be that kind of system.
- Structure: actual components and concrete relations realizing the
  organization at any moment. VARIABLE.

A cell's organization (self-producing network) stays the same.
Its structure (specific molecules) changes constantly.

### Organizational Closure

Processes of production form a closed network — each component produced
by others within the network. The network produces the boundary (membrane)
that makes it a discrete unity.

This closure is ORGANIZATIONAL, not thermodynamic — open to flows of
matter and energy, closed in organization.

## Structural Coupling

When an autopoietic system and its environment have a history of recurrent
interactions leading to structural congruence, they are structurally coupled.

### Perturbation vs Instruction

The environment PERTURBS the system — does not INSTRUCT it. The system is
structurally determined: its own current structure determines what changes
a perturbation triggers. Same perturbation, different systems → different
responses. No "instructive interactions" — nothing from outside specifies
the internal state change.

The environment selects which organisms persist but does not specify
their responses.

## Enactivism (Varela, Thompson & Rosch, 1991)

From The Embodied Mind. Cognition is not internal computation over
representations. It is embodied action.

Core claims:
- Cognition depends on experience from having a body with particular
  sensorimotor capacities
- The organism brings forth (enacts) a world of significance through
  its activity
- Organism and environment are co-specified
- No pre-given world "out there" that the mind mirrors
- The world of an organism is enacted through coupling with environment

Draws on Merleau-Ponty, Husserl, Buddhist dependent co-arising,
and Varela's autopoiesis.

## Autonomy as Organizational Closure

Autonomy = self-determination of identity through self-production.
Not independence from environment.

An autopoietic system is autonomous because its organization specifies
what counts as perturbation, where its boundaries are, what interactions
are possible. Identity emerges from operational closure, not external
imposition.

The system defines its own norms — what counts as maintaining or
threatening its existence is determined by its own organization.

## Relation to FEP

Three positions in active debate:

### Compatibility (Ramstead, Kirchhoff & Friston, 2020)

"Active inference is enactive inference." Generative models should not
be read as structural representations but as realizing inference-and-
control. The "pragmatic turn."

### Bridging (Kirchhoff, 2018)

FEP and autopoietic enactivism are "genuinely continuous on central
issues." FEP provides formal tools for modeling sensorimotor autonomy.

### Tension (Froese & Di Paolo)

Biological systems are NOT well described as state-determined systems
attracted toward NESS (the FEP picture). Enactive life-mind continuity
requires autonomy, adaptivity, sense-making — FEP alone doesn't meet
these demands. FEP may be necessary but not sufficient.

Key tension: FEP applies to ANY system that persists. Enactivism insists
cognition requires specific organizational features beyond mere persistence.

## Viability Connection

An autopoietic system must maintain its organization or disintegrate (die).
The set of structural states compatible with maintaining organization =
the domain of viability.

Connection to viability theory (Aubin 1991): the viability kernel maps
to the set of structural configurations from which organization can be
maintained. Di Paolo explicitly connects: adaptivity is regulating oneself
with respect to conditions of viability.

## Biological Autonomy (Moreno & Mossio, 2015)

Most rigorous formalization of organizational closure.

### Closure of Constraints

Distinguishes processes (thermodynamic flows) from constraints (entities
acting on processes while being conserved at relevant timescale).

Biological organization = closure of constraints: constraints are mutually
dependent, each depending on and contributing to maintaining others in
the network.

### Function

A trait is functional when it is part of the closed, mutually dependent
constraint network that results in self-production and self-maintenance.
Naturalized, non-teleological account of biological function.

More precise than Maturana/Varela: works at constraint level, addresses
multi-scale organization, regulatory mechanisms, emergence of agency.

---

## Sense-Making (Di Paolo, 2005)

### The Gap in Bare Autopoiesis

Autopoiesis is binary — system maintains organization or doesn't. No
degrees. Accounts for conservation (staying alive) but not evaluative
interaction.

### Adaptivity

An adaptive system can: (a) monitor its states relative to the boundary
of its viability domain, and (b) actively regulate to remain viable or
improve conditions. Allows DEGREES — things can be better or worse.

### Sense-Making

The most basic cognition: an organism evaluates interactions relative
to maintenance of its own viability. The world becomes significant
(has valence, matters) because the organism's continued existence is
at stake and it can differentially respond.

Not representation — the organism's activity of constituting a meaningful
world through adaptive engagement.

## Critiques

**Too restrictive?** Original definition tied to molecular production with
physical boundary. Can software self-replicate? Can social systems be
autopoietic? Maturana was skeptical of extensions beyond biology.

**Luhmann's social extension:** Applied autopoiesis to social systems
(components = communications, not molecules). Controversial — argued
to dilute the concept. Boundary problem acute: social systems lack
clear physical boundary.

**Boundary problem:** Even in biology, defining where an autopoietic
system begins and ends is non-trivial beyond single cells.

**Cognitivist critique:** Cannot explain abstract thought, language,
mathematics — phenomena seemingly requiring representations and
computation beyond sensorimotor coupling.

**Closure vs openness tension:** Simultaneous organizational closure
and causal interaction (structural coupling) seen as underspecified.
